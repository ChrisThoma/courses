# Projects (MUS-40H)

Three graded projects plus the final Operator's Demo. Every one is a proof of **operation**, not artistry. Nobody is grading whether your beat is good; they are grading whether you can *drive the machine* cleanly and without getting lost. Record your screen or your hands for each so you can grade yourself cold, and judge honestly: a fumbled, lucky pass is not a pass.

Each project has a **deliverable**, a **points rubric**, and a plain-language **pass bar**.

---

## Project 1: Configured & Mapped (Week 1, 10%)

**The goal:** prove the machine is no longer opaque, that you can configure it, navigate it, get clean sound in and out, edit, and never lose work.

**Deliverables:**
1. A correctly **configured machine**: firmware current (5.52 or later), brightness, auto-off, and pad sensitivity set, and a **backup that exists** (show the backup on the SD card, or the exported copy on a computer).
2. A **one-page "machine map"** you author: a labeled sketch or list of the controls and what each does, the four-bus signal flow in your own words, and the five shortcuts you use most. This is your cheat sheet; making it is the learning.
3. A **2–3 minute recording** (screen or hands) in which you: open the system menu and change a setting, play across three banks, connect a source and set a healthy level, record a dry sample with auto-trigger, trim it to a clean loop, copy it to another bank, and run a backup.

**Rubric (20 pts):**

| Criterion | Pts |
|---|---|
| Firmware current and backup shown | 3 |
| Settings configured sensibly | 2 |
| Machine map is accurate and genuinely yours | 4 |
| Navigation in the recording is confident, not hunting | 4 |
| Healthy recording level (meter strong, no clip) | 3 |
| Clean trim and seamless loop | 2 |
| Backup current at the end, no lost work | 2 |

**Pass bar:** you set the machine up, found everything without long hunting, recorded a clean sample at a good level, looped it cleanly, and backed up. The map reflects real understanding, not copied text.

## Project 2: The Three Engines (Week 2, 15%)

**The goal:** prove you can operate the effects engine, the sequencer, and resampling, the core of the machine, in one short chain.

**Deliverable:** a **2–4 minute recording** in which you, in one flow:
1. Route a pad to BUS 1 or BUS 2 and apply an insert effect that audibly hits only that sound.
2. Apply a master effect on BUS 3 or BUS 4 that colors the whole mix.
3. Program a one-bar (or longer) pattern, using **both** TR-REC (step) and a live overdub, and apply quantize and a little swing.
4. **Resample** the playing result down to a single pad and play that one pad.
5. Narrate (or caption) what each step is doing, especially which bus affects what.

**Rubric (25 pts):**

| Criterion | Pts |
|---|---|
| Insert effect correctly routed to one sound only | 5 |
| Master effect correctly affecting the whole mix | 4 |
| Pattern programmed in TR-REC | 4 |
| Live overdub layered on, then quantized | 4 |
| Swing/velocity used for feel | 2 |
| Resample to one pad, played back correctly | 4 |
| Narration shows you know what each bus does | 2 |

**Pass bar:** the insert effect demonstrably hits only its pad, the master effect hits everything, you used both sequencer methods, and you resampled the result to one pad on purpose. You can say what each bus is doing and why.

## Project 3: Connected (Week 3, 15%)

**The goal:** prove you can put the SP into a real setup, connected to a computer, audio and MIDI.

**Deliverable:** a **recording or short write-up with screenshots** showing all three:
1. **USB audio:** record the SP's output into a DAW track over USB (set USB to MIX OUT, SP as the DAW input). Show the recorded audio track.
2. **MIDI clock sync:** sync the SP's sequencer to the DAW's clock (or the DAW to the SP), and show them locked at the same tempo. State which device is the leader.
3. **File export:** export one sample (or a resampled beat) off the SP to the computer with the app or card, and show it opening on the computer.

**Rubric (25 pts):**

| Criterion | Pts |
|---|---|
| SP recognized as a USB audio device; routing set correctly | 4 |
| SP output recorded into a DAW track (shown) | 6 |
| Clock sync working, leader/follower stated correctly | 6 |
| A sample/beat exported to the computer and opened | 5 |
| You can explain the one-leader clock rule and the USB routing setting | 4 |

**Pass bar:** you recorded the SP into a DAW over USB, synced the clock with a clearly chosen leader, and moved a file off the machine, each demonstrably working, not just described.

## Final Capstone: The Operator's Demo (Week 3, 25%)

**The goal:** demonstrate, in one continuous take, that you can operate the whole machine, cold, without the manual.

**Deliverable:** a **single continuous recorded take, 3–5 minutes** (screen and/or hands), with no edits and no manual open, in which you drive the SP through a real workflow that touches every major subsystem:

1. **Get sound in** at a healthy level (sample a source or use skip-back).
2. **Edit** it (trim, loop, or pitch) and place it on a pad.
3. **Route it through the bus engine** (an insert effect and a master effect).
4. **Sequence a pattern** over it (any method) with quantize/swing.
5. **Resample** to commit and glue.
6. **Perform a moment**: a DJFX looper sweep, a hold-and-drum, or a DJ-mode blend.
7. **Export or record the result** off the machine (USB into a DAW, or resample-and-export), and confirm your **backup** is current.

**Rubric (40 pts):**

| Criterion | Pts |
|---|---|
| Navigation throughout: confident, reads the screen, no long hunting | 8 |
| Clean sound in at a healthy level | 5 |
| Editing done cleanly | 4 |
| Bus routing correct and explained by what you hear | 6 |
| A pattern sequenced and tightened | 5 |
| Resampling used on purpose | 4 |
| A live performance moment executed | 3 |
| Result exported/recorded off the machine | 3 |
| Backed up; no lost work; one continuous take | 2 |

**Pass bar (this is the whole course):** in one take, you operated every major subsystem of the SP-404 MK2, navigation, I/O, editing, the four-bus engine, the sequencer, resampling, a performance gesture, and getting the result out, **without getting lost and without the manual.** The music does not have to be good. The *operation* has to be clean. If you can do this, the machine is no longer in your way, and you are ready for the craft (the sister course, [MUS-45H](#/sp404-mk2-sampling)).

---

### A note on grading yourself

For every project, record it and watch it back **the next day, cold**. Ask: did I hesitate? Did I have to undo? Did I get lucky, or did I know what I was doing? Operation you cannot repeat on demand has not set in yet. If a project felt shaky, do the matching drills (see [DRILLS.md](DRILLS.md)) and run it again. The standard is 90% (see [RUBRIC.md](RUBRIC.md)), and the honest path there is reps, not leniency.
