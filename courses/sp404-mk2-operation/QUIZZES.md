# Quizzes & Final Exam (MUS-40H)

Closed-book. Answer from memory, then grade against [ANSWER-KEYS.md](ANSWER-KEYS.md). Each quiz has a written section (recall and understanding) and an **applied section** you perform at the machine, because operation is the point. Mastery is **90%**. Below that, re-study the weak area and the matching drills before moving on.

Take Quiz 1 on Day 6, Quiz 2 on Day 13, and the Final Exam on Day 21.

---

## Quiz 1, Week 1: The Machine, Navigation & Signal (100 pts)

### Part A: Written (60 pts)

1. **(6)** Name the five "machines" the SP-404 MK2 is in one box.
2. **(6)** What holds projects, what does a project hold, and how many pad slots is that in total?
3. **(6)** Write out the three-step gesture that runs almost every operation on the machine.
4. **(6)** Why is it a mistake to memorize "[CTRL] knob 2 always does X"? What should you do instead?
5. **(6)** Explain line level vs mic level, and what goes wrong if you mismatch a source to the wrong input.
6. **(6)** What is the target for a recording level, and what are the two ways to get it wrong?
7. **(6)** Name the three play modes and the best use of each.
8. **(6)** What does [REMAIN]/hold let you do, and when do you want a pad in mono (cut its own sound) vs poly?
9. **(6)** List the six steps of the record flow in order.
10. **(6)** What is skip-back sampling, and what does auto-trigger do for you?

### Part B: Applied (40 pts), do these at the machine

11. **(10)** From a cold start, open the system menu and change one setting, then return to sample mode. Smoothly, no long hunting.
12. **(10)** Connect a source and set a healthy recording level on its loudest part (meter strong, no clip).
13. **(10)** Record a dry sample with auto-trigger and trim it to a seamless loop.
14. **(10)** Copy that pad to another bank, then run a backup to an SD card or the app.

## Quiz 2, Week 2: The Engines (100 pts)

### Part A: Written (60 pts)

1. **(6)** What is the difference between BUS 1/2 and BUS 3/4?
2. **(6)** A pad routed to DRY: does a BUS 3 effect touch it? Does a BUS 1 effect? Explain.
3. **(6)** Name the SP's "character" effects and what they do to a sound.
4. **(6)** Why can you not run unlimited effects at once, and what is the standard move when you hit the limit?
5. **(6)** What does INPUT FX do, and when would you use it instead of adding an effect after?
6. **(6)** Describe real-time (overdub) recording in steps, and say why tempo must be set first.
7. **(6)** How does TR-REC differ from real-time recording, and how do you fix a single wrong hit in TR-REC without re-recording?
8. **(6)** What does quantize do, and what is the trade-off between light and full?
9. **(6)** In one sentence, what is resampling, and name the three things it lets you do.
10. **(6)** What does the DJFX looper do, and how do you keep a looper performance instead of losing it?

### Part B: Applied (40 pts), do these at the machine

11. **(10)** Put an insert effect on BUS 1 and route one pad to it so only that pad is affected; prove it by ear.
12. **(10)** Put a master effect on BUS 3 that colors the whole mix.
13. **(10)** Program a one-bar pattern in TR-REC, then overdub a part live and quantize the take.
14. **(10)** Resample the playing result to a single pad and play it back.

## Final Exam, Cumulative (100 pts)

### Part A: Written (55 pts)

1. **(5)** Trace the playback signal path from a pad to the output, naming the four buses and what each pair does.
2. **(5)** Give the one habit that keeps you from losing work, and the one action that can corrupt a project.
3. **(5)** "My effect is on everything" and "my effect does nothing": give the cause of each.
4. **(5)** Explain the difference between USB audio and USB MIDI over the one cable, and name the third thing the cable carries.
5. **(5)** What does class-compliant mean for connecting the SP to a computer?
6. **(5)** What does MIDI clock sync synchronize, and what is the rule about how many clock leaders a setup has?
7. **(5)** Which kind of Utility setting decides what the SP sends over USB, and what do you set it to for recording the full mix into a DAW?
8. **(5)** How do you enter DJ mode, and what do [CTRL 1], [CTRL 2], and [CTRL 3] do there?
9. **(5)** Name the cleanest way to get audio of a finished beat off the SP, two routes.
10. **(5)** List the seven steps of the complete operational workflow in order.
11. **(5)** Give three entries from the troubleshooting table: a symptom, its cause, and its fix.

### Part B: Applied (45 pts), do these at the machine, cold

12. **(10)** From a cold start, record a sound in at a healthy level and edit it onto a pad.
13. **(10)** Route it through the bus engine (one insert, one master effect) and sequence a short pattern over it.
14. **(10)** Resample the result to one pad.
15. **(10)** Record the SP into a DAW over USB **or** export a sample to the computer, and confirm your backup is current.
16. **(5)** Diagnose one fault aloud: name a symptom, its cause, and fix it on the machine.

> The applied parts are the real exam. Anyone can memorize the bus diagram; the course is passed by *doing* the operations cleanly, cold, without the manual. Grade Part B strictly: hesitation, undo, and guessing all cost points.
