# Mastery Rubric: Operating the SP-404 MK2 (MUS-40H)

This rubric measures one thing: **can you drive the machine?** Ten operation domains, each on a four-level ladder from Novice to Solid. "Solid" is the bar for someone who can pick up the SP cold and use every major subsystem without the manual. Each domain has a concrete **Test** you can run on yourself.

Score each domain 1–4 against the Test, performed cold (no notes, no peeking). Mastery for the course is **90%**: at least **36 of 40 points**, with no domain below 3.

How to grade honestly: do the Test with your screen or hands on camera, then watch it back. If you hesitated, guessed, or had to undo, that is not yet "Solid." Operation you cannot repeat on demand is not operation you have.

---

## 1. Navigation & the menu system

| Level | What it looks like | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Novice | Lost without the manual; hunts for everything. | (none) |
| 2 | Finds common functions slowly, by trial and error. | Open the system/utility menu within 15 seconds. |
| 3 | Moves confidently through menus; reads the screen to know what knobs do. | Change three different settings in the system menu, then return to sample mode, no fumbling. |
| 4 Solid | The "function then knob then pad" gesture is automatic; rarely looks. | Asked for any setting, reach it and change it within 10 seconds while narrating what each control does. |

## 2. Signal flow understanding

| Level | What it looks like | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | No mental model of where sound is. | (none) |
| 2 | Knows inputs go to pads and pads go to output. | Name the inputs and outputs and what each is for. |
| 3 | Can trace a sound from input through pad settings to output. | Draw the record and playback paths from memory. |
| 4 Solid | Can place any sound on the four-bus path and predict what each stage does to it. | Given a pad routed to BUS 1 with a master effect on BUS 3, explain exactly what the listener hears and why. |

## 3. Projects, storage & backup

| Level | What it looks like | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Has lost work and does not know why. | (none) |
| 2 | Backs up when reminded. | Run a backup to an SD card (or export with the app) on command. |
| 3 | Backs up on schedule; switches and creates projects. | Create a new project, then switch back to the old one, nothing lost. |
| 4 Solid | Backs up and restores without thinking; knows where work lives. | Back up the machine, show where the restore lives, and explain what auto-save does and does not protect. |

## 4. Inputs, outputs & levels

| Level | What it looks like | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Records too quiet or clipping. | (none) |
| 2 | Gets sound in, level by luck. | Connect a source and get audible sound onto a pad. |
| 3 | Sets a healthy level deliberately, every time. | Set the input level so the meter is strong but never clips, on the loudest part. |
| 4 Solid | Chooses line vs mic, mono vs stereo, and monitoring correctly for the source. | Set up a mic and a line source correctly in turn, explaining each choice. |

## 5. Pads & performance

| Level | What it looks like | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Presses pads, no control of mode or dynamics. | (none) |
| 2 | Plays pads; one play mode only. | Play across three banks from memory. |
| 3 | Sets gate/one-shot/loop per pad; uses velocity and hold. | Set one pad to each mode and demonstrate the difference; hold a loop hands-free. |
| 4 Solid | Performs fluently: banks, velocity, hold, sub pad, mono retrigger. | Keep a loop held while finger-drumming a one-shot pad in time, eyes off the pads. |

## 6. Recording & editing operations

| Level | What it looks like | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot reliably get a clean sample onto a pad. | (none) |
| 2 | Records a sample with help. | Record a sample using auto-trigger. |
| 3 | Records clean, trims tight, loops cleanly, manages pads. | Record, trim to a seamless loop, then copy that pad to another bank. |
| 4 Solid | The whole capture-and-edit pipeline is reflex, including skip-back and INPUT FX. | Use skip-back to grab a sound you "missed," trim it, and place it, smoothly. |

## 7. The four-bus effects engine

| Level | What it looks like | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Turns effects on and off randomly. | (none) |
| 2 | Toggles an effect, unsure what it touches. | Turn on one effect and hear it. |
| 3 | Routes pads to BUS 1/2 and applies master FX on BUS 3/4 deliberately. | Put a filter on one bus affecting only drums, and a master effect on everything. |
| 4 Solid | Reasons about the whole bus layout and the FX limit; resamples when needed. | Build a two-bus routing, hit the processing limit on purpose, and resample to free the engine. |

## 8. The pattern sequencer

| Level | What it looks like | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot record a pattern. | (none) |
| 2 | Records a real-time pattern that mostly works. | Record a one-bar pattern live and play it back. |
| 3 | Uses both real-time and TR-REC; applies quantize and swing; edits. | Step-program a beat in TR-REC, quantize a live take, add swing. |
| 4 Solid | Edits length, copies, chains, and uses the sub-pad roll fluently. | Build two patterns, chain them, and edit a wrong note without re-recording. |

## 9. Resampling

| Level | What it looks like | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Does not know what resampling is. | (none) |
| 2 | Can describe it. | Explain in one sentence what resampling does. |
| 3 | Resamples the machine's output to a pad on purpose. | Resample an effected loop to a new pad and play the result. |
| 4 Solid | Uses resampling as a routine move to commit FX, glue, and free voices. | Glue a chopped sequence into one pad and explain why you would. |

## 10. The machine connected (DJ, USB, MIDI, files)

| Level | What it looks like | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Has never connected the SP to anything. | (none) |
| 2 | Knows the USB cable carries audio and MIDI. | Enter DJ mode and assign a sample to a channel. |
| 3 | Records the SP into a DAW or syncs over MIDI clock; moves a file. | Record the SP's output into a DAW track over USB. |
| 4 Solid | Operates DJ mode, USB audio, MIDI sync, and file import/export confidently. | Sync the SP to a DAW's clock, then export a sample to the computer with the app. |

## Expected trajectory

- **End of Week 1:** Domains 1–6 around 3; the connected and engine domains still 1–2. You can navigate, manage projects, get clean sound in and out, and play and edit. That is the foundation.
- **End of Week 2:** Domains 7–9 reach 3; the Week 1 domains firm up toward 4. You command the effects engine, the sequencer, and resampling.
- **End of Week 3:** Domain 10 reaches 3, most others 4. You can operate the whole machine, including connecting it to the outside world, cold.

Do not expect 4s everywhere at the finish. A few 3s that you keep using will quietly become 4s over the following month, because operation is the kind of skill that only sets in with repetition. The course's job is to get every domain to at least "Solid enough to use." Speed and polish are the gift of the hours you put in next.
