# Practice Menu (Drills): REC-24

The lessons prescribe a default daily drill, but no single drill fits every day. This is the full menu: **pick one drill per active skill and rotate every few days** so nothing goes stale. Two things need *daily* reps from Day 1 to the end: **gain staging by the meter** (A) and **critical listening** (H). Keep those going forever.

**Building your daily ~20 minutes:** 3 min the day's setup/level drill (A) · 5 min the week's focus skill (B–G) · 5 min a recording task · 5–7 min listen back and judge (H). Always set levels by the meter; always judge by listening back.

> **The one rule above all drills:** *get a clean, strong signal at every stage, then listen back and tell the truth.* Most recording problems are a level set wrong or a problem you did not listen for. Fix the input, then fix your ears.

---

## A. Gain staging & levels (start Day 2, never stop)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **A1: Ten-second level set** | Start a source, set a healthy trim by the meter, stop, repeat | the core reflex of recording |
| **A2: Level ladder** | Record a part too quiet, too hot, and just right; play back and hear hiss vs distortion vs clean | training the ear to the failure modes |
| **A3: Best-sound nudge** | Set by the meter, then push hotter until it sounds best, back off the instant it is worse | finding tape saturation without clipping |
| **A4: Blind meter read** | Glance at the meter for one second and call "low / good / clipping" before adjusting | reading the meter instantly |

## B. Signal flow & routing (start Day 1)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **B1: Draw-the-flow** | Redraw the input-to-output signal path from memory; check against Reference §2 | the mental model behind every problem |
| **B2: Arm-route-roll** | Pick a track, set DIRECT, confirm its meter, record 5 s, stop, set SAFE | recording routing as reflex |
| **B3: Where-is-it** | Have someone (or yourself) create a "no sound" situation; find where on the path it broke | diagnosing fast |
| **B4: DIRECT vs BUSS** | Record one part DIRECT, then a two-channel blend via BUSS to a track | knowing both routing paths |

## C. Instruments & pedals in (start Day 3)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **C1: Front-end check** | Plug instrument-through-pedal in and set a clean trim from scratch | always going through a buffer |
| **C2: Straight vs buffered** | Record passive guitar straight in, then through a pedal; A/B the tone and level | hearing the impedance fix |
| **C3: Source roulette** | Set a clean level for guitar, then bass, then a line source, switching fast | adapting gain to any source |

## D. Click & timing (start Day 8)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **D1: Click-in-a-minute** | From nothing, get a click in, leveled, and recorded to a track | fast timing setup |
| **D2: Lock-in pass** | Play a riff to the click, record 20 s, play back and find every drift | objective timing |
| **D3: Slow-to-lock** | When you cannot lock, drop the tempo until you can, record there | tempo honesty |

## E. Overdubbing & cue mix (start Day 10)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **E1: Cue-mix set** | With parts on tape, balance click + existing + input fast | the half of overdubbing nobody mentions |
| **E2: Lock-to-the-part** | Overdub a part and judge whether it agrees with the existing parts, not just the click | parts that sit together |
| **E3: Track-budget plan** | Sketch which parts go on T1–T4 and what bounces, before recording | not running out of tracks |

## F. Punch-in (start Day 11)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **F1: Rehearse-then-punch** | Record a small flub, set in/out points, rehearse, then punch it clean | repair without re-recording |
| **F2: Seam check** | Play back across both punch points; if you can hear the punch, redo with better points | invisible repairs |
| **F3: Gap-finding** | On a take, mark the rests/sustains where a punch would hide | choosing punch points |

## G. Bouncing & mixing on the 424 (start Day 12)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **G1: Submix-and-commit** | Balance two tracks and bounce them to a third; trust the balance | committing fast on a four-track |
| **G2: Balance-first mix** | Mix a multitrack using only the faders, no EQ/effects | the truth that level is most of mixing |
| **G3: Sweep-and-cut** | Boost the swept mid, find the ugly frequency, cut it | musical EQ |
| **G4: Fader-ride pass** | During a mixdown, ride one part up/down for its moment | hands-on mixing |

## H. Critical listening (start Day 1; every day, no exceptions)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **H1: Two-system check** | Play a take on headphones and on another speaker; note what each reveals | honest evaluation |
| **H2: Five-point judge** | Score a take on level, noise, timing, tone, performance | the keep-or-redo decision |
| **H3: Reference listen** | Play a record you love, then your mix; name three differences | aiming your ear |
| **H4: Cold playback** | Record something, leave it ten minutes, judge it cold | hearing what is really there |

## Rules that make any drill work

1. **Set the level by the meter, first, every time.** A part recorded at a bad level cannot be fixed later. Ten seconds, no exceptions.
2. **Listen back to everything.** The fastest feedback in recording is hearing your own take cold and asking what is wrong with it.
3. **Always go through a buffer.** A passive instrument straight into the line input is a problem; a pedal or DI is the fix.
4. **Protect good takes (SAFE).** There is no undo on tape; the instant a take is good, set its track to SAFE.
5. **Commit.** Four tracks reward decisions and punish dithering. Pick a tone, a take, a balance, and move on; you can always re-record.
6. **Keep the heads clean.** A dull machine is almost always dirty heads, not a fault.
7. **Keep one number.** Log how fast you can set a clean level, how few passes a clean take takes, how tight your timing is. The line going up is the motivation.
8. **Short and daily beats long and rare.** Twenty focused minutes at the controls, most days, wires this in faster than a weekend marathon.
