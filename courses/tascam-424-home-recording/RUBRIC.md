# Mastery Rubric: REC-24

Grade yourself in each domain. **The course bar: Level 3 (Proficient) in every technical domain by the end, with the core ones (signal flow, gain staging) reaching Level 4.** Level 4 across the board is the longer arc, earned by recording for months after the course (see the trajectory note at the bottom). The honest test for most domains is the same: *record something, play it back cold, and judge the sound.* Levels:

- **1 (Novice):** Aware of the concept; can only do it with the manual open, slowly, and with luck.
- **2 (Developing):** Can do it with effort and frequent mistakes; needs to check each step.
- **3 (Proficient):** Can do it reliably and correctly without referring to notes; rare errors.
- **4 (Solid):** Automatic and fast; you do it as a reflex while thinking about the music, and you catch problems before they happen.

---

## Domain 1: Signal flow & machine fluency

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Gets lost; cannot say where a signal is or why something is silent |
| 2 | Can trace the path with the diagram in front of you |
| 3 | Draws the input-to-output path from memory; diagnoses most "where did it go?" problems |
| 4 | Navigates the whole machine by reflex; instantly locates any signal problem on the path |

**Test:** From memory, draw the signal flow from a guitar to the LINE OUT, naming every stage, and explain where you would look if a recorded track plays back silent.

## Domain 2: Gain staging & levels

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Sets levels by guessing the knob position; records too quiet or clipped |
| 2 | Uses the meter but slowly, and still misses peaks |
| 3 | Sets a strong, clean level by the meter in a few seconds, every time |
| 4 | Gain-stages the whole chain by reflex; uses tape saturation on purpose without clipping |

**Test:** With a part playing its loudest, set trim and level so the meter reads strong and never clips, in under ten seconds, and explain the sound of "too low" and "too high."

## Domain 3: Instruments & pedals in

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Plugs a passive guitar straight in and accepts the thin, quiet tone |
| 2 | Knows a pedal helps but cannot say why |
| 3 | Routes guitar/bass through a buffered pedal or DI and gets a full, strong signal; picks the right input |
| 4 | Diagnoses any impedance/level issue and fixes it; chooses the cleanest path for each source |

**Test:** Record the same passive guitar straight in and through a buffered pedal; hear the difference and explain the impedance cause and the fix.

## Domain 4: Recording to tape

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Unsure which switch arms a track; records to the wrong track or not at all |
| 2 | Can record one track DIRECT with the manual open |
| 3 | Arms and records any track, DIRECT or via the buss, using the transport and counter confidently |
| 4 | Routes and records fluently, protects finished takes on SAFE by reflex, navigates by the counter |

**Test:** Record a part to track 3 via DIRECT, zero and use the counter, set the other tracks to SAFE, and explain when you would use BUSS instead.

## Domain 5: The medium (dbx, tape & care)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Uses whatever tape; unsure what dbx does |
| 2 | Knows to use Type II and keep dbx on |
| 3 | Explains dbx, the double speed, and the track-4 sync exception; cleans the heads |
| 4 | Manages the medium without thinking: right tape, dbx correct, heads maintained, drift anticipated |

**Test:** Explain why you use Type II tape and keep dbx on, when you would defeat dbx on track 4, and demonstrate cleaning the heads.

## Domain 6: Click track & timing

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Records with no timing reference; tempo wanders |
| 2 | Can record a click to a track but struggles to play to it |
| 3 | Generates and records a click, monitors it, and plays a part in time to it |
| 4 | Locks every part to the click effortlessly; the whole song stays in time across overdubs |

**Test:** Record a click to track 4, then record a guitar or bass part to it that stays in time start to finish (check it back against the click).

## Domain 7: Multitrack & overdubbing

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Can record one part only; loses the others when adding a new one |
| 2 | Overdubs but the cue mix is unusable; parts drift apart |
| 3 | Sets a clear cue mix and overdubs parts that lock to what is already down |
| 4 | Builds a full arrangement fluently; balances the cue mix so every overdub sits right |

**Test:** With one part already on tape, overdub a second part that locks to it, using a cue/monitor mix you set deliberately.

## Domain 8: Punch-in & track management

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Re-records the whole take to fix one mistake |
| 2 | Can punch in roughly but often clips the good parts |
| 3 | Punches in and out cleanly at planned points using rehearsal mode |
| 4 | Punches by reflex (footswitch or hand), plans track use and headroom ahead of time |

**Test:** Repair a single bad bar in the middle of a take with a clean punch-in and punch-out, leaving the surrounding performance intact.

## Domain 9: Bouncing & mixing on the 424

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot combine tracks; runs out of tracks and stops |
| 2 | Can bounce but the submix balance is wrong and not fixable after |
| 3 | Bounces a balanced submix to free a track; builds a clear mix with level, pan, and EQ |
| 4 | Plans bounces to fit a big arrangement on four tracks; mixes with confident balance, pan, EQ, and an effect |

**Test:** Bounce three parts down to one track with a balance you commit to, then build a mix of the result with level, pan, and at least one EQ move and one effect.

## Domain 10: Transfer to Ableton & finishing

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot get audio off the tape into the computer |
| 2 | Captures a stereo bounce but at a bad level or out of time |
| 3 | Transfers a clean stereo bounce *and* four stems; warps past tape drift; exports a finished file |
| 4 | Chooses stereo vs stems deliberately, transfers at correct level, finishes the mix in the box, exports a polished track |

**Test:** Transfer a song both ways (a stereo bounce and four stems) at a correct level, warp it so it sits in time, and export a finished file.

Expected trajectory: end of Week 1 → Domains 1–5 at Level 3. End of Week 2 → Domains 6–8 at Level 3, Domains 1–2 climbing to 4. End of Week 3 → all technical domains at 3–4, Domain 10 (transfer) at 3+. The taste-driven side of Domains 9 and 10 (mixes that sound like records, arrangement choices that serve a song) reaches a stable Level 4 only with months of continued recording after the course; that is normal and expected. The fundamentals (clean signal flow, levels, routing, a working multitrack method) are what this course locks in.
