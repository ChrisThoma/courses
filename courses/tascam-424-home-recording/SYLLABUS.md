# REC-24 Syllabus: Home Recording on the Tascam 424 MKIII

## Course description

A three-week intensive in home multitrack recording for the guitarist or bassist who wants to *make recordings*, not just read about gear. Students learn the Tascam 424 MKIII from the ground up: how a four-track cassette Portastudio combines a mixer and a tape deck, how signal flows through it, and how to set clean levels at every stage. From there students get guitar, bass, and pedals into the machine cleanly, record real parts to tape with dbx, keep time to a click track, overdub and punch in to build a song, bounce tracks to fit a full arrangement onto four, mix the result on the built-in board, and transfer the finished work into Ableton Live as a stereo bounce or as four separate stems. By the end, students can track, overdub, mix, and export a finished song, and they understand signal flow well enough to record on any equipment.

This is an **applied / studio course**: the bulk of the work is at the controls, recording, every day.

## Learning objectives

By the end of this course you will be able to:

1. **Diagram the signal flow** of the 424 MKIII from input to output and locate where any signal is on that path.
2. **Gain-stage** a recording: set trim and levels by the meter so a part is strong and clean, and use tape saturation deliberately rather than by accident.
3. **Connect guitar, bass, and pedals** correctly, explaining the impedance problem and why a buffered pedal or DI solves it.
4. **Record to tape** using the RECORD FUNCTION switches: arm tracks, route DIRECT or via the L/R buss, run the transport, use the counter and RTZ, and protect finished takes with SAFE.
5. **Use dbx and the tape medium** correctly: choose Type II tape, run at high speed, keep dbx on, know when to defeat it on track 4, and maintain the heads.
6. **Keep time to a click track** that you generate and record yourself, monitoring it while you track.
7. **Overdub** a multitrack arrangement, setting a usable cue/monitor mix so each new part locks to the ones already down.
8. **Punch in and out** to repair a mistake without re-recording a whole take, using rehearsal mode and a footswitch.
9. **Bounce (ping-pong)** tracks to combine parts and free up space, planning track use and understanding the generation-loss tradeoff.
10. **Mix on the 424's board**: set balance, pan, and EQ, and add an outboard effect through the send-and-return path.
11. **Transfer to Ableton Live**: capture a stereo bounce and four individual stems, set correct levels, and handle tape drift by warping.
12. **Finish a song**: take a recording from first take to a mixed, exported file, and judge it honestly against a standard.

## Daily structure (~45–75 minutes)

| Block | Time | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| Setup | 5 min | Power up, load/label the tape, clean the heads if due, the day's quick drill from the Practice Menu |

Grade yourself against `RUBRIC.md`. **Mastery threshold: 90%.** The honest grading here is in the *listening*: play your recordings back cold, on headphones and on a different speaker, and judge the sound (clean levels, steady time, a balance you can hear into). A recording that is noisy, distorted, or rushed is not done, no matter how good the playing felt.

## Course policies (the real failure modes of home recording)

- **Set the level by the meter, every single time.** The two most common beginner ruins are recordings that are too quiet (buried in hiss) and too loud (clipped). Both are prevented by a ten-second meter check you never skip.
- **Protect finished takes.** Set completed tracks to **SAFE** and label your cassettes. There is no undo on tape; an erased take is gone.
- **Commit.** Four tracks means deciding: a tone, a take, an arrangement. Endless second-guessing is the enemy on a four-track. Print it and move on; you can always re-record.
- **Listen back to everything.** The fastest learning in recording is hearing your own take cold and asking "what is wrong with this?" Do it after every pass.
- **Keep the heads clean.** A dull, muddy machine is almost always dirty heads, not a broken unit. Clean and demagnetize on schedule.
- **Closed-book quizzes.** The point is to find gaps, not to feel good.
- **Falling behind:** use the studio day to catch up. This course is cumulative: shaky signal flow in Week 1 wrecks every recording in Weeks 2 and 3. If you must drop something, drop the optional ★ extensions, never the core tracking days.
