# Projects: REC-24

Projects are where the skills turn into *recordings you actually made.* Each has a deliverable (an audio file) and a grading rubric. **Listen back to everything** and grade yourself by listening, on headphones and on a second speaker, which is far more honest than judging while you record. "I basically got it" is a 70, not a 95. The standard everywhere is **clean, steady, and finished**, not "fast" or "it sounded fine in the room."

> **Why audio deliverables?** Because a meter and a microphone do not flatter you. Your sense of a take while you play it is always better than the recording. Listening back cold is the fastest way to improve, and the files become a record of how far you have come.

---

## Project 1: One Great Track *(Day 7 · 10% of grade)*

**Goal:** prove the Week-1 foundation is solid: a single part captured to tape that sounds genuinely good, clean level, no noise, a tone you like. This is the Domain 1–5 test.

### Deliverable
A short recording (30–90 seconds) of **one guitar or bass part**, recorded to tape through your pedal/DI front end, then played back and captured (a phone recording of the playback is fine for grading):
1. **A clean, strong level**: not buried in hiss, not clipping or distorting (unless distortion is the intended tone).
2. **A signal with no avoidable noise**: no hum, clicks, or cable crackle.
3. **A tone you chose on purpose**, going *through* a pedal or DI (not a passive instrument straight in).
4. **A spoken note** (written or recorded): what your front-end chain was and what trim/level you aimed for.

### Rubric (10 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 3 | Level is strong and clean (healthy on the meter, no clipping, not lost in hiss) |
| 3 | No avoidable noise (hum, clicks, crackle) |
| 2 | Tone is intentional and full (went through a buffer; not thin/dull from impedance) |
| 2 | You can state your signal chain and how you set the level |

**Pass bar:** someone could drop this track into a song and not have to apologize for the recording.

## Project 2: A Multitrack Built to a Click *(Day 14 · 15% of grade)*

**Goal:** demonstrate real multitrack craft: parts locked in time, stacked, repaired, and bounced. This is the Domain 6–9 test.

### Deliverable
A short, complete musical idea (a verse-and-chorus or a 60–120 second piece), recorded on the 424:
1. **A click track** you generated and recorded (kept as drums or as a muted scratch guide).
2. **At least two musical parts** (e.g., rhythm guitar + bass) **locked to the click.**
3. **At least one overdub** recorded against the existing parts with a real cue mix.
4. **At least one bounce (ping-pong)** that combined tracks to free up space.
5. **At least one punch-in repair** (a clean punch-in/out), on the project itself or, if your take is already clean, on a copied or practice take. Note where you punched and why.
6. **A spoken/written note:** your four-track plan (what went where, what you bounced, what you punched).
7. Deliver a stereo playback of the multitrack (a rough mix is fine).

### Rubric (15 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 4 | Timing: the parts lock to the click and to each other (steady, not drifting) |
| 3 | A clean overdub with a usable cue mix (the added part sits with the rest) |
| 3 | A bounce that combined parts with a committed, sensible balance |
| 2 | A punch-in repair with seams you cannot hear |
| 2 | Clean levels throughout (no clipping, no buried parts) |
| 1 | The track plan note is present and shows you planned the four tracks |

**Pass bar:** it sounds like a small arrangement that was *built*, in time, on purpose, not a pile of takes.

## Project 3: A Finished, Transferred Track *(Day 20 · 15% of grade)*

**Goal:** take a recording from tape all the way to an exported file: mixed on the 424, transferred to Ableton, finished, and bounced out. This is the Domain 9–10 test.

### Deliverable
A finished song (it can build on Project 2), delivered as an **exported audio file** (WAV or MP3):
1. **Mixed on the 424** (level, pan, EQ, optional effect) before transfer.
2. **Transferred to Ableton** by your choice of method, with a stated reason:
   - a **stereo bounce** (LINE OUT), or
   - **four stems** (TAPE OUT 1–4).
3. **Warped/handled** so the song sits sensibly in time (no obvious drift problems if you added in-the-box parts).
4. **Finished in the box**: balance, light processing, a clean intro/outro.
5. **Exported** to a shareable file at a correct level (loud and clean, not clipped).
6. **A short written note:** which transfer method you chose and why, and what finishing you did in Ableton.

### Rubric (15 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 4 | The 424 mix has a clear balance (every part audible, parts not fighting) |
| 3 | The transfer is clean and correctly leveled (no clipping, strong signal) |
| 3 | Drift handled appropriately (warped where needed; sits in time) |
| 2 | Finishing in the box is tasteful (balance/processing helps, does not flatten it) |
| 2 | The export is a clean, correctly-leveled file |
| 1 | The method note is present and the reason is sound |

**Pass bar:** you would send this file to a friend as "a song I recorded," without caveats about the recording quality.

## Final Project: The Tape-to-DAW Single *(Day 21 · 25% of grade)*

**Goal:** the capstone. A complete song that proves the whole craft in one piece: tracked to tape, built into an arrangement, mixed, transferred, finished, and exported. This is the graduation requirement and the Domain 1–10 test.

### Specification
1. **A complete song or substantial piece** (90 seconds to a few minutes), **guitar and/or bass focused**, recorded on the 424 MKIII.
2. **Tracked to a click** you made, with **at least one overdub** and **at least one bounce** (a real four-track arrangement, planned).
3. **Pedals used deliberately** as front end and/or printed effects (show you understand getting tone in).
4. **At least one punch-in repair** somewhere (on the song, or on a copied/practice take if the song needs no fix), demonstrating editing on tape.
5. **Mixed** (on the 424, in Ableton, or both) with intentional balance, pan, and EQ.
6. **Transferred to Ableton** (stereo bounce or stems, your choice) and **finished and exported** to a shareable file.
7. **A one-page written reflection:** your four-track plan, your signal chain, why you chose the transfer method you did, what came easiest, what is still hardest, and what you would do differently next time.

### Rubric (25 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 6 | Clean recordings throughout: strong levels, no avoidable noise, no clipping (the core skill) |
| 5 | Steady time: parts locked to the click and to each other across the whole song |
| 4 | A real four-track arrangement: planned tracks, a sensible bounce, parts that serve the song |
| 3 | A clear mix: balance, pan, and EQ that let you hear into the song |
| 3 | A clean transfer and finished, correctly-leveled export |
| 2 | Intentional tone and at least one clean punch repair |
| 2 | The reflection is thoughtful and honest |

**Graduation bar:** 23+/25, **and** a listener who knows nothing about your gear would hear it as "a song someone recorded," with the recording quality never getting in the way of the music. If you hit that, you did not just finish a course; you became a home recordist. Save the file; it is your "before."
