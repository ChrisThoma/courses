# Quizzes and Final Exam: REC-24

Rules: **closed book, machine allowed only where a question says "do."** Write answers on paper (or out loud), then grade with [ANSWER-KEYS.md](ANSWER-KEYS.md). Passing = 90%. The "do" questions are graded by honest self-assessment against the stated standard; record yourself and listen back if unsure.

---

## Quiz 1: The Machine & Your First Track *(Day 6 · 40 points · ~40 min)*

### Part A: The machine & signal flow (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What two machines does a Portastudio combine in one box?
2. Name the four stages of the signal flow, in order.
3. If a recorded track plays back silent, name one place on the path you would check.
4. What kind of cassette must you use in the 424 MKIII?
5. At what speed (relative to a normal cassette) does the 424 record, and why?
6. Why do you not flip a four-track cassette?
7. Roughly how many minutes does a C-60 give you at high speed?
8. What is the biggest mindset difference between a four-track and a DAW?

### Part B: The channel strip & levels (8 pts, 1 each)
1. In what order do you set the fader, trim, and EQ?
2. What does the meter tell you that the knob position does not?
3. What is the target level the meter should show on the loudest part?
4. What is the failure mode of recording too quiet? Too loud?
5. How do you use the swept mid to find a frequency to cut?
6. For a pedal or instrument output, which jack do you use and what do you set the INPUT select to?
7. What does the PAN control do *besides* place a sound left/right?
8. What is "gain staging" in one sentence?

### Part C: Instruments & pedals in (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Why does a passive guitar plugged straight into the line input sound thin and quiet?
2. What is the 424's line-input impedance, and what does a passive pickup want to see?
3. How does a pedal fix the problem?
4. Which instruments can drive the machine fine on their own, and why?
5. What does a DI box do in this context?
6. Name one type of pedal that works as an always-on buffer.
7. After the front end, what is the next thing you set, and how?
8. Why are a pedalboard and a Portastudio a natural pair?

### Part D: Recording to tape & the medium (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Name the three RECORD FUNCTION positions and what each does.
2. In DIRECT, which channel feeds track 2?
3. Which two transport buttons start recording?
4. What does RTZ do?
5. What must you do to a track the instant its take is good, and why?
6. What does dbx do, in one sentence?
7. Why must dbx be on for both recording and playback?
8. What is tape saturation, and how is it different from clipping?

### Part E: Do (8 pts) *(machine allowed)*
1. (4) Set a clean recording level on a guitar/bass-through-pedal by the meter, then record 20 seconds to a track; self-score on level (strong, not clipping) and noise.
2. (4) Route and record a part to track 3 via DIRECT, set the other tracks to SAFE, and play it back; self-score on correct routing and a clean capture.

## Quiz 2: Multitrack & Time *(Day 13 · 40 points · ~40 min)*

### Part A: The click track (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Does the 424 MKIII have a built-in metronome? So how do you play to a click?
2. Why does multitrack recording need a shared timing reference?
3. Which track traditionally holds the click, and why?
4. Do you keep dbx on for an audible click?
5. When would you defeat dbx on track 4?
6. What is the difference between a "keep" click and a "scratch" click?
7. Which musical part do you usually record first, and why?
8. What is the fix when you cannot lock to the click at your chosen tempo?

### Part B: Overdubbing & the cue mix (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What is overdubbing?
2. What three things does a cue mix blend?
3. Why does a bad cue mix wreck an overdub?
4. When do you use DIRECT vs BUSS for an overdub?
5. With one track holding the click, how many are left for music before bouncing?
6. Besides "is it on the click," what should you listen for in an overdub?
7. Where do beginners most commonly rush, and where do they drag?
8. Why set the cue mix *before* you hit record?

### Part C: Punch-in & repair (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What does punching in let you do?
2. What does rehearsal mode do, and why use it?
3. Where should you place punch points to hide the seams?
4. Why do you start playing a bar before the in-point?
5. What is the RC-30P for?
6. How do you check whether a punch is clean?
7. When should you stop punching and just re-record the take?
8. What two counter values do you need before an auto-punch?

### Part D: Bouncing & tone (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What does bouncing (ping-pong) let you do, and why is it needed on a four-track?
2. What gets permanently baked in when you bounce?
3. What is generation loss, and how do you keep it small?
4. Which parts should you bounce, and which should you keep live?
5. What does it mean to "print" an effect, and which effects should you print?
6. How do you set up an effect send/return given there is no dedicated return jack?
7. What is the dependable default for recording bass, and why?
8. Why is committing your tone at the source a feature, not a limitation?

### Part E: Do (8 pts) *(machine allowed)*
1. (4) Record a click, then a part locked to it; play back against the click and self-score on timing.
2. (4) Overdub a second part with a deliberate cue mix, then bounce two parts down to a third track; self-score on the overdub locking and the bounce balance.

## Final Exam *(Day 21 · 60 points · ~60 min)*

### Part A: Speed fundamentals (10 pts, 1 each; answer each in ≤15 s)
1. The four stages of the signal flow? 2. Type I or Type II tape? 3. The three RECORD FUNCTION positions? 4. In DIRECT, which channel feeds track 4? 5. What does RTZ do? 6. dbx on or off for normal recording? 7. What does a pedal fix between a passive guitar and the 424? 8. Which output carries the stereo mix? 9. Which outputs carry the four stems? 10. What do you set first, before EQ or fader?

### Part B: Signal, levels & instruments in (12 pts, 2 each)
1. Draw or describe the signal path from a guitar to the LINE OUT, naming each stage.
2. Explain gain staging and the target level for tape, and name both failure modes.
3. Explain the impedance problem with a passive instrument and the fix.
4. What is tape saturation, and how do you use it on purpose without clipping?
5. Explain DIRECT vs BUSS routing and when you use each.
6. Why use Type II tape and keep dbx on, and when is dbx defeated?

### Part C: Time, overdubs & repair (10 pts, 1 each)
1. How do you "play to a click" on a machine with no metronome?
2. Which part do you record first, and why?
3. What three things does a cue mix blend?
4. Why must every part lock to the same click?
5. What does rehearsal mode let you do before a punch?
6. Where do you place punch points, and why?
7. Why start playing before the punch-in point?
8. When do you re-record instead of punching?
9. What is the RC-30P?
10. What is the most common beginner timing mistake?

### Part D: Bouncing & mixing (10 pts, 1 each)
1. What does bouncing let you do?
2. What is baked in when you bounce, and what is generation loss?
3. Which parts do you bounce, and which stay live?
4. Name the four mixing tools on the 424, in the order you reach for them.
5. Why is level most of a mix?
6. What is a classic stereo pan spread?
7. Why is cutting EQ usually better than boosting?
8. Why mix with everything playing rather than soloing?
9. What does the effect send/return loop add at mixdown?
10. What does it mean that a mixdown is a "performance"?

### Part E: Transfer & finishing (10 pts, 1 each)
1. What is the stereo-bounce transfer, and what do you end up with?
2. What are the four stems, and where in the path do they come from?
3. At what level does the 424 output, and how do you set the DAW input?
4. What is tape drift, and why does it happen?
5. What does Ableton's Warp do for a transferred mix?
6. When do stems beat a stereo bounce?
7. Why are the four stems automatically in sync with each other?
8. Why record to tape and finish in the box rather than recording into the computer?
9. What is the reamp / print-back move?
10. Name one honest limit of the tape-and-DAW workflow.

### Part F: Do / Applied (8 pts) *(machine allowed; record it)*
1. (3) Set a clean level and record a guitar/bass part to tape, then play it back; self-score on level and noise.
2. (2) Mix a multitrack on the 424 with level, pan, and one EQ move, and do a mixdown pass; self-score on balance.
3. (3) Transfer a mix to Ableton (stereo bounce or stems), set the input level correctly, and export a file; self-score on a clean, correctly-leveled transfer.
