# Project 2: The Beat

**Due:** end of Week 2 · **Weight:** 20% · **Rubric dimensions:** D3, D4, D5, D6, D8

Time to make something that bangs. Project 1 was a loop; Project 2 is a **beat**: drums and a flipped musical element, grooving together, that you'd be happy to loop for two minutes. This is the set you'll **arrange and mix into your capstone** in Week 3, so build it clean.

## The brief

Produce a **16–32 bar sample-based beat** in Ableton Live that contains:

1. **Chopped/sliced drums**: either a re-sequenced break, a built-and-layered one-shot kit, or (best) both combined. The drums must **groove** (deliberate swing/velocity/pocket, not flat quantization).
2. **A flipped melodic/harmonic element**: a chopped or pitched sample that carries the harmony (your Project 1 flip, evolved, is fair game and encouraged).
3. **At least one element that went through the resampling loop**: something you committed to audio and then chopped, stretched, or re-processed (Lecture 9).
4. **A low-end element**: a bass, a sub layer, or a pitched-down chop that anchors the low frequencies (kept mono).

## Constraints

- **Tracks separated and named.** Drums, bass, melody, texture each on their own track(s). You're mixing this next week: a messy session now is a tax on future-you.
- **One clear groove.** Pick a tempo and feel and commit. Better a simple beat with a deep pocket than a busy one that doesn't nod.
- **Show the techniques.** Your set must demonstrate: a chop (D4), a sampled instrument *or* layered kit (D5), creative warping/resampling (D3), and at least one moment of deliberate sound design (D6).
- **Levels under control.** Master peaks ~−6 dB; nothing clips. Rough balance only: full mixing is Week 3.

## Deliverables

```
project2_beat/
├── beat.als            # the Live set: tidy, tracks named, parts separated
├── beat.wav            # a 16–32 bar loop bounce (−6 dB-ish peak)
├── stems/ (optional)   # bounced stems: drums.wav, bass.wav, melody.wav, texture.wav
└── LOG.md              # production log (graded)
```

### LOG.md must contain

- **The drums:** break or kit or both? What groove/feel, and how you got the pocket (swing amount, velocity, micro-timing).
- **The flip:** the source and how you chopped/pitched it into the harmony.
- **The resampling move:** the one element you committed to audio and re-processed: what and why.
- **One thing you're proud of and one thing you'd fix**: honest D8 self-critique.
- **Self-assessment:** claimed levels on D3, D4, D5 with one artifact-pointing sentence each.

## Grading rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| **Drums & groove (D4):** chopped/built drums with a real pocket: it nods | 30% |
| **The flip (D4/D5):** a genuinely transformed musical element carrying harmony | 25% |
| **Time & resampling (D3):** correct warping + at least one real resampling-loop transformation | 20% |
| **Arrangement-readiness & cleanliness (D1):** named, separated tracks; controlled levels; mono low end | 15% |
| **Log & self-critique (D8):** decisions and honest assessment articulated | 10% |

**The trap to avoid:** the "loop that never moves and never grooves." Two failure modes to check yourself against: (1) *flat drums*: if every hit is the same velocity dead on the grid, add groove and ghost notes until it breathes; (2) *the static 1-bar loop*: even at this stage, give it some life (a fill, a filter move, a dropped element) across its 16–32 bars so it's not literally one bar copied. You're proving you can make something *feel good*, not just something technically assembled.
