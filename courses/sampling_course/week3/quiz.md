## Answer Key

**Q1 — B.** Elastic, tempo-locked audio (warping) is the defining feature.

**Q2 — C.** Classic mode, tuned via root/Transpose first.

**Q3 — B.** Slicing writes MIDI that reproduces the original; the flip is in editing that MIDI.

**Q4 — B.** Re-Pitch links pitch to speed (tape behavior).

**Q5 — B.** Recording Live's output back to a new clip for further transformation.

**Q6 — B.** Low-mid buildup; subtractive EQ (high-pass + complementary cuts) clears it.

**Q7 — B.** Dynamic ducking of bass under the kick = the pumping groove and clash fix.

**Q8 — B.** 16-bit/44.1 with dither, sensible LUFS via the limiter (not crushed), tail rendered.

**Q9.** **Classic** — playable melodic instrument from a sustained sound; **One-Shot** — single hits (kick, stab, vocal chop); **Slice** — chop a phrase/break and re-sequence.

**Q10.** No length/transformation exemption exists — short, altered samples can still infringe both the composition and master copyrights, and fair use is narrow and unreliable. Three clean routes (any three): clear/license it; use royalty-free/Creative-Commons/public-domain material or official sample packs; sample your own recordings; interpolate/re-perform the part yourself.

**Q11.** Make something (played instrument, heavy effect chain, extreme stretch) → bounce/record it to a new audio clip → chop/warp/process *that* → repeat. Requires-audio example: slicing a performance you played on a sampled instrument — you must resample to audio before you can slice it. (Or: committing a CPU-heavy/fixed effect chain so you can stack more processing.)

**Q12.** Any four: drop/add elements (esp. dropping drums); automate a filter sweep for builds/breakdowns; use silence/negative space before a drop; vary sections (intro/breakdown/outro with fewer or different elements); add risers/impacts (resampled) at transitions; automate reverb/volume swells. Core idea: contrast through subtraction/addition and automation.

**Q13.** **EQ (static):** cut one element where the other lives (e.g., dip the bass at the kick's punch frequency, high-pass the chord off the bass region) so they occupy different bands — fixes *frequency* masking permanently. **Dynamics (sidechain):** duck one under the other in time (kick ducks bass) so they share the same range but not the same *moment* — fixes *temporal* masking and adds groove. EQ separates by frequency; sidechain separates by time.

**Q14.** **(a) Pad:** load the "ah" as a clip, Warp on, Complex Pro (or Texture/granular), stretch it over many bars so transients dissolve into an evolving texture; tune it, maybe loop the sustain in Simpler/Sampler across a couple of zones; add filter+envelope movement; **resample** the best 4 bars to a clean pad sample. **(b) Chopped:** take that pad bounce (or the raw "ah"), slice it (Simpler Slice / Drum Rack) by beat or transient, re-pitch and re-order the slices into a rhythmic phrase, apply groove/velocity; resample again if you want to process the chop further. Credit for naming modes, the stretch, and at least one resample point.
