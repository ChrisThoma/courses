# Supplemental Resources — Watch / Read / Listen Guide

The lectures are written as **self-contained studio sessions** — you can read and work through them without leaving the file. This guide maps each lecture to the best external material so you can watch a fast producer do the same moves and go as deep as you want.

**How to use this file**
- Each lecture lists **▶ Watch** (video), **📄 Read** (article/docs), **🔊 Listen** (ear training), and **⬇ Get** (sources/tools).
- A ⭐ marks the highest-value resource for that lecture — if you do nothing else, do that one.
- **Before** the lecture: skim the ⭐. **After**: go deep on the rest.
- Product UIs and links churn; if something moved, search the title — the *techniques* are stable. Verified June 2026.

> **The one channel to bookmark:** Ableton's own **Learning Music** (learningmusic.ableton.com) and **Learning Synths** sites, and the **Ableton YouTube channel** (@Ableton). For sample-based production specifically, the producer-educators worth your time include **Andrew Huang**, **You Suck at Producing**, **In The Mix**, **Underbelly**, **busy works beats**, and **SeamlessR** — search a technique + their name and you'll find a focused tutorial.

## Tools & legal sources (set these up in Week 1)

- ⬇ ⭐ **WhoSampled.com** — the database of what sampled what. Your primary ear-training tool: listen to original→flip back to back. https://www.whosampled.com
- ⬇ **Legal sources for breaks & samples:** **Splice** (subscription, licensed), **Ableton's own Packs** (many free, fully licensed), **Looperman** (free, check per-upload license), **Freesound.org** (Creative Commons — check each license), and **archive.org** (public-domain & live recordings). For drum breaks specifically, search "royalty free drum break pack" — several producers release cleared break collections.
- ⬇ **Splice / Ableton Pack** browsers integrate into Live's browser — drag straight in.
- 📄 **Ableton Live manual** — the authoritative reference for Simpler, Sampler, warping, Racks, and export. https://www.ableton.com/en/manual/

## Week 1 — Foundations

### Lecture 1 — What Is Sampling? Culture, Craft, and the Law
- ⭐ ▶ **"Sampling — the documentary / history of sampling" type overviews** (search "history of sampling hip hop documentary"). The classic reference film is *Copyright Criminals* (PBS) — on the culture and the law at once.
- 🔊 ⭐ **WhoSampled** — do the Lab-1 exercise here: J Dilla, Kanye, DJ Premier, The Avalanches, Madlib entries are master classes in flipping.
- 📄 **"A history of sampling"** features on Red Bull Music Academy archive / Resident Advisor (search the titles; RBMA Daily archive is a goldmine).
- 📄 **The law, plainly:** search "sampling law clearing samples explained" — the **Soundfly** and **Splice blog** explainers are accurate and readable. (For the case law: *Bridgeport v. Dimension* and *VMG Salsoul v. Ciccone* are the two to know.)

### Lecture 2 — How Ableton Live Thinks
- ⭐ ▶ **Ableton — "Live Basics" official video series** (ableton.com/learn-live or @Ableton on YouTube): Session vs Arrangement, the browser, and warping, from the source.
- 📄 ⭐ **Ableton manual — "Audio Clips, Tempo, and Warping"** chapter. The definitive explanation of warp modes and markers. https://www.ableton.com/en/manual/audio-clips-tempo-and-warping/
- ▶ **"Warping in Ableton explained"** (search — In The Mix and You Suck at Producing both have excellent ~10-min versions).

### Lecture 3 — Simpler: The Core Sampling Instrument
- ⭐ ▶ **"Ableton Simpler tutorial — everything you need"** (search; the Ableton official Simpler overview + a producer deep-dive). Watch one of each.
- 📄 **Ableton manual — "Simpler."** Classic/One-Shot/Slice modes, warp, envelopes. https://www.ableton.com/en/manual/live-instrument-reference/#simpler
- ▶ **"How to make a melody from one sample in Simpler"** — search; this is Lecture 3.3 in video form.

### Lecture 4 — Audio Fundamentals for Samplers
- ⭐ ▶ **"Gain staging explained"** (search — there are excellent 10-min versions). The most useful non-obvious skill there is for clean tracks.
- 📄 **"Sample rate and bit depth explained"** — the **Sound on Sound** and **iZotope Learn** explainers are clear and correct.
- 🔊 **Transients:** find a "what is a transient / transient shaping" video and *watch the waveform* while you listen — train eye and ear together.

## Week 2 — Construction

### Lecture 6 — Chopping & Slicing in Depth
- ⭐ ▶ **"How to chop samples in Ableton" / "flip a sample like [your favorite producer]"** — search; pick a tutorial in a genre you love and follow along.
- 🔊 ⭐ **The Amen break — its story** (the "Winstons / Amen Brother" mini-docs on YouTube are essential listening for any sampler). Then load the break and chop it yourself.
- 📄 **Ableton manual — "Slice to New MIDI Track."** The exact workflow. (In the manual's Drum Rack / clip sections.)
- ▶ **J Dilla / Madlib chopping breakdowns** — search "how Dilla chopped samples"; the *Dilla Time* talks and breakdowns teach feel, not just mechanics.

### Lecture 7 — The Full Sampler: Multisampling & Modulation
- ⭐ ▶ **"Ableton Sampler tutorial — multisampling"** (search). If you don't have Sampler, watch anyway for the concept, then do the Simpler version.
- 📄 **Ableton manual — "Sampler."** Zones, modulation matrix, LFOs. https://www.ableton.com/en/manual/live-instrument-reference/#sampler
- ▶ **"How sample libraries work / make your own sampled instrument"** — search; SeamlessR and others cover velocity layers and round-robin well.

### Lecture 8 — Drums from Samples
- ⭐ ▶ **"Ableton Drum Rack tutorial"** + **"how to layer drums / kick"** (search; two short videos). Layering is the highest-leverage drum skill.
- ▶ ⭐ **"Groove / swing in Ableton — the Groove Pool"** — search; this is where your beats start to *nod.*
- 📄 **Ableton manual — "Drum Racks"** and **"Groove."**
- 🔊 **"What makes Dilla's drums feel like that"** — micro-timing breakdowns. Listen for the *late* snare.

### Lecture 9 — Pitch, Time & Texture
- ⭐ ▶ **"Resampling in Ableton — why it changes everything"** (search). The most important workflow video of the week.
- ▶ **"Extreme time-stretching / making pads from anything"** — search "Paulstretch / Complex Pro pad from a vocal." Andrew Huang has classic versions.
- 📄 **Ableton manual — warp modes** (re-read with sound-design eyes): Re-Pitch, Texture, Complex Pro and the Grain/Flux controls.
- ▶ **Granulator III / granular synthesis intro** (Suite) — search; for the granular mindset.

## Week 3 — Production & Polish

### Lecture 11 — Sound Design with Samples
- ⭐ ▶ **"Audio Effect Racks & Macros in Ableton"** (search) + **"sound design with resampling"**. Racks + resampling is the producer's core loop.
- ▶ **"Turn a bad sample into a great one" / "sound design from field recordings"** — Andrew Huang and Red Means Recording are excellent here.
- 📄 **Ableton manual — "Instrument, Drum, and Effect Racks"** and the **Audio Effects reference** (Saturator, Redux, Auto Filter, Echo).

### Lecture 12 — Arrangement: From Loop to Song
- ⭐ ▶ **"How to arrange a song from a loop in Ableton"** (search — this is *the* skill video; In The Mix and Underbelly have strong versions).
- 📄 ⭐ **"Arrangement / song structure for electronic music"** — read one solid explainer on tension/release and section grammar (Splice blog, EDMProd).
- 🔊 **The "map a track's structure" exercise** from Lecture 12 — do it for three tracks you love. Best arrangement training there is.
- ▶ **"How to finish tracks"** — search; the psychology of finishing matters as much as the technique (the "finish more music" talks).

### Lecture 13 — Mixing Sample-Based Music
- ⭐ ▶ **"Mixing basics in Ableton — EQ, compression, sidechain"** (search). Then **"how to fix a muddy mix."**
- 📄 ⭐ **"Subtractive EQ / high-pass everything"** explainers and **"sidechain compression in Ableton"** — the two ideas that fix 80% of sample-based mixes.
- ▶ **"Mix referencing / mono checking"** — search; learn to A/B level-matched and to mono-check.
- 📄 **Ableton manual — EQ Eight, Compressor, Utility.**

### Lecture 14 — Finishing & the Law
- ⭐ ▶ **"Mastering in Ableton (the simple version)" / "how loud should my track be (LUFS)"** — search; keep it light per the lecture.
- 📄 ⭐ **"How to clear a sample" / "sample clearance explained"** — Splice blog, Soundfly, and the **DIY Musician (CD Baby)** posts are practical and current.
- 📄 **Ableton manual — "Exporting Audio and Video"** and dithering. https://www.ableton.com/en/manual/exporting-audio-and-video/
- 📄 **Creative Commons & royalty-free licensing** — read the actual license types on creativecommons.org so you know what "free" means (attribution? commercial use?).

## If you want to go further (post-course)

- **Books:** *Dilla Time* (Dan Charnas) — the definitive read on rhythm and sampling feel; *Making Music* (Dennis DeSantis, free from Ableton) — 74 strategies for finishing music, the best anti-writer's-block resource for producers.
- **Channels for the long haul:** @Ableton, Andrew Huang, You Suck at Producing, In The Mix, Underbelly, Red Means Recording, SeamlessR, busy works beats.
- **The only assignment that never ends:** sample your own life, finish a track every couple of weeks, and study the flips of the producers you love until you can hear the moves. That's the whole career.
