# Project 1 — The Flip

**Due:** end of Week 1 · **Weight:** 15% · **Rubric dimensions:** D1, D2, D3, D5

The oldest move in sampling: take one source and turn it into a musical loop that *grooves on its own.* No drums required yet, no arrangement — just prove you can hear music in a sound and pull it out with Live's core tools. This is the seed your Week-2 beat will grow from, so pick a source you actually like.

## The brief

Starting from **one source sample**, produce an **8–16 bar looping musical idea** in Ableton Live that:

1. Is built primarily from **that one source** (you may add at most one supporting element — a bass note, a pad — and simple drums are optional, not required).
2. **Loops cleanly** at a tempo you chose on purpose, in time and in tune.
3. Sounds **musical and intentional** — not just the original playing back, and not a random mess. A listener should feel it could be the foundation of a track.

## Constraints (these are the point)

- **One source.** Discipline beats variety here. Squeezing music out of *one* sound is the skill.
- **Use Simpler.** At least one of: a Classic-mode playable instrument built from the source, *or* a Slice-mode chop re-sequenced into a new phrase. (Ideally show both.)
- **Warp it correctly.** The source must be on the grid by your hand — set the tempo and warp deliberately; don't just hope the auto-guess held.
- **Level it.** Gain-stage so it peaks around −6 dB on the master and nothing clips.
- **Legal-to-learn-on is fine; note your source.** Use anything to practice. If you'd want to release it, say in your log how you'd clear or replace the source.

## Suggested approaches (pick one, or combine)

- **The chop-and-resequence.** Slice a melodic loop, trigger the slices in a new order to build a fresh phrase (the classic hip-hop flip).
- **The pitched instrument.** Find a chord or single note, build a Classic-mode instrument from it, and *play a new progression* the original never contained.
- **The loop-and-filter.** Find a 2–4 bar section that already grooves, warp it tight, and transform it with pitch, filtering, and edits so it becomes *yours.*

## Deliverables

```
project1_flip/
├── flip.als            # the Live set, tidy: tracks named, source on its own track
├── flip.wav            # an 8–16 bar bounce of the final loop (exported, −6 dB-ish peak)
├── source/             # the original source file(s) you started from
└── LOG.md              # your production log (see below) — this is graded as much as the audio
```

### LOG.md must contain

- **The source:** what it is, where it's from, and *why you chose it* (what music did you hear in it?).
- **The moves:** the 3–5 key things you did to transform it (mode, slicing, tuning, warp mode, edits). One line each.
- **One before/after:** describe (or link timestamps to) how the source sounded vs. the flip.
- **Release note:** if you wanted to release this, how would you clear or replace the source? (One sentence — proves you absorbed Lecture 1.)
- **Self-assessment:** your claimed level on D2 and D3 with one sentence of evidence each.

## Grading rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| **Source & taste (D2):** a non-obvious, musical choice; you heard something in it | 20% |
| **Transformation (D4/D5):** it's genuinely *flipped* — Simpler used with intent, not just looped | 30% |
| **Time & tune (D3):** warped to the grid by hand, in time, in key, loops seamlessly | 25% |
| **Cleanliness (D1):** gain-staged, no clipping, tidy named project | 15% |
| **Log quality:** decisions articulated; release note present | 10% |

**The trap to avoid:** the "untransformed loop." If your flip is just the original section playing back at a slightly different tempo, you've warped, not flipped. The test: could a listener who knows the original *not immediately recognize it as the same thing*? If recognition is instant and you added nothing, push it further — re-sequence, re-pitch, re-harmonize. The grade is in the *transformation*, not the source.
