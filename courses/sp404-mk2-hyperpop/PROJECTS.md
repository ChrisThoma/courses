# Projects: MUS-47H

Projects are where the techniques turn into *a song someone else could hear.* Each has a bounced deliverable and a grading rubric. **Bounce everything to a file** (resample to a pad, record the output, or capture over USB) and grade yourself by *listening back cold, on a different speaker, next to a reference track.* "It basically works" is a 70, not a 95. The standard everywhere is **hard, tuned, and finished**, not clever or complicated.

> **Why the reference A/B?** Because this genre has a public, streaming, free-to-hear standard, and your ear already knows it. Playing your bounce immediately after a real track is uncomfortable and unbeatable as feedback: the gap you hear is the curriculum for your next session. The files also become a record of how far you have come, which by Day 21 will be farther than you think.

---

## Project 1: The Palette + An Eight-Bar Loop *(Day 7 · 10% of grade)*

**Goal:** prove the Week-1 foundation: a designed hyperpop palette and the three instrumental legs assembled at full energy. This is the Domain 3–5 test.

### Deliverable
1. **The palette, on pads:** a four-piece designed kit (hard kick, clipped snare/clap, bright hat, one self-recorded metallic percussion), two printed 808s (one round, one violent), and a chromatic synth instrument with at least two chord-stab prints.
2. **An eight-bar loop** bounced to a file: drums, tuned 808 bassline, and stab rhythm, at 150–180 BPM, with one audible variation between halves.
3. **One sentence in writing:** your song's home note/key (by pad or by name) and which reference track you A/B'd against.

### Rubric (10 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 3 | The kit is genuinely hard and bright: designed transients, clean trims, the metallic piece has identity |
| 3 | The 808 is tuned to the loop, locked with the kick (no mud, no flam), and reads on a phone speaker |
| 2 | The stabs are in tune with the bassline and rhythmically interlock with the drums |
| 2 | The loop is at genre tempo and energy, with a variation, and survives a straight-faced A/B with the reference |

**Pass bar:** thirty seconds of the loop, played after your reference track, sounds like the same genre, made by someone who meant it.

## Project 2: A Hook and a Drop *(Day 14 · 15% of grade)*

**Goal:** demonstrate the course's center: an original hook, written and recorded in your own voice, processed into the hyperpop vocal, and assembled with the palette into a build and drop. This is the Domain 6–8 test.

### Deliverable
A bounced passage (build → gap → drop, roughly 12–16 bars total):
1. **An original hook** you wrote: short, blunt, repeated; no interpolated melodies.
2. **The vocal chain in evidence:** hard-tune (Auto Pitch, correct KEY, high AT PITCH), at least one double, and at least one pitched-up or formant-shifted layer, printed as a stack.
3. **A build** that thins the palette, uses a self-made riser, and programs a real gap before the downbeat.
4. **A drop** where the full palette and the hook stack land together on the one, with an impact, and a variation at the halfway bar.
5. **A spoken or written note:** which of the three pitch-up roads your lead uses, and one thing you would fix with another week.

### Rubric (15 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 4 | The vocal is committed and processed correctly: snapped to the right key, no sour notes, the stack sounds like one big voice |
| 3 | The hook is memorable at conversational bluntness and sits on top of the drop, clearly audible |
| 3 | The build genuinely withholds (thinned, rising, gap present) so the drop lands with force |
| 3 | The drop is maximal without mud: kick/808 locked, stabs and voice not fighting, phone-audible |
| 2 | Self-made ear candy (riser + impact) marks the seam; nothing downloaded as-is |

**Pass bar:** played cold for a friend, the drop makes them react (a nod, a grin, an eyebrow), and the voice in it is not obviously a beginner's first vocal, because by now it is not.

## Project 3: The Arranged Demo *(Day 20 · 15% of grade)*

**Goal:** the whole song standing: pop structure, contrast, dressed seams, and a first real mix. This is the Domain 9–10 test and the dress rehearsal for Day 21.

### Deliverable
The full song bounced start to end (roughly 1:30–2:30):
1. **A complete structure:** at minimum intro, verse, pre-chorus, chorus, a second contrasting section (verse 2, breakdown, or bridge), final chorus, ending. Written map included (pattern numbers → sections).
2. **A verse vocal** distinct from the hook (more words, lighter processing) so the voice itself signals the section.
3. **Every seam dressed** at the right size, exits announced, one saved trick spent at the biggest seam.
4. **Real dynamics:** the quietest moment genuinely quiet; the final chorus the biggest thing in the song.
5. **A first mix:** chorus mixed first, levels and carving done, master clean, checked in mono and on a phone.

### Rubric (15 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 4 | The form reads blind: a stranger can tell verse from chorus and feel where the song is going |
| 3 | Real contrast: the energy curve has valleys and a highest peak, not a plateau |
| 3 | Transitions are sized to their seams and nothing feels spliced |
| 3 | The mix translates: every survivor of the mute test audible, master unclipped, phone and mono pass |
| 2 | The verse vocal works and differs audibly from the chorus voice |

**Pass bar:** you could play it start to finish for a friend without touching the machine or explaining anything, and they would know when it was about to end.

## Final Project: The Song *(Day 21 · 25% of grade)*

**Goal:** the capstone. One complete, original hyperpop song that proves the whole chain: design, writing, recording, processing, structure, and mix. The Domain 1–10 test in one file.

### Specification
1. **A finished song, roughly 1:30–2:30**, made entirely on the SP-404 MK2, bounced to a single audio file.
2. **All original or clean sources:** your own voice for all vocals, self-designed drums and 808, synth sources you may legally release, no interpolated melodies.
3. **The full genre kit in evidence:** designed palette at genre tempo, a hard-tuned and stacked hook, a lighter verse vocal, at least one vocal-chop or stutter moment, self-made ear candy, a build and drop-chorus, and a bridge or breakdown where the song eats its own material.
4. **A real pop form** with genuine dynamics: a stranger can follow it, the last chorus towers, and it ends on purpose (cold stop, tape stop, or decay, chosen, not defaulted).
5. **A mix that translates:** vocal on top, 808 phone-audible, master clean, mono-safe, and it passes the playlist test between your two reference tracks.
6. **A short written reflection (½ page):** what came easiest, what is still hardest, which of the song's sounds is most *yours*, and what the second song will do differently.

### Rubric (25 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 6 | It is a finished song: complete form, follows blind, ends on purpose (the core) |
| 5 | The voice: committed takes, correct tuning, a stack that sounds like a record, hook on top and memorable |
| 4 | The palette: hard, bright, tuned, and identifiably yours (the metallic hit, the self-made candy) |
| 4 | Dynamics and seams: real valleys and one towering peak; transitions sized and placed like the records |
| 3 | The mix: playlist test survived, phone and mono pass, distortion all deliberate |
| 3 | Craft breadth (chops, candy, bridge-from-own-material all present) + reflection honest |

**Graduation bar:** 23+/25, **and** the playlist test verdict: a listener hearing your song between your two references would not ask "what is this doing here." If you hit that, you did not just finish a course; you made a hyperpop record on a sampler with your own voice in it, which three weeks ago was the least likely sentence in your life. Save the file; it is your "before."
