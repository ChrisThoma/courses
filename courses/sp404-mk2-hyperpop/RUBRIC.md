# Mastery Rubric: MUS-47H

Grade yourself in each domain. **Mastery = Level 4 in every technical domain.** The honest test for almost every domain is the same: *bounce your work, put it in a playlist next to a real hyperpop track, and listen cold the next day.* Your memory flatters your work; a playlist does not. Levels:

- **1 (Novice):** Aware of the concept, can only do it by hunting through menus, with stops and luck.
- **2 (Developing):** Can do it slowly with effort, frequent mistakes, and results that sound rough.
- **3 (Proficient):** Can do it reliably and musically at a working pace; rare errors; results sound clean.
- **4 (Solid):** Automatic and fast; you do it without thinking about the machine, so your attention is on the music.

---

## Domain 1: The genre ear

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | "Hyperpop" is a vibe word; cannot say what makes a track one |
| 2 | Recognizes the obvious markers (pitched vocals, distortion) but not the craft under them |
| 3 | Can name a track's drum palette, bass treatment, vocal chain, and structure by ear |
| 4 | Hears a new track once and can describe how to rebuild its main moves on the SP |

**Test:** Play any hyperpop track you have never analyzed and name, in one listen, the tempo range, the vocal processing, one drum choice, and one signature move.

## Domain 2: Workflow on the machine

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Fumbles the basics (record, trim, chop, pattern) from MUS-45H |
| 2 | Can do the basics but slowly; loses ideas to menu time |
| 3 | The MUS-45H loop (sample, edit, sequence, resample) runs without thought; backups are habit |
| 4 | The machine disappears; ideas reach pads at the speed you think of them |

**Test:** From power-on, get a new sound recorded, trimmed, into a pattern, and resampled in under ten minutes.

## Domain 3: The drum palette

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Drums are stock sounds at default settings; soft and generic |
| 2 | Has hard sounds but they clash or smear together |
| 3 | Builds a kit that is hard, bright, and distinct: kick, clap/snare, metallic percussion, rolls |
| 4 | The kit is a sound of its own; distortion and layering choices are deliberate and repeatable |

**Test:** Build a four-piece hyperpop kit from raw sources in one sitting and program a pattern that slaps next to a reference.

## Domain 4: The 808

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | No usable bass, or a bass that is inaudible on small speakers |
| 2 | Has an 808 but it is out of tune or fights the kick |
| 3 | Designs a distorted 808 from one note, tunes it to the song, plays a bassline in chromatic mode |
| 4 | The 808 is tuned, audible on a phone, locked with the kick, and has a personality (drive, length, movement) |

**Test:** Make an 808 from a single sampled note, tune it to a loop by ear, and play a bassline that a phone speaker still conveys.

## Domain 5: Synth building

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | No melodic parts beyond the vocal |
| 2 | Can place a sampled chord but it sounds thin or out of tune |
| 3 | Builds bright leads and stabs from sampled notes; plays and sequences them chromatically |
| 4 | Layers and resamples sampled sources into big, wide, supersaw-style parts on demand |

**Test:** From one or two sampled synth notes, build a bright lead and a chord stab, in tune with your song, inside 20 minutes.

## Domain 6: Vocal recording

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Avoids recording, or takes are clipped/too quiet/roomy |
| 2 | Gets a take down but needs many tries; levels inconsistent |
| 3 | Records clean, healthy-level takes on demand; doubles match the original take's timing |
| 4 | Recording is casual; captures multiple usable takes and doubles quickly, mic technique is consistent |

**Test:** Record a hook take plus one tight double, both clean and level-matched, in under ten minutes.

## Domain 7: Vocal processing

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Vocals are dry, or processing is random knob-turning |
| 2 | Can turn on Auto Pitch but the key is wrong or the effect is smeared |
| 3 | Sets KEY and AT PITCH correctly, uses FORMANT and pitch-up deliberately, stacks a double |
| 4 | Owns a personal vocal chain: hard-tune, pitch-up, stacks, and chops chosen per song, executed fast |

**Test:** Take a dry hook take to a finished hyperpop vocal (tuned, doubled, one pitched layer) that survives A/B with a reference.

## Domain 8: Sound design & ear candy

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | The track is only drums, bass, synth, vocal; no motion between them |
| 2 | Uses downloaded risers/impacts as-is |
| 3 | Makes own risers, impacts, stutters, and tape stops by resampling; places them musically |
| 4 | Ear candy is a signature: self-made transitions and glitches that other people would sample |

**Test:** Make a riser, an impact, and a tape-stop transition from your own material, and place all three in a section change.

## Domain 9: Arrangement & song form

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Only ever makes a loop |
| 2 | Chains a couple of patterns; sections do not contrast |
| 3 | Builds verse, pre-chorus, chorus with real contrast, a build, and clean transitions |
| 4 | The form serves the song: sections are the right lengths, energy is managed, nothing overstays |

**Test:** Arrange existing material into intro / verse / pre / chorus / second section / out, under 2:30, that a listener can follow without being told.

## Domain 10: The mix & the finish

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Elements bury each other; loudness is accidental clipping |
| 2 | Roughly balanced but dull, or loud but muddy |
| 3 | Every element audible, distortion is chosen, survives mono and a phone speaker |
| 4 | The bounce sits in a playlist next to real tracks without embarrassment; loud, bright, and clear |

**Test:** Bounce the song, play it after a reference on a phone speaker, and confirm the vocal, kick, and 808 all read.

Expected trajectory: end of Week 1 → Domains 1–5 at Level 3, Domain 2 pushing 4. End of Week 2 → Domains 6–8 at Level 3, with Domain 7 the one to watch (the vocal chain is the course's center of gravity). What this course locks in is the workflow, the vocal chain, and the habit of finishing.
