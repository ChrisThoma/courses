# Quizzes and Final Exam: MUS-47H

Rules: **closed book, machine allowed only where a question says "at the machine."** Write answers on paper (or out loud), then grade with `ANSWER-KEYS.md`. Passing = 90%. The "at the machine" questions are graded by honest self-assessment against the stated standard; bounce it and listen back if you are unsure.

---

## Quiz 1: The Genre & The Palette *(Day 6 · 40 points · ~40 min)*

### Part A: The genre (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Who founded PC Music, where, and in roughly what year?
2. What was "bubblegum bass"?
3. Name the producer whose metallic sound design and pop-plus-abrasion approach shaped the genre, and the 2016 Charli XCX release that carried it toward the mainstream.
4. What 2019 album by 100 gecs blew the sound up, and how did the genre get its name that same year?
5. What is digicore, in one sentence?
6. Name the three instrumental legs of the hyperpop palette.
7. What tempo range is the course's home base, and is it faster or slower than standard pop?
8. What is the "exaggeration test"?

### Part B: Drums (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Name three ways a hyperpop kit inverts a lo-fi kit.
2. Why is the kick kept short and mid-forward in this genre?
3. Describe the signature snare/clap treatment and name the two effects that get you there.
4. What is "the metallic one," and why does the course insist you record it yourself?
5. Where does hardness live, if not in volume?
6. Describe the design loop, all five steps.
7. What is a hi-hat roll, and name one way to program one and one way to perform one.
8. Why keep the raw source after printing a designed drum?

### Part C: The 808 (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What is an 808, and where does the name come from?
2. Why does distortion make an 808 *more* audible on small speakers?
3. Which three effects are the drive options, and roughly how do their flavors differ?
4. How do you find your song's key with no music theory, and which two later settings depend on that answer?
5. How do you sequence a bassline in chromatic mode?
6. Why does "simple and tuned" beat "busy and clever" for basslines?
7. Name the three fixes for a kick/808 fight, in preference order.
8. What is the phone test, and what does failing it tell you?

### Part D: Synths (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What is a sawtooth wave, and what is a supersaw?
2. What genre did hyperpop inherit the supersaw from?
3. How do you fake oscillator detune on a sampler?
4. What does JUNO Chorus do to a stacked saw?
5. How do you build a chord stab with no theory?
6. Why should the stab rhythm interlock with the drums instead of shadowing them?
7. Why leave sounds bright in Week 1 rather than taming them immediately?
8. What is the "hole" you leave in an instrumental loop, and for what?

### Part E: At the machine (8 pts) *(machine allowed)*
1. (4) Play your assembled eight-bar loop and phone-test it; self-score on hardness, tuning, and whether the 808 reads.
2. (4) Find the home note of any loop in chromatic mode by ear inside a minute; self-score on lock.

## Quiz 2: The Voice *(Day 13 · 40 points · ~40 min)*

### Part A: Writing & recording (10 pts, 1 each)
1. What are the three writing constraints for a hook?
2. Why does plain beat clever in this genre's lyrics?
3. Why does a small melodic range survive hard-tuning better than leaps?
4. What can you do if you cannot find a melody at all?
5. Walk through the recording setup: input, level-setting, monitoring, room.
6. Why must the mic not hear the beat?
7. What is a double, what must it match, and what can it fudge?
8. Why perform bigger than feels reasonable?
9. Why keep failed takes?
10. Why back up after every vocal session specifically?

### Part B: Auto Pitch (10 pts, 1 each)
1. What does pitch correction do, and what turns it into the hard-tune sound?
2. What does KEY control, and what should you set it to?
3. When would you use CHROMA, and what is its risk?
4. What does AT PITCH control?
5. What does PITCH do, and what is its range?
6. What does FORMANT change, independent of what?
7. What does ROBOT do, and what is its sensible dose?
8. What does BALANCE control?
9. What is the dry-safety vs printed-input-FX trade-off, and the course's default sequence?
10. If the tuner outputs sour notes, what is the most likely cause and the fix?

### Part C: Pitch-up, stacks & chops (10 pts, 1 each)
1. Name the three roads to a pitched-up voice and one situation for each.
2. What is nightcore, and what did hyperpop inherit from it?
3. List a full stack's layers and their level relationship to the lead.
4. What does the Harmony effect do, and where should it sit relative to real takes?
5. Why print the stack, and why keep the layer pads?
6. Name the four vocal-chop moves in escalating order.
7. When do you TR-REC a stutter and when do you perform it with the DJFX Looper?
8. How do you build a pitch-ramp stutter?
9. How does one sung note become a releasable choir texture?
10. What is the chops-vs-hook failure mode and the rule against it?

### Part D: Candy & the drop (5 pts, 1 each)
1. What job does ear candy do, in one sentence?
2. Give one self-made recipe each for a riser and an impact.
3. What do Stopper, Downer, and Back Spin each do?
4. Name the four pieces of drop anatomy.
5. Why is the gap "the secret," and what is the first fix for a weak drop?

### Part E: At the machine (5 pts) *(machine allowed)*
1. (3) Play your build → gap → drop passage; self-score on withholding, the gap, and the landing.
2. (2) Demonstrate your vocal stack, then mute all layers but the lead; self-score on whether the stack sounds like one big voice.

## Final Exam *(Day 21 · 60 points · ~60 min)*

### Part B: The palette (12 pts)
1. (2) Describe the hyperpop kit's four pieces and each one's treatment.
2. (2) Walk through designing a distorted 808 from a single note, all five steps.
3. (2) How do you find the key by ear, and name everything downstream that depends on it.
4. (2) How do you build a supersaw-style instrument and a chord stab on a sampler?

6. (2) What is the assembly order for a full-energy loop, and why drums first?

### Part C: The voice (12 pts)

### Part D: Structure & dynamics (13 pts, 1 each)

7. Why do transitions have a size hierarchy, and which seam gets the biggest?

11. When a song plateaus, what is the fix and why?
12. Name the three maximal-without-mud rules.

### Part E: The mix & the finish (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Why mix the chorus first?
2. What are the four order-of-operations stages?
3. Where may distortion live, and where may it never?
4. When the master clips, what is the only legitimate fix?
5. Why high-pass layers that do not need lows?
6. What is the playlist test, and why the hour away?
7. Why reference every few minutes while mixing?
8. What three playback checks must the final bounce survive?

### Part F: At the machine / applied (5 pts) *(machine allowed; this is graded on your actual song)*
1. (3) Play the final song start to finish; self-score on whether the form reads blind, the last chorus towers, and it ends on purpose.
2. (2) Run the playlist test one final time (your song between your two references, cold); self-score on the verdict honestly.
