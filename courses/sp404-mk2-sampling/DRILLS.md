# Practice Menu (Drills): MUS-45H

The lessons prescribe a default daily drill, but no single drill fits everyone every day. This is the full menu: **pick one drill per active skill and rotate every few days** so nothing goes stale. Two things need *daily, lifelong* reps: a steady **groove** (C) and your **ear** (F); keep those going forever.

**Building your daily ~30 minutes:** 5 min power up + a quick sample or pad warm-up (A) · 5 min the week's focus skill (B/C/D/E) · 5 min ear/listening (F) · 10–15 min working on an actual beat (G) · always listening back honestly.

> **The one rule above all drills:** *finish small things; do not polish forever.* A drill done to completion teaches more than a fragment fussed over for an hour. If a drill stalls, the answer is usually **commit (resample) and move on**, not keep tweaking. Deciding is the skill.

---

## A. The machine: getting around (start Day 1)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **A1: Eyes-closed pad-find** | Pick four pads; play them in a pattern without looking | the foundation of finger drumming |
| **A2: Level-set sprint** | Arm REC, set a healthy level on whatever is playing, disarm, repeat | making good levels a reflex |
| **A3: Weekly backup** | Back up to an SD card or the computer weekly; confirm the backup exists | never losing work (the most important habit) |
| **A4: Signal-flow recital** | Trace input → pad → bus → output → resample from memory | reasoning about the machine instead of guessing |

## B. Sampling and prep (start Day 3)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **B1: Five clean loops** | Make five usable loops from five sources: record clean, trim tight, loop seamless, pitch | the whole sample-prep pipeline |
| **B2: Loop-the-seam** | Set a rhythmic sample looping; nudge the end point until the seam vanishes | hearing a clean loop |
| **B3: Click hunt** | Find and fix a clicking sample (move the point or add attack/release) | clean edges |
| **B4: Skip-back catch** | Play a source; grab a great moment after it passes using skip-back | never missing a sound |

## C. Groove and drums (start Day 10; never stop)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **C1: Backbeat hold** | Finger-drum kick on 1 and 3, snare on 2 and 4, hats over a metronome; hold steady | the core of all beatmaking |
| **C2: One beat, one minute** | TR-REC a basic working beat fast | speed at the foundation, so time is left for the art |
| **C3: Dead-to-alive** | Take a flat beat and bring it to life with swing, velocity, and ghost notes only | hearing and fixing a stiff beat |
| **C4: Velocity pass** | Re-do a beat varying every hit's strength deliberately | the biggest single upgrade to drums |

## D. Chopping (start Day 8)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **D1: Chop-and-replay** | Chop a sample, then play the pieces back in the original order in time | truly owning your chops |
| **D2: New-phrase-a-day** | From any chops, build one new in-time phrase you like; play it cleanly three times | recomposing (the heart of sampling) |
| **D3: Auto-then-fix** | Auto-chop a break, then nudge every mark to land cleanly on the sound | turning a rough grid into musical chops |
| **D4: Chop-to-melody** | Copy one chop across pads at different pitches; play a melodic line | melodic chopping |

## E. Sequencing and resampling (start Day 11)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **E1: TR-then-real** | TR-REC a beat, then add a real-time part; compare the feel of each | choosing the right tool for the part |
| **E2: Commit-and-continue** | Make one decision about an in-progress sound, resample it, build on top | curing endless-tweak paralysis |
| **E3: Loop to beat in fifteen** | Turn one prepared loop into a chopped, drummed, sequenced, resampled beat, fast | the whole beatmaking pipeline end to end |
| **E4: Layer-and-print** | Stack two drums into one, resample to a single pad | fat drums and freed-up voices |

## F. The ear and listening (start Day 1; every day, no exceptions)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **F1: Sampler's-ear listening** | Listen to any song and find the two seconds you would steal and where you would chop it | the skill the whole craft rests on |
| **F2: Lock-it-in** | Play two pitched samples together; adjust one until it sounds right | the musical ear; key matching |
| **F3: Find-the-home-note** | With a loop playing, find the bass note that locks with it, fast | building harmony and bass by ear |
| **F4: Name-the-drums** | In any beat, pick out the kick, snare, and hats by ear | hearing how beats are built |
| **F5: Can-I-hear-it** | Play a beat; name every element and whether it is clearly audible | 80% of mixing |

## G. Effects, arrangement, and finishing (start Day 15)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **G1: One-effect-better** | Improve a sound with exactly one effect, then stop | restraint (the cure for muddy beats) |
| **G2: Near-and-far** | Place one copy of a sound close (dry) and one far (reverb, filtered) | depth as a dimension you control |
| **G3: Subtract-for-impact** | Create a moment of impact using only drop-out and return | the arrangement insight that makes tracks |
| **G4: Finish-something** | Take any sketch all the way to a bounced, listenable file | the single rarest and most valuable skill |

## Rules that make any drill work

1. **Finish, do not polish.** A completed flawed thing teaches more than a perfect fragment. Get to the end.
2. **Commit your moves.** When something sounds good, resample it and keep going. Endless undo is not production.
3. **Back up weekly.** The drill you never have to redo is the one whose project you did not lose.
4. **Listen back on another speaker.** Your phone and your memory disagree about your beat; the phone is right.
5. **Listen with a sampler's ear all day.** It is free, it never stops paying off, and it costs no machine time.
6. **Keep one number.** Log your honest count of finished beats, or clean loops made, or your fastest "loop to beat" time. The line going up is the motivation.
7. **When a drill gets easy, retire it** and pull the next one. Easy drills are entertainment, not practice.
8. **Short and daily beats long and rare.** Twenty focused minutes a day, with sleep between, wires this in faster than weekend marathons.
