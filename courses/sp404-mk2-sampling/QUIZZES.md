# Quizzes and Final Exam: MUS-45H

Rules: **closed book, machine allowed only where a question says "at the machine."** Write answers on paper (or out loud), then grade with `ANSWER-KEYS.md`. Passing = 90%. The "at the machine" questions are graded by honest self-assessment against the stated standard; bounce it to a file and listen back if you are unsure.

---

## Quiz 1: The Machine & The Sample *(Day 6 · 40 points · ~40 min)*

### Part A: The machine & signal flow (8 pts, 1 each)
1. In one sentence, what is a sampler?
2. How many banks does a project have, and how many pads per bank?
3. Where does everything you make get stored, and what is the one habit that keeps you from losing it?
4. Name the two audio inputs you can sample through.
5. In the playback signal path, what sits between a pad's sample and the output?
6. What is resampling, in one sentence?
7. The SP saves your work automatically. What is the one habit that still protects it, and what should you never do while the machine is writing?
8. Name the effect and the producer most associated with the SP sound.

### Part B: Playing the pads (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What does "velocity-sensitive" mean?
2. Name the three play modes.
3. Which play mode suits a kick drum?
4. Which play mode suits a background loop you build over?
5. Which play mode suits a horn stab you want to play rhythmically by hand?
6. What does the hold/remain function let you do?
7. When would you want a pad to cut off its own previous sound?
8. Why does varying velocity matter for a beat?

### Part C: Recording (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What is the one principle that matters most when recording a sample?
2. What are the two ways to get the level wrong?
3. Can a sample that was recorded clipping be fixed afterward?
4. What does auto-trigger do for you?
5. What is skip-back sampling?
6. Why record dry rather than through effects when you are starting out?
7. Name one good organizing habit for a filling-up project.
8. Where would you connect a phone playing music to sample it?

### Part D: Trimming & pitch (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What do the start and end points control?
2. What must a loop's start and end points line up with for it to be seamless?
3. What causes a click at the edge of a sample? Name one fix.
4. What is the difference between repitch and time-stretch?
5. When you pitch a sample down, what happens to its length?
6. Describe in one line how to match two samples' keys by ear.
7. Why might you record drums in mono?
8. What does the sped-up sampling trick give you?

### Part E: At the machine (8 pts) *(machine allowed)*
1. (4) Record a sample at a healthy level, trim it tight, and make it loop seamlessly; self-score on level and seam.
2. (4) Take that loop and pitch it to lock with a second sample by ear; self-score on whether it sounds in tune.

## Quiz 2: Chopping & Drums *(Day 13 · 40 points · ~40 min)*

### Part A: Chopping (10 pts, 1 each)
1. In one sentence, what is chopping, and why is it more than replaying a sample?
2. Describe the lazy (by-hand) chop method.
3. Why is the looseness of a hand chop a feature, not a flaw?
4. What are the two automatic chop methods?
5. Which auto method is best for a drum break, and why?
6. Which auto method is best for a steady loop with no clear transients?
7. After auto-chopping, what is the single most important thing to do to the marks?
8. How do you stop chops from clicking at the joins?
9. What does choke (mono re-trigger) do for a row of melodic chops?
10. Why is gate mode usually right for performed chops?

### Part B: Drums (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Name the three core drum roles.
2. Where in the bar does the kick usually land? The snare?
3. What is the "backbeat"?
4. What play mode should drum hits use?
5. What is layering, and what does it do?
6. Name two sources you can get drum sounds from.
7. Why does a flat, equal-velocity beat sound dead?
8. What is the most fundamental groove in popular music, in one line?

### Part C: The sequencer (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What is a pattern, and what does the sequencer free your hands to do?
2. What does TR-REC stand for in spirit, and how does it work?
3. Why is TR-REC good for a beginner?
4. How does real-time recording differ from TR-REC?
5. What does quantize do?
6. What is the danger of quantizing too hard?
7. For a one-bar backbeat in 16 steps, which steps get the kick? The snare?
8. Where are patterns stored, and what can you do with several of them?

### Part D: Groove & resampling (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What does swing/shuffle do to the timing, and where do you feel it most?
2. What is the single biggest fix for a beat that sounds programmed?
3. What are ghost notes?
4. What are the two usual causes of a lifeless beat?
5. What is resampling, and name one reason it is central to the workflow.
6. How does resampling let you "print" an effect?
7. What beginner habit does the "resample mindset" cure?
8. How does resampling help a small machine make a dense beat?

### Part E: At the machine (8 pts) *(machine allowed)*
1. (4) Chop a sample and play the pieces back in a new, in-time order; self-score on clean chops and steady time.
2. (4) TR-REC a backbeat with swing and varied velocity so it grooves; self-score on whether it makes you nod.

## Final Exam *(Day 21 · 60 points · ~60 min)*

### Part F: At the machine / applied (8 pts) *(machine allowed; bounce it)*
1. (3) Play a finished beat of yours start to finish: chopped sample, grooving drums, a low or melodic element; self-score on groove and finish.
2. (2) Give that beat a deliberate sound with one or two effects and resample to print it; self-score on intent and cleanliness.
3. (3) Demonstrate one real arrangement move (a break or a build) and confirm the mix still reads in mono; self-score.
