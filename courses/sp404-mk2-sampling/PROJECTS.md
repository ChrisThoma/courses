# Projects: MUS-45H

Projects are where the buttons turn into *music you can actually play for someone.* Each has a recorded deliverable and a grading rubric. **Bounce everything to a file** (resample to a pad, or record the output, or capture it over USB) and grade yourself by *listening back on a different speaker*, which is far more honest than judging while you play. "It basically works" is a 70, not a 95. The standard everywhere is **clean, in time, and musical**, not clever or complicated.

> **Why bounce and listen elsewhere?** Because your phone speaker, your headphones, and your memory all disagree, and the ones that are not your memory are right. Listening back on another system is the fastest way to hear what is actually wrong, and the files become a priceless record of how far you have come.

---

## Project 1: A Clean, In-Tune Loop *(Day 7 · 10% of grade)*

**Goal:** prove the Week-1 foundation is solid: a sample recorded cleanly, trimmed tight, looping seamlessly, and pitched to a comfortable key. This is the Domain 1–4 test.

### Deliverable
1. **One prepared loop** sitting on a pad: recorded at a healthy level, trimmed with no dead air, looping with no audible seam, set to loop mode, pitched to a key and tempo that feels good.
2. **A short recording (30–60 seconds)** of the loop playing continuously, so its cleanliness and seamlessness are audible.
3. **One sentence in writing:** what you sampled and what you did to prepare it.

### Rubric (10 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 3 | Recorded at a healthy level: strong, no clipping, no noise floor problems |
| 3 | Loops seamlessly: no hiccup, click, or timing stumble when it comes around (listen to ten passes) |
| 2 | Trimmed tight: starts and ends exactly on the sound, no dead air |
| 2 | Pitched to a comfortable, musical key and speed |

**Pass bar:** you could loop it for two minutes under a future beat and never once wince at a seam or a level.

## Project 2: A Chopped Loop + a Sequenced Beat *(Day 14 · 15% of grade)*

**Goal:** demonstrate real beatmaking: a sample chopped and used, drums built and sequenced with feel. This is the Domain 5–8 test, and the center of the whole course.

### Deliverable
A beat (roughly 8–16 bars, looping is fine) bounced to a file:
1. **A sample you chopped** (by hand or auto) and used in the beat, whether replayed in a new order or as a clean loop made from your chops.
2. **Your own drums:** a kit of one-shots (kick, snare, hats at minimum) sequenced into a groove.
3. **A sequenced pattern** built with TR-REC and/or real-time recording, with **audible groove**: some swing, varied velocity, ideally a ghost note or two. Not a flat grid.
4. **At least one resample** used somewhere in building it (a printed chop phrase, a layered drum, or a committed loop).
5. **A spoken or written note:** name your sample source and one thing you did to make the beat groove.

### Rubric (15 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 4 | The beat grooves: steady time, swing/velocity/dynamics make it move, not a stiff grid |
| 3 | Drums are clean and sit well: kick and snare clear, a real backbeat |
| 3 | The sample is chopped and used musically, clean at the edges, in tune and in time |
| 3 | The pattern is sequenced competently (TR-REC and/or real-time) and loops without stumbling |
| 2 | Resampling was used to commit at least one element |

**Pass bar:** a stranger hearing it would nod their head, and would not be able to tell it was made by a beginner.

## Project 3: A Polished Beat *(Day 20 · 15% of grade)*

**Goal:** take one beat from "it works" to "it sounds good": a deliberate sound, a low-end and melodic element, a touch of structure, and a balanced mix. This is the Domain 9–10 test, and the dress rehearsal for the final track.

### Deliverable
A polished beat (16–32 bars) bounced to a file:
1. **A deliberate sound:** the SP's effects used with intent (Vinyl Sim, a filter, delay, or reverb) to give the beat a clear sonic identity, nothing muddy or over-effected.
2. **A low-end or melodic element you played:** a bassline or melody built by ear on pitched pads that locks with the beat.
3. **A touch of arrangement:** at least one moment of change (a break where elements drop out and return, a filter sweep intro, a variation), not just a static loop.
4. **A balanced mix:** every element clearly audible, checked in mono and on at least one other speaker, with headroom at the top.
5. **A one-line written note:** the sonic identity you chose and one mix decision you made.

### Rubric (15 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 4 | Balanced mix: every element audible, nothing buried or harsh, translates off your main speakers |
| 3 | Deliberate sound: effects chosen with intent give it an identity; nothing muddy |
| 3 | A bassline or melodic element, played by ear, that locks in tune and in time |
| 3 | At least one real arrangement moment (drop, build, or variation) |
| 2 | Overall musicality: it sounds *finished*, not just functional |

**Pass bar:** you would post it or play it for a friend without apologizing for it.

## Final Project: The Final Track *(Day 21 · 25% of grade)*

**Goal:** the capstone. A finished piece of music that proves you ended the course an actual beatmaker. This is the graduation requirement and the Domain 1–10 test, all of it, in one track.

### Specification
1. **A finished track, roughly one to one and a half minutes**, made entirely on the SP-404 MK2, bounced to a single audio file.
2. **It must use the whole craft:** at least one sampled and chopped (or prepared) source, your own sequenced drums with groove, a low-end and/or melodic element, resampling somewhere in the build, the effects to give it a sound, and a real arrangement (an intro, a main section, at least one variation or break).
3. **A real arrangement with a shape:** it must go somewhere, not loop unchanged. A listener should feel a beginning, a middle, and an end.
4. **A mix that translates:** balanced, audible in mono, and checked on your phone and one other speaker. Decide and commit to your clean-vs-lo-fi aesthetic on purpose.
5. **A short written reflection (½ page):** what came easiest, what is still hardest, the sonic identity you were going for, and which producer or track you want to learn from next.

### Rubric (25 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 6 | It grooves and it is finished: steady, musical, complete start to end (the core) |
| 5 | A real arrangement with a shape: intro, development, and a change, not a static loop |
| 4 | A mix that translates: every element audible, works in mono and on a phone |
| 4 | A deliberate sound: sampling, chopping, effects, and resampling combine into an intentional identity |
| 3 | Musicality and feel: it sounds like *music*, not an exercise |
| 3 | Craft breadth (drums, low end, resampling all present) + reflection thoughtful and honest |

**Graduation bar:** 23+/25, **and** a listener who knows nothing about samplers would enjoy it start to finish and not guess a beginner made it. If you hit that, you did not just finish a course; you became a beatmaker. Save the file; it is your "before."
