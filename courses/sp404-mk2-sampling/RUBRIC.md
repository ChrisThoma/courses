# Mastery Rubric: MUS-45H

Grade yourself in each domain. **Mastery = Level 4 in every technical domain.** The honest test for almost every domain is the same: *bounce your work to a file and listen back on a different speaker.* A recording does not flatter your timing, your tuning, or your levels the way your memory does. Levels:

- **1 (Novice):** Aware of the concept, can only do it by hunting through menus, with stops and luck.
- **2 (Developing):** Can do it slowly with effort, frequent mistakes, and results that sound rough.
- **3 (Proficient):** Can do it reliably and musically at a working pace; rare errors; results sound clean.
- **4 (Solid):** Automatic and fast; you do it without thinking about the machine, so your attention is on the music.

---

## Domain 1: The machine and signal flow

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Lost in the menus; unsure where sound comes in or goes out |
| 2 | Can navigate the basics with the manual open; saves only when reminded |
| 3 | Knows the layout and signal flow; saves and backs up as a habit |
| 4 | Moves through the machine without thinking; never loses work; reasons about the signal path to solve problems |

**Test:** Trace, from memory, how sound goes from an input to a pad, and from a pad through effects to the output, and explain where resampling fits.

## Domain 2: Playing the pads

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Pokes pads one at a time while looking at them |
| 2 | Plays pads but timing is shaky; unsure of play modes |
| 3 | Plays across banks without looking; chooses gate, one-shot, or loop correctly |
| 4 | Fluent, expressive pad playing with velocity; the pads feel like an instrument |

**Test:** With a loop running, finger-play a steady part along with it without looking at the pads.

## Domain 3: Recording a clean sample

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Records at random levels; samples are too quiet or clipping |
| 2 | Gets sound in but forgets to set the level; inconsistent results |
| 3 | Sets a healthy level every time; uses auto-trigger; samples are clean and strong |
| 4 | Recording cleanly is reflex; uses skip-back and input FX deliberately; never wastes a take |

**Test:** Record three samples from a source, each at a healthy level with a clean front edge, in under a few minutes.

## Domain 4: Trimming, looping, and pitch

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Samples have dead air, clicks, and hiccupy loops |
| 2 | Can trim roughly but loops are not seamless and edits click |
| 3 | Trims tight, makes seamless loops, kills clicks, pitches to a comfortable key |
| 4 | Produces a clean, in-tune, seamless loop fast and by ear, every time |

**Test:** Turn a raw recording into a tight, click-free, seamlessly looping, in-tune sample.

## Domain 5: Chopping

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot reliably slice a sample into usable pieces |
| 2 | Can auto-chop but the slices are sloppy and click |
| 3 | Chops by hand and auto, fixes the marks, assigns clean playable pieces to pads |
| 4 | Chops fluently and recomposes the pieces into new phrases as a matter of course |

**Test:** Chop a sample and play the pieces back in a new, in-time order that the original never contained.

## Domain 6: Drums and groove

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot hold a steady beat; drums are random or stiff |
| 2 | Can play a basic backbeat slowly with concentration |
| 3 | Builds a kit and plays/sequences a steady backbeat with some dynamics |
| 4 | Drums groove and breathe: swing, velocity, and ghost notes used by ear; the beat makes you nod |

**Test:** Make a beat that a stranger would tap their foot to, with audibly varied dynamics and feel, not a flat grid.

## Domain 7: The sequencer

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot get a pattern recorded |
| 2 | Can TR-REC a simple pattern with effort |
| 3 | Programs patterns with TR-REC and real-time, uses quantize and swing appropriately |
| 4 | Builds patterns fast, chooses TR-REC vs real-time for the job, and chains patterns into structure |

**Test:** Program a grooving one-bar beat with TR-REC, then add a real-time part with the right amount of quantize.

## Domain 8: Resampling and workflow

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Does not understand or use resampling |
| 2 | Can resample when walked through it |
| 3 | Resamples to commit edits, print effects, and free voices as part of building |
| 4 | Resampling is the spine of the workflow; commits decisions and keeps building without stalling |

**Test:** Build something live (a chop phrase or layered drum), resample it to one pad, and continue building on top.

## Domain 9: Effects and sound

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Effects are random or absent; or everything is over-effected mud |
| 2 | Can turn on an effect but not shape it to the track |
| 3 | Uses Vinyl Sim, filters, delay, and reverb tastefully and with intent |
| 4 | A deliberate sonic identity; effects and resampling build a sound that is a choice, not an accident |

**Test:** Give a beat a clear, intentional sound using a small number of well-chosen effects, with nothing muddy.

## Domain 10: Arrangement and mix (the finished track)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Only ever makes a looping eight bars; mix is unbalanced |
| 2 | Can chain a couple of patterns; some elements are buried or too loud |
| 3 | Builds a short structure (intro, main, variation, break); every element is audible |
| 4 | The track goes somewhere and the mix translates to any speaker, in mono and on a phone |

**Test:** Finish a roughly one-minute track with a clear arrangement and a mix that sounds good on phone, headphones, and one other speaker.

Groove and sound are the long game; the workflow and the daily habit are what this course locks in.
