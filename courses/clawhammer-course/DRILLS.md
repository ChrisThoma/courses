# Practice Menu (Drills): MUS-17

The lessons prescribe a default daily drill, but no single drill fits everyone every day. This is the full menu: **pick one drill per active skill and rotate every few days** so nothing goes stale. Two things need *daily, lifelong* reps: a steady **bum-ditty** (A) and your **ear** (F); keep those going forever.

**Building your daily ~30 minutes:** 5 min tune + warm-up (A) · 5 min the week's focus skill (B/C/D) · 5 min ear/listening (F) · 10–15 min working a tune (E) · always with a metronome for anything rhythmic, always slow enough to be clean.

> **The one rule above all drills:** *slow and even beats fast and sloppy.* If a drill gets ragged, the answer is almost always **slow down**, never speed up. Speed is the reward for accuracy, never a substitute.

---

## A. The groove engine: bum-ditty & hands (start Day 1, never stop)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **A1: Metronome stare-down** | Bum-ditty on G at a tempo where it's *perfectly* even; hold it; only nudge up 2–3 bpm after a flawless minute | the core skill of the whole style |
| **A2: Clean-strike count** | Strike one called string cleanly; any buzz/extra-string = reset; count clean strikes in a row | strike accuracy & tone |
| **A3: Bum-ty hold** | At ~60 bpm, even strike–thumb alternation; strike on the click, thumb on the "and" | thumb timing; un-crowding the di-ty |
| **A4: Even-ness audit** | Record 30 s of bum-ditty; listen back; is every bum equally spaced? | honest self-assessment |
| **A5: Loose-hand check** | Bum-ditty while consciously relaxing wrist, forearm, jaw, shoulders | tension (the enemy of tone & speed) |

## B. Ornaments (start Day 8)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **B1: Ring-it-out** | One hammer-on + one pull-off, alternating; each must *ring clearly* or it's a reset | clean ornaments |
| **B2: The 2→4 slide** | 3rd string, strike 2nd fret, slide to 4th, ringing all the way; 20 reps | the signature old-time swoop |
| **B3: Ornament roulette** | In a slow bum-ditty, randomly pick hammer/pull/slide for each measure's bum; groove must not flinch | ornaments as choices on a steady frame |
| **B4: Weak-finger drill** | Find the finger/string where your ornaments are weakest; drill only there | targeted repair |

## C. Drop-thumb (start Day 10)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **C1: Thumb-target** | Call an inside string (2nd/3rd); drop the thumb to sound it cleanly; reset | thumb accuracy off the drone |
| **C2: Double-thumb roll** | Strike–brush–thumb(5th)–strike–brush–thumb(2nd), slow loop | the drop-thumb groove |
| **C3: Drop-in-a-tune** | Take one phrase of a known tune and replace one drone "ty" with a drop-thumb melody note | melodic drop-thumb |

## D. Chords & changes (start Day 5)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **D1: Chord-flip** | Call a random chord (G/C/D7); form it with all fingers landing together; strum to check clean | fast chord forming |
| **D2: Change in the groove** | Bum-ditty going; change G↔C↔D7 on cue without stopping the right hand | changes that don't break time |
| **D3: Clean-ring check** | Form each chord; strike each chorded string individually; fix any buzz with placement, not force | clean fretting |

## E. Tunes & repertoire (start Day 6)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **E1: Phrase-looping** | Take one 2-bar phrase; loop it slowly until clean; then move the loop forward | how tunes actually get learned (chunking) |
| **E2: Click-disagreement hunt** | Play a known tune with the metronome; find every spot you and the click part ways; loop it slowly | killing rush/drag |
| **E3: Whole-tune pass** | Play a tune AABB start to finish without stopping, however slow | getting *through* a tune (a distinct skill) |
| **E4: Vary-the-repeat** | Play AABB; make the second A and B different from the first | escaping robotic repetition |
| **E5: Tone lab** | Same phrase struck near bridge / middle / over the neck; pick your favorite | deliberate tone |
| **E6: Kickoff reps** | Give a steady two-bum-ditty "potato" and launch a tune in tempo | confident starts |

## F. The ear & listening (start Day 1; every day, no exceptions)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **F1: Required listening** | Play a recording of the tune you're learning; hum along until it's in your head | the foundation of everything |
| **F2: Three-note ear-find** | Hum any short fragment (a jingle, doorbell); find its first 3 notes by ear, no tab | the most valuable long-term skill |
| **F3: First-note hunt** | Hum a known tune; find just its first note on the banjo by matching your hum | starting any by-ear tune |
| **F4: Higher-or-lower** | For a melody in your head, name whether each next note is higher/lower before finding it | the core by-ear question |
| **F5: Lock-in** | Play backup along to a recording; stay perfectly *with* it; re-lock when you drift | social timing (the real goal) |
| **F6: Steal-a-lick** | On a recording you love, find one small thing the player does and work it into your version | building your voice |

## G. Tunings (start Day 15)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **G1: Retune-and-verify** | gDGBD → Sawmill (2nd string B→C); confirm by ear *before* checking the tuner; return to gDGBD | fluent, ear-aware retuning |
| **G2: Sawmill noodle** | In Sawmill, improvise a slow bum-ditty wandering the strings; listen for "right" notes | hearing the modal world |

## Rules that make any drill work

1. **Slow enough to be perfect.** A drill done sloppily fast trains sloppiness. The tempo where you're flawless *is* the right tempo today.
2. **Always with a metronome** for anything rhythmic. It's a mirror, not a tyrant.
3. **Record yourself weekly.** It's uncomfortable and it's the fastest feedback you'll ever get.
4. **Loose hands always.** If your forearm aches, you're squeezing; stop, shake it out, reset.
5. **Listen every day.** You can't play what you haven't heard; the ear leads the hands.
6. **Keep one number.** Log your honest top *clean* tempo, your clean-strike streak, your by-ear tune count. The line going up is the motivation.
7. **When a drill gets easy, retire it** and pull the next one. Easy drills are entertainment, not practice.
8. **Short and daily beats long and rare.** Twenty focused minutes every day, with sleep between, wires this in faster than weekend marathons.
