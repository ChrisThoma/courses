# Quizzes and Final Exam: MUS-17

Rules: **closed book, banjo allowed only where a question says "play."** Write answers on paper (or out loud), then grade with `ANSWER-KEYS.md`. Passing = 90%. The "play" questions are graded by honest self-assessment against the stated standard; record yourself if unsure.

---

## Quiz 1: The Frailing Hand *(Day 6 · 40 points · ~40 min)*

### Part A: The banjo & tuning (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Is the "1st string" the one nearest the floor or the ceiling when you hold the banjo?
2. What is special about the 5th string (two things: its length and its job)?
3. Write the gDGBD tuning from the 5th string to the 1st.
4. What chord do the open strings sound?
5. What instrument is the white "head" of the banjo, functionally?
6. Why must you re-tune every time you play?
7. The banjo descends from West African lutes like which named instrument?
8. Name one thing the akonting shares with clawhammer banjo.

### Part B: The strike & the thumb (8 pts, 1 each)
1. In clawhammer, do you pluck up or strike down?
2. Which part of the finger contacts the string?
3. Which two fingers can be the "striking finger"?
4. Which string does the thumb play in the basic pattern?
5. Where over the head do you strike for a balanced tone?
6. Name one common tone-killer.
7. Should the strike come from a flicking finger or a loose wrist/forearm?
8. In "bum-ty," which event is the strike and which is the thumb?

### Part C: The bum-ditty (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Name the three parts of the bum-ditty in order.
2. Which striking-hand motion makes each part (strike / brush / thumb → I / B / T)?
3. Is the bum-ditty three separate motions or one continuous downward roll?
4. Which part lands *on* the beat?
5. What is the near-universal beginner timing mistake?
6. What's the fix for that mistake?
7. How many bum-ditties make up a typical old-time measure?
8. What single tool best exposes whether your bum-ditty is even?

### Part D: Chords (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Which chord requires no fretting at all?
2. For C, which string/fret does your first finger usually press? (name any one note of the C shape)
3. Where do you press the string relative to the fret wire?
4. What's the rule about how *hard* to press?
5. Where should the left thumb sit on the neck?
6. When a chord change is too slow, what must the *right* hand keep doing?
7. What three chords does Boil Them Cabbage Down use?
8. What does "listen first" mean as a rule for learning any tune?

### Part E: Play (8 pts) *(banjo allowed)*
1. (4) Play 8 bars of an *even* bum-ditty on G with a metronome; self-score on evenness and steady time.
2. (4) Play G → C → G → D7 → G with a steady bum-ditty, changing without stopping the groove; self-score on clean chords and unbroken time.

## Quiz 2: Melody & Drive *(Day 13 · 40 points · ~40 min)*

### Part A: Ornaments (10 pts, 1 each)
1. In a hammer-on, what makes the second note sound (without a second strike)?
2. In a pull-off, which way do you move the finger?
3. Why not lift the finger straight up on a pull-off?
4. How many right-hand strikes does an ornamented two-note pair take?
5. What must you maintain throughout a slide for it to ring?
6. Can you start a slide from an open (unfretted) string? Why/why not?
7. On which string is the famous 2→4 slide usually played?
8. Which tune is that 2→4 slide the signature of?
9. Where in the bum-ditty do ornaments usually happen?
10. Feel-wise, how does a slide differ from a hammer-on between the same two notes?

### Part B: Drop-thumb (6 pts, 1 each)
1. What does the thumb do differently in drop-thumb?
2. Which strings can the thumb "drop" to?
3. Which part of the bum-ditty (bum/di/ty) does drop-thumb change?
4. Why is drop-thumb hard for a beginner specifically?
5. Roughly how long should you expect drop-thumb to take to feel fluid?
6. What does drop-thumb let you do that plain frailing can't?

### Part C: Tunes & the learning method (8 pts, 1 each)
1. In frailing a fiddle tune, which part of the bum-ditty carries the melody note?
2. What does "AABB" mean for playing a tune?
3. List the five steps of the tune-learning method.
4. What must you be able to do before putting hands on the banjo for a new tune?
5. What form do Cripple Creek and Old Joe Clark share?
6. In plain terms (no theory), what gives Old Joe Clark its twangy sound?
7. What key is Old Joe Clark traditionally in?
8. Why does your *second* tune go faster to learn than your first?

### Part D: Drive & timing (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What is "drive"? Is it the same as speed?
2. What is a metronome *really* for, beyond setting tempo?
3. Where do beginners most commonly rush?
4. Where do they most commonly drag?
5. What is a "potato" kickoff?
6. Why is practicing at your ragged top speed counterproductive?
7. What kind of music is old-time, and what is its groove's job?
8. Which comes first in your development: even time or drive?

### Part E: Play (8 pts) *(banjo allowed)*
1. (4) Inside a steady bum-ditty, land a clean hammer-on, a pull-off, and a 2→4 slide, each in time; self-score.
2. (4) Play the A part of Cripple Creek (or Old Joe Clark), twice through, groove intact; self-score on melody recognizability and steady time.

## Final Exam *(Day 21 · 60 points · ~60 min)*

### Part A: Speed fundamentals (10 pts, 1 each; answer each in ≤15 s)
1. Strike up or down? 2. Which finger plays the 5th string? 3. The three parts of the bum-ditty? 4. gDGBD from the 5th string? 5. The chord of all-open strings? 6. One string change to get Sawmill from gDGBD? 7. Which tune's signature is the 2→4 slide? 8. AABB means what? 9. Even time or drive: which first? 10. The banjo's closest African relative?

### Part B: Technique (12 pts)
1. (2) Describe the bum-ditty motion in one or two sentences, naming each part and which hand-motion makes it.
2. (2) Explain hammer-on vs. pull-off: what's different and how many strikes each takes.
3. (2) What is a slide and what must you do for it to keep ringing?
4. (2) What is drop-thumb, and what does it let you play that plain frailing can't?
5. (2) What's the near-universal beginner timing mistake, and the fix?
6. (2) Name two tone-killers in the striking hand and how to avoid them.

### Part C: Tunings & repertoire (10 pts, 1 each)
1. What single string change turns gDGBD into Sawmill?
2. Name two reasons old-time players retune instead of just fretting.
3. What tuning is Shady Grove usually played in?
4. How do you get from gDGBD to Double C?
5. What should you always do with the tuning when you finish a session?
6. Name the four core tunes of this course.
7. Which of the four is modal/in an alternate tuning?
8. What kind of sound (in plain terms) does a modal tune have vs. a major one?
9. Name two more modal/minor tunes you could learn next.
10. What does it mean that old-time is an "aural tradition"?

### Part D: The ear & learning tunes (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What's the non-negotiable first step before finding a tune by ear?
2. When hunting the next note by ear, what's the key question you ask?
3. Why work in tiny phrases rather than the whole tune?
4. Once you have the bare melody, how do you make it a clawhammer arrangement?
5. Why is being slow/frustrated on your first by-ear tune expected?
6. Why does this course teach tunes by ear + linked tab rather than one fixed transcription?
7. What is "backup," and what's the banjo's job in it?
8. What's the strategy for learning to sing and play at the same time?

### Part E: Drive, voice & history (12 pts, 1 each)
1. What is drive, and where does it come from (name two contributors)?
2. Where do beginners rush, and where do they drag?
3. Name three ways to vary a tune.
4. How does *where* you strike change your tone?
5. Where does a personal "voice" come from, and can you force it?
6. What is the Round Peak style known for?
7. Name two Round Peak master players.
8. Trace, in one sentence, the banjo's path from West Africa to Appalachian old-time.
9. What was Briggs' Banjo Instructor (1855), and why does it matter?
10. What's the single best thing you can do for your playing after this course?
11. Name one source-recording site for hearing old-time the way it really sounds.
12. In one sentence: what is the actual *goal* of this whole course?

### Part F: Play / Applied (8 pts) *(banjo allowed; record it)*
1. (3) Play one full tune (any of the four), AABB, from memory, in time at a danceable tempo; self-score on groove and accuracy.
2. (2) Re-tune to Sawmill and play a recognizable phrase of Shady Grove; self-score on the retune and the tune.
3. (3) Find the first three notes of a *new* simple melody (one not in the course) **by ear, no tab**, and frail them; self-score on whether your ear led your hands.
