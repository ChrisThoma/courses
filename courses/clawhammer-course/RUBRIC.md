# Mastery Rubric: MUS-17

Grade yourself in each domain. **Mastery = Level 4 in every technical domain.** The honest test for almost every domain is the same: *record yourself and listen back against a metronome.* Levels:

- **1 (Novice):** Aware of the concept, can't do it without stopping, looking, and luck.
- **2 (Developing):** Can do it slowly with effort, frequent flubs, and breaks in the rhythm.
- **3 (Proficient):** Can do it reliably and *in time* at a moderate tempo; rare errors.
- **4 (Solid):** Automatic, in time, relaxed, and musical; you can do it while thinking about something else (the melody, the singer, the dancers).

---

## Domain 1: The striking hand (the strike & the thumb)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Plucks up like a guitar; hand is tense; tone is thin or buzzy |
| 2 | Can strike *down* with the nail and catch the 5th string with the thumb, slowly, one at a time |
| 3 | Clean down-strike and a reliable thumb; relaxed claw shape; consistent tone string to string |
| 4 | Effortless, relaxed striking hand; full round tone; never thinks about the mechanics |

**Test:** Strike each of the four melody strings cleanly in turn, then catch the 5th string with the thumb, 20 times, no buzzes, hand visibly loose.

## Domain 2: The bum-ditty (the groove engine)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Knows the words "bum-ditty"; can't yet produce it without stopping |
| 2 | Can play strike–brush–thumb slowly but the rhythm is lumpy and uneven |
| 3 | Even, steady bum-ditty at a moderate tempo with a metronome; doesn't rush the ditty |
| 4 | Rock-steady, relaxed bum-ditty at multiple tempos; can hold it for minutes without drifting |

**Test:** Play 16 bars of bum-ditty on a G chord at 80 bpm with a metronome; the click and your thumb land together every time, start to finish.

## Domain 3: The fretting hand (chords & changes)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Can form one chord slowly; notes buzz or are muted |
| 2 | Forms G, C, and D7 but must stop the groove to change |
| 3 | Changes G↔C↔D7 in time without stopping the bum-ditty; chords ring clean |
| 4 | Chord changes are invisible: no hitch in the groove, no looking at the hand |

**Test:** Play the G–C–G–D7–G changes of Boil Them Cabbage Down in tempo with a steady bum-ditty, twice through, no breaks.

## Domain 4: Ornaments (hammer-on, pull-off, slide)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Knows the names; the ornament note doesn't sound |
| 2 | Can produce each ornament in isolation, slowly, when not also keeping time |
| 3 | Executes all three cleanly *inside* a bum-ditty without breaking rhythm |
| 4 | Ornaments are in time, in tune, and expressive: used by choice for feel, not just where the tab says |

**Test:** Inside a steady frailing groove, land a hammer-on, a pull-off, and a 2→4 slide, each clearly audible and on the beat.

## Domain 5: Drop-thumb

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Thumb only ever plays the 5th string |
| 2 | Can drop the thumb to an inside string slowly, out of time |
| 3 | Drops the thumb to sound a melody note in time within a tune |
| 4 | Single and double drop-thumb flow seamlessly; thumb finds melody notes by reflex |

**Test:** Play a drop-thumb figure (e.g., the Cripple Creek or a double-thumb lick) cleanly in time at a moderate tempo.

## Domain 6: Repertoire

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Can play fragments of one tune with the music in front of you |
| 2 | Can get through one tune slowly with stops |
| 3 | Plays 3–4 tunes from memory, both parts (A and B), with the groove intact |
| 4 | Plays the repertoire confidently from memory at a danceable tempo, with personal ornaments |

**Test:** From memory, play all four core tunes (Boil Them Cabbage Down, Cripple Creek, Old Joe Clark, Shady Grove), AABB, in time.

## Domain 7: Drive & timing (the point of it all)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Tempo wanders; rushes the hard parts, drags the easy parts |
| 2 | Stays roughly in time at one slow tempo with concentration |
| 3 | Holds steady time with a metronome across a whole tune; the groove feels good |
| 4 | Plays with *drive* (a propulsive, danceable feel) and locks in with a recording or another player |

**Test:** Play a full tune with a metronome at a danceable tempo with no rushing/dragging, then play it along with a recording and stay locked in.

## Domain 8: The ear (learning tunes & playing by feel)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Can only play from tab; can't find a note without it |
| 2 | Can find a few melody notes by ear with lots of hunting |
| 3 | Can work out a simple tune's melody by ear with patience, then frail it |
| 4 | Learns new tunes primarily by ear; tab is a backup, not a crutch; hears chord changes coming |

**Test:** Learn the melody of a *new* simple tune by ear (no tab) well enough to frail a recognizable version.

## Domain 9: Tunings & range (optional-mastery domain)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Only knows gDGBD exists |
| 2 | Can re-tune to Double C or Sawmill with a tuner |
| 3 | Plays at least one tune in an alternate tuning and knows why it's used |
| 4 | Comfortable moving between gDGBD, Double C, and Sawmill; picks the tuning a tune wants |

**Test:** Re-tune to Sawmill and play Shady Grove; explain in a sentence why that tuning suits the tune.

## Domain 10: History & context

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Thinks the banjo is "an American/Appalachian invention," full stop |
| 2 | Knows the banjo has African roots |
| 3 | Can sketch the arc: West African lutes → minstrel era → Appalachian old-time → revival, and name a few key players |
| 4 | Understands the tradition well enough to seek out source players and regional styles they love and learn from them |

**Test:** In a paragraph, trace the banjo from West Africa to a modern old-time jam and name three players or recordings you want to learn from.
