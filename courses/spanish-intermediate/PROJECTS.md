# Projects: SPAN-20

Projects are where a tense you can conjugate becomes a story you can tell, an instruction you can give, or an opinion you can defend. Each has a handwritten part and a recorded part, because at this level both halves must work. **Record everything and grade by listening back.** "I basically said it" is a 70, not a 95. The written standard is exact correctness of tense, accents, and agreement; the spoken standard is correct structures produced in flowing, understandable speech.

> **Why both written and recorded?** Writing by hand forces the accuracy that distinguishes "hablo" from "habló" and a correct participle agreement. Recording forces the reality of producing these tenses in real time. Together they are your most honest mirror, and the recordings track how far you have come.

---

## Project 1: Tell a Story *(Day 7 · 10% of grade)*

**Goal:** prove you can narrate the past with both tenses and use object pronouns. This is the Domain 1 to 4 test.

### Deliverable
1. **A handwritten past narrative** of eight to twelve sentences about something that happened to you: set the scene in the imperfect (where you were, what was going on) and narrate the events in the preterite. Include at least one "was ...ing when..." sentence and at least two object pronouns. All accents in place.
2. **A recording (about ninety seconds)** of you telling the story aloud, ideally not read word for word.

### Rubric (10 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 4 | Preterite and imperfect chosen correctly (events vs. scene) and formed correctly |
| 2 | At least one correct "imperfect interrupted by preterite" sentence |
| 2 | Object pronouns used correctly and naturally |
| 1 | Handwriting correct: accents, agreement, punctuation |
| 1 | Spoken delivery understandable and reasonably fluid |

**Pass bar:** a listener follows the story and the tense choices track the events vs. the background correctly.

## Project 2: Give Instructions and Make a Case *(Day 14 · 15% of grade)*

**Goal:** demonstrate commands, the future, the conditional, and comparisons in two practical genres. This is the Domain 5 to 7 test.

### Deliverable
1. **A handwritten set of instructions** (six to eight steps) for a real task, using commands (mix affirmative and negative, choose tú or usted to fit), at least two object or reflexive pronouns attached correctly, and one sentence about what will happen as a result (future).
2. **A handwritten short persuasive case** (five to seven sentences) for something you actually want, using the conditional to be polite, at least two comparisons, and correct por/para at least twice.
3. **A recording** of you delivering both: the instructions, then the case.

### Rubric (15 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 4 | Commands correct in form and placement, register consistent |
| 3 | Future and conditional used correctly |
| 3 | At least two correct comparisons; por/para correct |
| 3 | Handwriting correct across structures: accents, agreement |
| 2 | Spoken delivery clear, appropriately polite, understandable |

**Pass bar:** someone could follow your instructions and your case would sound polite and persuasive, not blunt or garbled.

---

## Project 3: Opinions and Hypotheticals *(Day 20 · 15% of grade)*

**Goal:** use the subjunctive and conditional to express opinions, wishes, doubts, and hypotheticals in connected speech. This is the Domain 8 to 9 test.

### Deliverable
1. **A handwritten opinion piece** of eight to twelve sentences on a topic you care about, using at least: three subjunctives covering different triggers (a wish or request, an emotion or doubt, and an impersonal expression or conjunction), two conditionals, and at least four connectors. Full accents.
2. **A recording** of you presenting the opinion aloud, plus answering one follow-up question of your own from memory, using a filler or circumlocution if you stall rather than switching to English.

### Rubric (15 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 5 | At least three correct subjunctives across different triggers |
| 3 | Conditionals correct and used for opinion/politeness |
| 3 | Connected speech: connectors used, ideas flow |
| 2 | Handwriting correct and fully accented |
| 2 | Spoken: stays in Spanish, recovers from any gap, understandable pace |

**Pass bar:** the opinion reads and sounds like connected intermediate Spanish, and the subjunctives are triggered and formed correctly.

---

## Final Project: The Final Interview *(Day 21 · 25% of grade)*

**Goal:** the capstone. A sustained conversation that ranges across time and mood and proves you can actually use the whole system in real exchange. This is the Domain 1 to 10 test together.

### Specification
1. **A recorded conversation of three to four minutes**, ideally with a partner (or both roles yourself), that touches: something that happened recently (past tenses), your current situation (present), your plans (future or near future), an opinion with a reason (subjunctive and connectors), and at least one hypothetical or polite request (conditional).
2. **At least three places where you handle a gap without switching to English:** a filler, a request to repeat or rephrase, or talking around an unknown word.
3. **The handwritten preparation** (an outline and the key sentences you wanted to land), fully accented and correct, turned in with the recording.
4. **A short written reflection:** which structures feel automatic now, which still take thought, and your concrete plan for continuing to practice with real speakers.

### Rubric (25 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 6 | Range and correctness of tenses and moods (both pasts, future, conditional, subjunctive) |
| 5 | Subjunctive used correctly where triggered |
| 4 | Connected, register-appropriate speech with connectors |
| 4 | Stays in Spanish; recovers from gaps with repair and circumlocution |
| 3 | Handwritten preparation correct: accents, agreement, tense forms |
| 3 | Reflection thoughtful, with a concrete practice plan |

**Graduation bar:** 23+/25, **and** a Spanish speaker would have a genuine, flowing conversation with you across past, present, and future without your needing to drop into English to survive. Save the recording next to your SPAN-10 final. The distance between them is the proof.
