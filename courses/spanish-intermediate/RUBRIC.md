# Mastery Rubric: SPAN-20

Grade yourself in each domain. **Mastery = Level 4 in every core domain.** For spoken domains, record yourself and listen back. For written domains, check every accent, ending, and agreement against the Reference, because in writing they are simply right or wrong. Levels:

- **1. Novice:** Aware of the structure; cannot produce it without stopping and looking it up.
- **2. Developing:** Can produce it slowly, with effort and frequent errors.
- **3. Proficient:** Can produce it reliably and correctly at a slow conversational pace; rare errors.
- **4. Solid:** Automatic and correct at a natural pace; you produce it while thinking about meaning, not mechanics.

---

## Domain 1: The preterite

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot conjugate without the table |
| 2 | Conjugates regular preterites slowly; irregulars unreliable |
| 3 | Conjugates regular and common irregular preterites correctly |
| 4 | Produces any preterite, including irregulars, instantly in speech |

**Test:** Narrate five completed past events aloud, including two with irregular verbs, all forms correct.

## Domain 2: The imperfect

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Confuses it with the preterite; cannot form it |
| 2 | Forms it slowly; forgets the three irregulars |
| 3 | Forms it correctly and uses it for habit, ongoing, and description |
| 4 | Produces it automatically for all its uses |

**Test:** Describe a past routine and a past scene aloud, all imperfect forms correct.

## Domain 3: Preterite vs. imperfect (the central skill)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Chooses essentially at random |
| 2 | Chooses correctly in obvious cases only |
| 3 | Chooses correctly in most narration, including the "was ...ing when..." structure |
| 4 | Choice is intuitive, including the meaning-shift verbs (sabía/supe) |

**Test:** Tell a two-minute story aloud; a listener confirms the tense choices track the events vs. the scene correctly.

## Domain 4: Object and reflexive pronouns

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Repeats nouns; cannot use pronouns |
| 2 | Uses single pronouns slowly; placement errors |
| 3 | Uses direct, indirect, and reflexive pronouns with correct placement |
| 4 | Uses double pronouns (se lo) fluently and naturally |

**Test:** Answer ten questions replacing nouns with pronouns, including two double-pronoun answers, all correct.

## Domain 5: Commands

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot form commands |
| 2 | Forms affirmative tú only |
| 3 | Forms affirmative and negative commands for tú, usted, ustedes |
| 4 | Uses commands with attached/preceding pronouns correctly and softens when appropriate |

**Test:** Give seven instructions (mixed affirmative/negative, tú and usted), with two pronoun attachments, all correct.

## Domain 6: Future and conditional

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot form either |
| 2 | Forms regular ones; forgets irregular stems |
| 3 | Forms both, including irregular stems, and uses the conditional to soften |
| 4 | Produces both automatically, including the future of probability |

**Test:** Make five predictions (future) and five polite requests or hypotheticals (conditional), all correct.

## Domain 7: Perfect, progressive, comparisons, por/para

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot form the perfect or progressive; por/para random |
| 2 | Forms the perfect with regular participles; overuses the progressive; por/para guessed |
| 3 | Uses the perfect with irregular participles, the progressive only for now, comparisons, and por/para correctly in clear cases |
| 4 | All of these are automatic, including the trickier por/para choices |

**Test:** Produce a present-perfect sentence with an irregular participle, a correct progressive, two comparisons, and four correct por/para sentences.

## Domain 8: The present subjunctive

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot form it |
| 2 | Forms regular subjunctives but rarely knows when to use them |
| 3 | Forms regular and irregular subjunctives and uses them after the main triggers |
| 4 | Reaches for the subjunctive correctly and automatically across wish, emotion, doubt, impersonal, and conjunction triggers |

**Test:** Produce six subjunctive sentences, one for each major trigger type, all correctly formed and triggered.

## Domain 9: Connected, register-aware speech

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Speaks in isolated short sentences; one register only |
| 2 | Links some sentences; switches to English when stuck |
| 3 | Links ideas with connectors, matches register, and uses repair strategies to stay in Spanish |
| 4 | Sustains natural, flowing, register-appropriate conversation and recovers from any gap without English |

**Test:** Sustain a three-minute conversation with connectors, correct register, and at least two recoveries by circumlocution, never switching to English.

## Domain 10: Handwriting and accuracy

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Omits accents; conjugations error-filled |
| 2 | Copies correctly but own writing drops accents and mixes tenses |
| 3 | Own handwritten sentences are correct across tenses, with accents and agreement |
| 4 | Writes a correct connected composition by hand across multiple tenses and moods, few or no errors |

**Test:** Hand-write a ten-sentence narrative-and-opinion piece across at least four tenses/moods with correct spelling, accents, and agreement.
