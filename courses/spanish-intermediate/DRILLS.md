# Practice Menu (Drills): SPAN-20

The lessons prescribe a default daily drill, but no single drill fits everyone every day. This is the full menu plus the spaced-repetition system. **Pick one drill per active skill and rotate every few days.** Two things need daily reps for the whole course and beyond: **spaced-repetition review (A)** and **speaking out loud (E)**. At this level a third is just as important: **keeping the old tenses warm (B)**, because the intermediate danger is not failing to learn a new form but losing an old one.

**Building your daily review (about 15 minutes, on top of the day's lesson):** 5 min spaced-repetition cards (A) · 5 min multi-tense conjugation by hand (B) · 5 min speaking out loud (E). Everything written is by hand; everything spoken is out loud and, weekly, recorded.

> **The one rule above all drills:** *correct and out loud beats fast and silent.* And never write a Spanish verb without its accents, the accent is what separates "hablo" from "habló."

---

## A. Spaced repetition (daily, never stop)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **A1: Verb-and-vocab cards** | One card per vocabulary item and per irregular verb form; review on a schedule | long-term retention |
| **A2: Daily review pass** | Go through due cards; say each answer aloud before flipping; sort knew/missed | the core memory drill |
| **A3: Leitner schedule** | Missed cards return tomorrow; known cards stretch to 3 days, a week, two weeks | efficient spacing |
| **A4: Trigger cards** | One card per subjunctive trigger phrase ("dudo que...", "es importante que...") with a sample | the subjunctive habit |

## B. Multi-tense conjugation by hand (daily)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **B1: Time-travel** | Take one verb and write it in every tense you know: present, preterite, imperfect, perfect, near future, future, conditional, subjunctive | keeping tenses distinct |
| **B2: Irregular sprint** | Write the preterite or subjunctive of five irregular verbs as fast as you can while staying correct; time it | irregular automaticity |
| **B3: Person-and-tense call** | For a called person+tense ("ellos, preterite, hacer") write just that form | the exact form on demand |
| **B4: Participle/gerund drill** | Write the past participle and gerund of ten verbs, including irregulars | perfect and progressive |

## C. Past-tense narration (start Week 1)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **C1: Two-past narration** | Tell a short real event, watching where you use preterite (events) vs. imperfect (scene); say it, then write it | the central choice |
| **C2: Interrupt sentences** | Write five "was ...ing when..." sentences (imperfect + preterite) | the combined structure |
| **C3: Meaning-shift pairs** | Use sabía/supe, conocía/conocí, quería/quise each in a sentence that shows the difference | the tricky verbs |

## D. Pronouns, commands & the subjunctive (start as introduced)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **D1: Pronoun replace** | Answer questions replacing nouns with object pronouns, including double (se lo) | concise speech |
| **D2: Command round** | Give the same instruction as affirmative tú, negative tú, and usted | command flexibility |
| **D3: Trigger-to-subjunctive** | Take a trigger ("espero que...") and finish it with a correct subjunctive about your real life | the subjunctive reflex |
| **D4: Indicative-or-subjunctive** | Decide for mixed sentences whether the trigger requires subjunctive (creo que vs. no creo que) | the choice, not just the form |

## E. Speaking out loud (daily, no exceptions)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **E1: Daily monologue** | Speak for two minutes on a topic, across at least two tenses | real-time production |
| **E2: Connector challenge** | Repeat a monologue and force in four connectors and one filler | flowing speech |
| **E3: Circumlocution game** | Pick words you do not know; talk around each until the meaning is clear | staying in Spanish |
| **E4: Shadowing** | Speak along a beat behind a natural-speed clip, copying rhythm and intonation | accent and pace |
| **E5: Record and grade** | Weekly, record a three-minute conversation or monologue; grade against the rubric | honest feedback |
| **E6: Register switch** | Say the same request three ways: blunt, neutral, and formally softened | register control |

## F. Listening & input (daily)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **F1: Level-appropriate input** | A few minutes daily of Spanish slightly above your level | ear and vocabulary |
| **F2: Tense-spotting** | While listening, note each past tense you hear and guess why that one | the choice in the wild |
| **F3: Dictation** | Write a heard sentence, then check spelling and accents against a transcript | sound-to-letter accuracy |

---

## Rules that make any drill work

1. **Correct first, fast later.** A drill done wrong trains the wrong form.
2. **By hand for anything written, out loud for anything spoken.** No silent reading, no typed conjugations.
3. **Always the accents.** In these tenses the accent carries the meaning; a missing accent is a wrong answer.
4. **Keep the old tenses warm.** Review previous tenses every day, not just the new one.
5. **Record yourself weekly.** The fastest feedback on pace and tense errors.
6. **Keep one number.** Log your card count, your irregular-sprint time, your longest unscripted spoken stretch.
7. **When a drill gets easy, retire it** and pull the next one.
8. **Short and daily beats long and rare.** Many short sittings, with sleep between, beat weekend marathons.
