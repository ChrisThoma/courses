# SPAN-20 Reference: Verb Tables, Pronouns & Vocabulary

This is your reference for every tense and structure in the course. Keep it open while you work. The tables below are the source of truth; when a lesson says "see the Reference," this is where to look.

A reminder on accents: in these tenses the accent is doing real grammatical work. **hablo** means "I speak"; **habló** means "he spoke." A missing or misplaced accent changes the meaning, so in all handwritten work it counts as an error.

---

## 1. The preterite (completed past actions)

### Regular

| | -ar: hablar | -er: comer | -ir: vivir |
|---|---|---|---|
| yo | habl**é** | com**í** | viv**í** |
| tú | habl**aste** | com**iste** | viv**iste** |
| él/ella/usted | habl**ó** | com**ió** | viv**ió** |
| nosotros | habl**amos** | com**imos** | viv**imos** |
| vosotros | habl**asteis** | com**isteis** | viv**isteis** |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | habl**aron** | com**ieron** | viv**ieron** |

Note that -er and -ir share endings in the preterite. The nosotros form of -ar and -ir verbs looks the same as the present (hablamos, vivimos); context tells you which.

### Common irregular preterites

These do not follow the pattern and must be memorized. Note: irregular preterites take **no accents** on the yo and él forms (tuve, tuvo, not tuvé).

| | ser / ir (identical) | hacer | tener | estar | poder | poner | querer | venir | decir | dar | ver |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| yo | fui | hice | tuve | estuve | pude | puse | quise | vine | dije | di | vi |
| tú | fuiste | hiciste | tuviste | estuviste | pudiste | pusiste | quisiste | viniste | dijiste | diste | viste |
| él/ella/ud | fue | hizo | tuvo | estuvo | pudo | puso | quiso | vino | dijo | dio | vio |
| nosotros | fuimos | hicimos | tuvimos | estuvimos | pudimos | pusimos | quisimos | vinimos | dijimos | dimos | vimos |
| ellos/uds | fueron | hicieron | tuvieron | estuvieron | pudieron | pusieron | quisieron | vinieron | dijeron | dieron | vieron |

ser and ir are identical in the preterite (fui, fuiste, fue...); context tells you whether someone "was" or "went." Verbs whose stem ends in j (decir, traer) use **-eron**, not -ieron, in the ellos form: dijeron, trajeron.

## 2. The imperfect (ongoing, habitual, background past)

### Regular

| | -ar: hablar | -er: comer | -ir: vivir |
|---|---|---|---|
| yo | habl**aba** | com**ía** | viv**ía** |
| tú | habl**abas** | com**ías** | viv**ías** |
| él/ella/usted | habl**aba** | com**ía** | viv**ía** |
| nosotros | habl**ábamos** | com**íamos** | viv**íamos** |
| vosotros | habl**abais** | com**íais** | viv**íais** |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | habl**aban** | com**ían** | viv**ían** |

### The only three irregular imperfects

| | ser | ir | ver |
|---|---|---|---|
| yo | era | iba | veía |
| tú | eras | ibas | veías |
| él/ella/usted | era | iba | veía |
| nosotros | éramos | íbamos | veíamos |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | eran | iban | veían |

That is the whole list. Every other verb is regular in the imperfect.

## 3. The future and the conditional

Both are built on the **whole infinitive** (not a chopped stem) for regular verbs, plus a set of endings. The endings are the same for all three verb families.

| | Future endings | Conditional endings |
|---|---|---|
| yo | -é | -ía |
| tú | -ás | -ías |
| él/ella/usted | -á | -ía |
| nosotros | -emos | -íamos |
| vosotros | -éis | -íais |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | -án | -ían |

So hablar gives **hablaré** (I will speak) and **hablaría** (I would speak); comer gives comeré / comería.

**Irregular stems** (used for both future and conditional, with the same endings):

| Verb | Stem | Example (future) |
|---|---|---|
| tener | tendr- | tendré |
| poner | pondr- | pondré |
| salir | saldr- | saldré |
| venir | vendr- | vendré |
| poder | podr- | podré |
| saber | sabr- | sabré |
| haber | habr- | habrá |
| querer | querr- | querré |
| hacer | har- | haré |
| decir | dir- | diré |

## 4. The perfect and progressive tenses

### Present perfect (have done)

Formed with the present of **haber** plus the past participle.

| haber | + participle |
|---|---|
| he | hablado |
| has | comido |
| ha | vivido |
| hemos | |
| habéis | |
| han | |

So: *He hablado* (I have spoken), *Hemos comido* (we have eaten), *¿Has visto...?* (Have you seen...?).

**Past participles:** -ar verbs to **-ado** (hablado), -er and -ir verbs to **-ido** (comido, vivido). Common irregulars: hacer to **hecho**, ver to **visto**, escribir to **escrito**, decir to **dicho**, poner to **puesto**, volver to **vuelto**, abrir to **abierto**, romper to **roto**, morir to **muerto**. In the perfect tense the participle never changes for gender or number; it stays -o.

### Present progressive (am doing)

Formed with **estar** plus the gerund (the -ing form).

- Gerund: -ar verbs to **-ando** (hablando), -er and -ir verbs to **-iendo** (comiendo, viviendo).
- *Estoy hablando* (I am speaking), *Están comiendo* (they are eating).

Use the progressive only for an action genuinely in progress right now. For habitual or general present, Spanish uses the simple present (Trabajo aquí, not Estoy trabajando aquí, for "I work here").

## 5. Object and reflexive pronouns

| Direct (DO) | Indirect (IO) | Reflexive |
|---|---|---|
| me | me | me |
| te | te | te |
| lo / la | le | se |
| nos | nos | nos |
| os | os | os |
| los / las | les | se |

**Placement:** before a conjugated verb (*Lo veo*), or attached to the end of an infinitive or gerund (*Voy a verlo / Estoy viéndolo*), or attached to an affirmative command (*Véalo*). With a negative command they go before (*No lo vea*).

**Double pronouns:** when you use both, the **indirect comes before the direct** (*Me lo da*, he gives it to me). And **le / les become se** before lo/la/los/las (*Se lo doy*, I give it to him, not "le lo").

## 6. Commands (the imperative)

| | Affirmative | Negative |
|---|---|---|
| tú | same as 3rd-person singular present: **habla, come, vive** | no + subjunctive: **no hables, no comas, no vivas** |
| usted | subjunctive: **hable, coma, viva** | **no hable, no coma, no viva** |
| ustedes | subjunctive: **hablen, coman, vivan** | **no hablen, no coman, no vivan** |
| nosotros (let's) | subjunctive: **hablemos, comamos, vivamos** | **no hablemos...** |

**Irregular affirmative tú commands** (memorize these eight): decir to **di**, hacer to **haz**, ir to **ve**, poner to **pon**, salir to **sal**, ser to **sé**, tener to **ten**, venir to **ven**.

Pronouns attach to affirmative commands (*Dímelo*, tell it to me) and go before negative ones (*No me lo digas*).

## 7. The present subjunctive

**Formation (regular):** take the **yo form** of the present indicative, drop the **-o**, and add the "opposite" endings:

| | -ar: hablar | -er: comer | -ir: vivir |
|---|---|---|---|
| yo | habl**e** | com**a** | viv**a** |
| tú | habl**es** | com**as** | viv**as** |
| él/ella/usted | habl**e** | com**a** | viv**a** |
| nosotros | habl**emos** | com**amos** | viv**amos** |
| vosotros | habl**éis** | com**áis** | viv**áis** |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | habl**en** | com**an** | viv**an** |

So -ar verbs take e-endings and -er/-ir verbs take a-endings, the reverse of the present indicative.

Because the subjunctive is built from the yo form, any irregular yo form carries in: tener (tengo) to **tenga**, hacer (hago) to **haga**, decir (digo) to **diga**, salir (salgo) to **salga**, ver (veo) to **vea**, conocer (conozco) to **conozca**. Stem changes carry too: querer to **quiera**, poder to **pueda**.

**Six fully irregular subjunctives** (not built from a yo form): ser to **sea**, estar to **esté**, ir to **vaya**, haber to **haya**, saber to **sepa**, dar to **dé**.

**When to use it (Week 3 details):** the subjunctive appears in a dependent clause after **que** when the main clause expresses a wish or request, an emotion, a doubt or denial, an impersonal opinion, or after certain conjunctions. The memory aid is **WEIRDO**: Wishes, Emotions, Impersonal expressions, Recommendations/Requests, Doubt/Denial, Ojalá and certain conjunctions.

## 8. por vs. para (quick reference; full treatment Day 13)

**para** generally points to a destination, goal, or purpose, and answers "for what / for whom / by when":
- purpose or goal: *Estudio para aprender.* (I study in order to learn.)
- recipient: *Es para mi jefe.* (It is for my boss.)
- destination: *Salgo para Madrid.* (I leave for Madrid.)
- deadline: *Es para el lunes.* (It is due Monday.)

**por** generally points to a cause, a means, an exchange, or movement through, and answers "because of / through / by means of / in exchange for":
- cause or reason: *Gracias por tu ayuda.* (Thanks for your help.)
- duration: *Trabajé por ocho horas.* (I worked for eight hours.)
- exchange: *Pagué diez dólares por el libro.* (I paid ten dollars for the book.)
- means: *Hablamos por teléfono.* (We spoke by phone.)
- through/along: *Caminé por el parque.* (I walked through the park.)

A short slogan: **para** is the destination or goal; **por** is the reason or the route.

## 9. Comparisons and superlatives

- **More than:** más + adjective + que. *Ella es más alta que yo.*
- **Less than:** menos + adjective + que. *Es menos caro que el otro.*
- **As ... as:** tan + adjective + como. *Soy tan rápido como tú.*
- **As much/many ... as:** tanto/a/os/as + noun + como. *Tengo tantos libros como tú.*
- **Superlative (the most):** el/la/los/las + más + adjective. *Es el más grande.*
- **Irregular forms:** mejor (better/best), peor (worse/worst), mayor (older), menor (younger). *Este es mejor que ese.*

## 10. Vocabulary for narration and opinion

Drill these with the spaced-repetition system in the [Practice Menu](DRILLS.md).

### Time and sequence words (essential for narrating)
ayer (yesterday), anteayer (the day before yesterday), anoche (last night), la semana pasada (last week), el año pasado (last year), antes (before), después (after), luego (then), entonces (then/so), primero (first), al principio (at the beginning), al final (in the end), de repente (suddenly), mientras (while), siempre (always), nunca (never), a veces (sometimes), todos los días (every day), una vez (once), cuando (when), ya (already), todavía (still/yet).

### Verbs of opinion, emotion, and influence (subjunctive triggers)
querer que (to want that), esperar que (to hope that), desear que (to wish that), pedir que (to ask that), recomendar que (to recommend that), sugerir que (to suggest that), necesitar que (to need that), alegrarse de que (to be glad that), sentir que (to be sorry that), temer que (to fear that), dudar que (to doubt that), no creer que (to not believe that), es importante que (it is important that), es necesario que (it is necessary that), es posible que (it is possible that), ojalá (I hope / hopefully).

### Connectors and discourse markers
sin embargo (however), aunque (although), por eso (that is why), además (besides), en cambio (on the other hand), por lo tanto (therefore), es decir (that is to say), o sea (in other words), por supuesto (of course), en realidad (actually), a propósito (by the way), bueno (well), pues (well/so), la verdad es que (the truth is that), me parece que (it seems to me that), creo que (I think that), depende de (it depends on).

### Useful professional and everyday verbs
explicar (to explain), preguntar (to ask a question), contestar (to answer), avisar (to let know), encargarse de (to take charge of), encontrar (to find), conseguir (to get/obtain), lograr (to manage to), resolver (to solve), cumplir (to fulfill/meet), revisar (to review/check), enviar (to send), recoger (to pick up), entregar (to hand in/deliver), reunirse (to meet), tratar de (to try to), darse cuenta de (to realize), acordarse de (to remember), olvidarse de (to forget).
