# Quizzes and Final Exam: SPAN-20

Rules: **closed book.** Write your answers by hand, with all accents, then grade against `ANSWER-KEYS.md`. Passing = 90%. The "say it" questions are graded by honest self-assessment against the stated standard. In any written Spanish answer, a missing accent or a wrong ending is a wrong answer, because at this level the accent often carries the tense.

---

## Quiz 1: Talking About the Past *(Day 6 · 40 points · about 40 min)*

### Part A: The preterite (10 pts, 1 each)
1. What does the preterite express?
2. Give the preterite endings for -ar verbs (all six).
3. Which two verb families share preterite endings?
4. Conjugate hablar in the preterite: yo, tú, ella.
5. Conjugate comer in the preterite: yo, nosotros, ellos.
6. Conjugate ser/ir in the preterite: yo, él, ellos.
7. Conjugate tener in the preterite: yo, tú, nosotros.
8. What is the él form of hacer in the preterite?
9. What is unusual about accents on irregular preterites? Give an example.
10. Name three time markers that signal the preterite.

### Part B: The imperfect (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What three kinds of past does the imperfect express?
2. Give the imperfect endings for -ar verbs.
3. Give the imperfect endings for -er/-ir verbs.
4. Name the only three irregular imperfect verbs.
5. Conjugate ser in the imperfect: yo, nosotros.
6. Conjugate ir in the imperfect: yo, nosotros.
7. Which tense describes weather, age, and time in the past?
8. Name two time markers that signal the imperfect.

### Part C: Choosing the past tense (10 pts, 1 each)
For each, write the correct tense (preterite or imperfect) and the correct form.
1. Ayer yo (hablar) con el jefe.
2. Antes nosotros (vivir) en otra ciudad.
3. (ser) las tres cuando ella (llegar). [two answers]
4. Mientras yo (trabajar), sonó el teléfono.
5. De niño, él (jugar) al fútbol todos los días.
6. La semana pasada ellos (terminar) el proyecto.
7. (hacer) frío y (llover) esa noche. [two answers]
8. Give the difference in meaning between "sabía" and "supe."

### Part D: Object pronouns (12 pts, 1 each except as noted)
1. Give the six direct object pronouns.
2. Give the six indirect object pronouns; which differ from the direct set?
3. Rewrite with a direct object pronoun: Tengo el informe.
4. Rewrite with a direct object pronoun: Veo a María.
5. Rewrite with a direct object pronoun: Compré los libros.
6. Add the indirect pronoun: ___ doy el libro a ella.
7. Where does a pronoun go with a conjugated verb? With an infinitive?
8. In double pronouns, which comes first, indirect or direct?
9. What do le/les become before lo/la? Give an example.
10. (2) Rewrite with double pronouns: Doy el informe al jefe. / Mando las cartas a María.

## Quiz 2: Commands, the Future & More *(Day 13 · 40 points · about 40 min)*

### Part A: Reflexives and commands (10 pts, 1 each)
1. What makes a verb reflexive?
2. Give the reflexive pronouns for all persons.
3. Conjugate levantarse: yo, tú, nosotros.
4. How do you form the affirmative tú command of a regular verb?
5. Give the irregular affirmative tú commands of hacer, ir, tener, poner.
6. How do you form the usted command (the quick trick)?
7. Give the affirmative and negative tú command of hablar.
8. Give the usted command of venir.
9. Where do pronouns go with an affirmative command? A negative one?
10. Rewrite with the pronoun attached: the affirmative tú command of "hacer" + "lo."

### Part B: Future and conditional (10 pts, 1 each)
1. What do you add the future endings to, for regular verbs?
2. Give the future endings for all persons.
3. Conjugate hablar in the future: yo, tú, ellos.
4. Give the irregular future stems of tener, hacer, decir, poder.
5. Besides plans, what special meaning can the future carry? Give an example.
6. What does the conditional express?
7. What endings does the conditional use?
8. Conjugate hablar in the conditional: yo, nosotros.
9. How does the conditional soften a request? Give an example.
10. Translate: "Could you help me?" (conditional)

### Part C: Perfect and progressive (8 pts, 1 each)
1. How is the present perfect formed?
2. Give the present of haber (all six).
3. Give the past participles of hablar, comer, hacer, ver.
4. Can you split haber and the participle, and does the participle change for gender?
5. How is the progressive formed?
6. Give the gerund of hablar, comer, vivir.
7. Translate: "I have finished the report."
8. Why is "estoy trabajando aquí" wrong for "I work here in general"?

### Part D: Comparisons and por/para (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Give the frame for "more than" and for "as ... as."
2. Give the irregular comparatives for good and bad.
3. Form a superlative: "the biggest" (el más...).
4. In one line, what is the difference between por and para?
5. por or para: Gracias ___ tu ayuda.
6. por or para: Este informe es ___ el jefe.
7. por or para: Estudio ___ aprender.
8. por or para: Caminé ___ el parque.

### Part E: Build it (4 pts) *(by hand, fully accented)*
1. (2) Write two instructions for a task using commands.
2. (2) Write a polite request using the conditional and a comparison.

## Final Exam *(Day 21 · 60 points · about 60 min)*

### Part E: The subjunctive (10 pts, 1 each)

### Part F: Say it and write it (10 pts)
1. (3) Speak: narrate a two-minute past story using both past tenses; self-score on the tense choices.
2. (3) Speak: give an opinion with a reason using at least two subjunctives and two connectors; self-score.
3. (4) Write by hand: a six-sentence piece that uses a past tense, the future or conditional, a subjunctive, an object pronoun, and a por or para; self-score on accents and correctness.
