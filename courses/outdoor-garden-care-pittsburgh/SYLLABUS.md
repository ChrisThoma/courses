# HORT E-15 Syllabus: The Home Garden (Soil, Plants, and Weeds in Pittsburgh)

## Course description

A three-week intensive in home horticulture for the Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania region (USDA Hardiness Zone 6b; parts of the urban core 7a). Students learn to read their own site and climate, understand and amend western Pennsylvania's heavy clay soils, select and care for plants suited to Zone 6b, manage pests and disease with an integrated approach, identify and control the region's most stubborn weeds and invasives, and run a year-round maintenance system. By the end, students can assess any home site, diagnose common plant and soil problems, write a season-by-season care plan, and keep a garden that is healthy, resilient, and a pleasure to tend rather than a chore.

## Learning objectives

By the end of this course you will be able to:

1. **Read a site:** determine your hardiness zone, frost dates, and growing-season length; map sun, slope, drainage, and microclimates; and match plants to conditions ("right plant, right place").
2. **Understand and improve soil:** identify soil texture and structure by hand, interpret a soil test (pH and N-P-K), and prescribe amendments, with special command of Pittsburgh's clay and urban-soil challenges, including lead safety.
3. **Care for plants:** select, plant, water, mulch, fertilize, prune, and deadhead correctly, and recognize the signs of over- and under-watering and nutrient deficiency.
4. **Manage pests and disease** using Integrated Pest Management (IPM): identify the region's common threats and choose the least-toxic effective response.
5. **Win against weeds:** identify the "Pittsburgh dozen," understand weed life cycles and the seed bank, and apply cultural, mechanical, and chemical controls appropriately, including a strategy for Japanese knotweed and garlic mustard.
6. **Design and run a healthy garden:** build biodiversity and pollinator support, manage (or replace) a cool-season lawn, and execute a month-by-month Pittsburgh maintenance calendar.

Grade yourself against `RUBRIC.md`. **Mastery threshold: 90%.** If you score below 90% on a quiz, re-study that week's material on the rest day before moving on; the course is cumulative, and a shaky grasp of soil will sink everything you plant in it.

## Course policies (yes, even self-taught courses need them)

- **Observe before you read.** Every day starts with a field walk. Ten minutes of looking at real plants builds judgment that no amount of reading can.
- **Closed-book quizzes.** The point is to find gaps, not to feel good.
- **Do the season-dependent tasks when your window comes.** Marked **🌱** in the lessons. If you're out of season, do the off-season alternative now and put the live task on your calendar; don't skip it.
- **Safety is not optional.** Wear gloves, learn to recognize poison ivy (Day 15), and never use a pesticide or herbicide except as the label directs. The label is the law.
- **Falling behind:** use the rest day. If you're more than a day behind, compress by skipping the optional exercises (marked ★), never by skipping lectures or quizzes.
