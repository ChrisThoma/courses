# Mastery Rubric: HORT E-15

Grade yourself in each domain. **Mastery = Level 4 in every domain.** Levels:

- **1 (Novice):** Aware of the concept, can't apply it without references.
- **2 (Developing):** Can apply it slowly with effort and frequent errors.
- **3 (Proficient):** Can apply it reliably with brief thought; rare errors.
- **4 (Master):** Instant, accurate, and can teach it / apply it to your own garden and to problems you've never seen.

---

## Domain 1: Site & climate literacy

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Has heard of hardiness zones; unsure of own zone |
| 2 | Knows their zone and frost dates; can read sun requirements off a tag |
| 3 | Maps sun/shade and slope/aspect of own site; identifies microclimates; respects the ~May 15 frost rule |
| 4 | Reads any site fluently (zone, frost, sun, slope, drainage, microclimates) and matches plants to it without thinking; "right plant, right place" is automatic |

**Test:** Walk a new yard and, in 10 minutes, describe its zone behavior, sun zones, microclimates, and what would and wouldn't grow there.

## Domain 2: Soil

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Knows "good soil matters"; can't assess it |
| 2 | Identifies clay vs. sand by feel; knows organic matter helps |
| 3 | Determines texture by ribbon/jar test, interprets a soil test (pH, N-P-K), prescribes lime/amendments, distinguishes texture from structure |
| 4 | Diagnoses any soil problem and prescribes the fix; handles urban-soil/lead safety; manages clay's drainage and structure confidently; "feed the soil" is instinct |

**Test:** Given a soil report (pH 5.4, low N, optimum P/K) and a clay site, prescribe the correct action in under a minute, and explain what *not* to add.

## Domain 3: Plant care

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Waters and hopes |
| 2 | Plants and waters with general guidelines; over- or under-does it |
| 3 | Plants correctly (right hole/depth/timing), waters deep-and-infrequent, fertilizes test-driven, prunes the 3 D's |
| 4 | Plants, waters, feeds, and prunes any plant with correct timing; diagnoses over/under-watering and deficiencies on sight; knows spring- vs. summer-bloomer pruning cold |

**Test:** Diagnose a wilting plant in wet soil (over- vs. under-watered) and prescribe correctly; state when to prune a lilac vs. a panicle hydrangea and why.

## Domain 4: Pest & disease management (IPM)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Reaches for spray at first sign of a bug |
| 2 | Knows a few pests; uses pesticides as primary tool |
| 3 | Applies the IPM ladder, identifies the region's common pests/diseases, prevents most problems culturally |
| 4 | Diagnoses before treating, uses least-toxic effective control, protects pollinators/predators, distinguishes "pest" problems from culture problems |

**Test:** Given Japanese beetles on roses, lay out the full IPM response and explain why pheromone traps and broad-spectrum sprays are mistakes.

## Domain 5: Weeds & invasives

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Pulls visible weeds occasionally |
| 2 | Recognizes common weeds; pulls reactively |
| 3 | IDs the Pittsburgh dozen, knows each one's life cycle and weak point, uses mulch/cultural control, recognizes poison ivy |
| 4 | Matches control to life cycle and situation, manages the seed bank (no-dig, never let weeds seed), executes a real multi-season plan for knotweed/garlic mustard, uses herbicide types correctly |

**Test:** Given a weed, state its type and the correct control; lay out a knotweed plan including timing and disposal.

## Domain 6: The garden system (lawn, design, calendar)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Tends plants individually; lawn mowed short and randomly |
| 2 | Has a vague routine; some diversity by accident |
| 3 | Cares for a cool-season lawn by season, designs for some diversity/pollinators, follows a basic calendar |
| 4 | Designs resilient, layered, native, pollinator-supporting plant communities; right-sizes lawn; runs a month-by-month calendar; understands and starts the healthy-garden virtuous cycle |

**Test:** Build a month-by-month maintenance calendar for a real yard and explain how the design choices reduce weeds, pests, and inputs over time.

## Domain 7: Applied gardening (the point of all of it)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Follows instructions without understanding why |
| 2 | Keeps a few plants alive by trial and error |
| 3 | Plans and maintains a healthy garden with intent; solves common problems from principles |
| 4 | Produces and executes a complete master plan for their own site; diagnoses novel problems from first principles; keeps a journal and improves every season |

**Test:** The Final Project (see `PROJECTS.md`), scored against its own rubric.
