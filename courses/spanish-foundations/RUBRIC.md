# Mastery Rubric: SPAN-10

Grade yourself in each domain. **Mastery = Level 4 in every core domain.** The honest test for the spoken domains is the same: record yourself and listen back. For the written domains: check every accent, every agreement, and every verb ending against the Reference, because in writing they are simply right or wrong. Levels:

- **1. Novice:** Aware of the concept; cannot produce it without stopping, looking it up, and luck.
- **2. Developing:** Can produce it slowly, with effort and frequent errors.
- **3. Proficient:** Can produce it reliably and correctly at a slow conversational pace; rare errors.
- **4. Solid:** Automatic, correct, and at a natural pace; you can do it while thinking about the meaning, not the mechanics.

---

## Domain 1: Pronunciation and stress

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | English vowels and stress dominate; hard to understand |
| 2 | Vowels mostly pure when concentrating; stress often misplaced |
| 3 | Pure vowels and correct stress on familiar words; can read new words aloud by the rules |
| 4 | Consistent, clear pronunciation and stress on any written word; trilled r emerging |

**Test:** Read a short paragraph of unfamiliar Spanish aloud; a native speaker understands every word and the stress is right.

## Domain 2: Nouns, articles, and agreement

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Uses nouns without articles; gender random |
| 2 | Knows el/la but guesses gender; adjectives often do not agree |
| 3 | Correct article and gender on known nouns; adjectives agree in gender and number |
| 4 | Gender and full agreement are automatic, including plurals and placement |

**Test:** Describe ten objects with a correctly agreeing article and adjective, no errors.

## Domain 3: ser vs. estar

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot reliably conjugate either; uses them interchangeably |
| 2 | Conjugates both but chooses wrong as often as right |
| 3 | Conjugates both correctly and chooses correctly in clear cases |
| 4 | Chooses correctly even in contrast cases (es/está aburrido); no hesitation |

**Test:** Given eight mixed sentences, choose ser or estar correctly in at least seven.

## Domain 4: Present-tense verbs (regular)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot produce conjugations without the table |
| 2 | Conjugates one family slowly; mixes up -er and -ir |
| 3 | Conjugates all three families correctly for all persons, with thought |
| 4 | Conjugates any regular verb instantly and correctly while speaking |

**Test:** Write the full conjugation of three unfamiliar regular verbs (one per family) from memory, no errors.

## Domain 5: Key irregular and stem-changing verbs

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Knows the verbs exist; cannot conjugate them |
| 2 | Conjugates tener, ir, ser slowly with errors |
| 3 | Conjugates tener, ir, querer, poder, hacer correctly; uses tener que and ir a |
| 4 | All high-frequency irregulars and the boot pattern are automatic in speech |

**Test:** Use tener que, ir a, querer, and poder each correctly in a spontaneous spoken sentence.

## Domain 6: Sentences, questions, and negation

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Produces only isolated words |
| 2 | Builds simple statements; questions and negatives are unreliable |
| 3 | Builds correct statements, yes/no and information questions, and negatives |
| 4 | Builds varied, connected sentences with question words and connectors, fluidly |

**Test:** Ask and answer six varied questions, and produce three correct negative sentences, in conversation.

## Domain 7: The gustar pattern

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Translates "I like" word for word; pattern broken |
| 2 | Uses "me gusta" as a fixed phrase only |
| 3 | Chooses gusta vs. gustan correctly and uses me/te/le/nos/les |
| 4 | Uses gustar and its relatives naturally, including "a mí/a ella" emphasis |

**Test:** Say five likes and dislikes with correct gusta/gustan and pronouns.

## Domain 8: Practical conversation

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot sustain an exchange beyond a memorized greeting |
| 2 | Manages a scripted exchange slowly with long pauses |
| 3 | Holds a short real conversation on familiar topics; uses repair phrases when stuck |
| 4 | Holds a natural short conversation, recovers from gaps with circumlocution, stays in Spanish |

**Test:** Sustain a two-minute unscripted conversation on a familiar topic, using repair strategies instead of switching to English.

## Domain 9: Handwriting and accuracy (the written craft)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Omits accents and question marks; copies with errors |
| 2 | Copies correctly but own writing drops accents and agreements |
| 3 | Own handwritten sentences are correct, with accents and agreement |
| 4 | Writes a correct connected paragraph by hand, fully accented, with few or no errors |

**Test:** Hand-write a six-sentence paragraph about your day with correct spelling, accents, agreement, and punctuation.
