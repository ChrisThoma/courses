# SPAN-10 Syllabus: Spanish I, Foundations for Speaking

## Course description

A three-week intensive in spoken and written Spanish for the absolute beginner who wants to **speak**, not just memorize words. Students learn Spanish pronunciation from the first day, then build the present-tense grammar system that beginning speakers rely on most: noun gender and articles, subject pronouns, regular and high-frequency irregular verbs, ser and estar, questions, negation, and adjective agreement. Along the way students build a core everyday vocabulary, learn how Spanish sentences are assembled, and practice every form out loud and by hand. By the end, students can hold a short, real conversation and write correct simple sentences, and they own a repeatable daily method for learning more.

This is a **speaking-and-writing course**: the bulk of the work is producing the language yourself, every day, out loud and on paper.

## Learning objectives

By the end of this course you will be able to:

1. **Pronounce any written Spanish word correctly** by applying the vowel, consonant, and stress rules, without needing it spelled out for you.
2. **Greet, introduce yourself, and exchange courtesies** appropriately, including choosing between **tú** and **usted**.
3. **Use noun gender, articles, and plurals** correctly, and make adjectives agree in gender and number.
4. **Conjugate and use regular -ar, -er, and -ir verbs** in the present tense for all persons.
5. **Choose correctly between ser and estar**, the two verbs for "to be."
6. **Use the highest-frequency irregular and stem-changing verbs**, including tener, ir, querer, poder, and hacer, in the present tense.
7. **Ask and answer questions** using the main question words, and **form negatives** correctly.
8. **Express likes and dislikes** with the gustar pattern, and tell time, dates, and basic weather.
9. **Build correct simple sentences** in Spanish word order, and stretch a small vocabulary into many sentences.
10. **Hold a short everyday conversation** on familiar topics, understood by a patient native speaker.

## Schedule overview

### Week 1: Sounds, People & the Verb To Be
| Day | Topic |
|---|---|
| 1 | The sounds of Spanish: the alphabet, the five pure vowels, and the stress rules |
| 2 | Greetings, introductions, courtesy, and tú vs. usted |
| 3 | Nouns: gender, the articles (el, la, los, las, un, una), and plurals |
| 4 | Subject pronouns and **ser** (to be): identity, origin, what something is |
| 5 | **estar** (to be): location and condition, and how to choose ser vs. estar |
| 6 | Numbers 0 to 100 and telling time, then **Quiz 1** |
| 7 | Review and consolidation day, plus Project 1 (Introduce Yourself) |

### Week 2: Regular Verbs & Sentence Structure
| Day | Topic |
|---|---|
| 8 | The present tense of regular **-ar** verbs, and basic sentence word order |
| 9 | The present tense of regular **-er** and **-ir** verbs |
| 10 | Asking questions: the question words and how questions are formed |
| 11 | Negation, and adjective agreement and placement |
| 12 | **tener** (to have), tener expressions, and tener que plus an infinitive |
| 13 | **ir** (to go), ir a plus an infinitive for the near future, and al/del, then **Quiz 2** |
| 14 | Review and consolidation day, plus Project 2 (My Day in Writing) |

### Week 3: Everyday Communication
| Day | Topic |
|---|---|
| 15 | Common stem-changing and irregular verbs: querer, poder, hacer, and friends |
| 16 | **gustar** and the like pattern: saying what you like and dislike |
| 17 | Days, months, dates, and weather, and making simple plans |
| 18 | Practical and workplace vocabulary, and basic polite requests |
| 19 | Simple commands (the affirmative usted command) and useful connectors |
| 20 | Putting it together: a full everyday conversation, plus Project 3 |
| 21 | **Final exam**, plus the Final Conversation recording is due |

## Daily structure (about 60 to 90 minutes)

| Block | Time | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| Warm up and review | 10 min | Say yesterday's vocabulary aloud; redo one conjugation from memory by hand; the day's quick drill from the Practice Menu |
| Listen and say | 5 min | Hear the day's new sounds, words, or dialogue and repeat each one out loud |
| Lecture | 15 to 20 min | Read the lecture; say every example aloud as you reach it |
| Write by hand | 15 to 20 min | Handwrite the day's conjugation tables and copy the example sentences, then write your own |
| Speak and record | 10 to 15 min | The day's speaking task; record it and listen back |
| Self-check | 5 min | Answer the self-check from memory; note what to review tomorrow |

| Project 1: Introduce Yourself | 10% |
| Project 2: My Day in Writing | 15% |
| Project 3: A Real Exchange | 15% |
| Final Conversation Recording | 25% |
| Final Exam | 15% |

Grade yourself against `RUBRIC.md`. **Mastery threshold: 90%.** The honest grading is split between the written work, where accents and agreement either are correct or are not, and the spoken work, where you record yourself and listen back without flattering yourself. If a sentence is right on paper but you cannot say it aloud at conversational speed, it is not done yet.

## Course policies

- **Out loud, always.** A Spanish sentence you have only read is not learned. Every example, every drill, every answer gets said aloud. Silent study does not count here.
- **By hand, always.** Conjugations and example sentences are written by hand, on paper, every day. Typing is not a substitute. The friction of the pen is the point.
- **Short and daily beats long and rare.** Forty focused minutes a day, six days a week, builds a language. One long weekend session does not, because memory needs sleep between repetitions.
- **Review before you add.** Always re-touch yesterday before starting today. This course is cumulative: weak verb endings in Week 1 wreck every sentence in Weeks 2 and 3.
- **Make mistakes on purpose.** Guess, say it wrong, find out, fix it. A wrong answer you corrected sticks far better than a right answer you looked up. Closed-book quizzes exist to find your gaps, not to make you feel good.
- **Record yourself weekly.** It is the most uncomfortable and most useful thing you will do. You hear your real pronunciation and pace, not the flattering version in your head.
- **Falling behind:** use the weekly review day to catch up. If you are more than a day behind, slow down rather than skip. Drop the optional vocabulary first, never the core grammar days.
