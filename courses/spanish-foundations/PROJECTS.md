# Projects: SPAN-10

Projects are where isolated grammar turns into things you can actually say to a person. Each has a written part (by hand) and a spoken part (recorded), because in a language both halves must work. **Record everything and grade yourself by listening back**, which is far more honest than judging while you speak. "I basically said it" is a 70, not a 95. The written standard everywhere is correct spelling, accents, agreement, and punctuation; the spoken standard is understandable, in correct forms, at a steady pace.

> **Why both written and recorded?** Writing by hand forces the accuracy (every accent and ending), and recording forces the reality (your real pronunciation and pace). Together they are the most honest mirror you have, and the recordings become a record of how far you have come.

---

## Project 1: Introduce Yourself *(Day 7 · 10% of grade)*

**Goal:** prove the Week 1 foundation: pronunciation, greetings, ser and estar, and basic nouns. This is the Domain 1 to 3 test.

### Deliverable
1. **A handwritten self-introduction** of six to eight sentences: greet, give your name, say where you are from (ser), your job (ser), how you are right now (estar), where you are (estar), and one or two more true facts. All accents and question marks included.
2. **A recording (about one minute)** of you saying that introduction aloud, clearly and at a steady pace, without reading word for word if you can manage it.

### Rubric (10 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 3 | ser and estar chosen and conjugated correctly throughout |
| 2 | Pronunciation: pure vowels, correct stress, understandable |
| 2 | Handwriting is correct, with all accents and punctuation |
| 2 | Greeting and courtesy included and appropriate (tú vs. usted consistent) |
| 1 | Delivered at a steady pace, not halting word by word |

**Pass bar:** a patient Spanish speaker would understand every sentence and the ser/estar choices would all be right.

## Project 2: My Day in Writing *(Day 14 · 15% of grade)*

**Goal:** demonstrate connected present-tense writing and speech: regular and key irregular verbs, tener que, ir a, and correct sentence structure. This is the Domain 4 to 6 test.

### Deliverable
1. **A handwritten paragraph (eight to twelve sentences)** describing a typical day: what you do (mix of -ar, -er, -ir verbs), where you go (ir a), what you have to do (tener que), and what time things happen. Use at least eight different verbs and at least two connectors.
2. **A recording** of you reading the paragraph aloud, plus one or two extra sentences answering "¿Qué vas a hacer mañana?" from memory.

### Rubric (15 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 4 | Verb conjugations correct across all three families and persons used |
| 3 | tener que and ir a used correctly with infinitives |
| 3 | Sentence structure and word order correct; connectors used |
| 3 | Handwriting correct: spelling, accents, agreement, punctuation |
| 2 | Spoken delivery clear and understandable at a steady pace |

**Pass bar:** the paragraph reads as correct connected Spanish, and the spoken "tomorrow" answer is produced without a script.

---

## Project 3: A Real Exchange *(Day 20 · 15% of grade)*

**Goal:** a realistic two-person conversation using the full toolkit, including gustar, questions, and practical phrasing. This is the Domain 6 to 8 test.

### Deliverable
1. **A handwritten dialogue (twelve to sixteen lines)** set in a situation you actually face, using at least: one ser and one estar, three different verbs, one tener que, one ir a or plan, one gustar, two question words, and two connectors. Use usted if the situation is formal.
2. **A recording** of the conversation performed: both parts yourself, or with a partner if you have one. Aim for a natural pace and clear, polite forms.

### Rubric (15 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 4 | All required structures present and correct (checklist above) |
| 3 | gustar used correctly (gusta/gustan and the right pronoun) |
| 3 | Questions and answers are correct and natural |
| 3 | Handwriting correct and fully accented |
| 2 | Spoken delivery: understandable, steady, appropriate register (tú/usted) |

**Pass bar:** the exchange could actually happen, and a Spanish speaker would follow it without effort.

---

## Final Project: The Final Conversation *(Day 21 · 25% of grade)*

**Goal:** the capstone. A sustained, realistic conversation that proves you can use the language, not just recite forms. This is the Domain 1 to 9 test together.

### Specification
1. **A recorded conversation of two to three minutes** on familiar topics: introductions, where you are from and what you do, your likes and dislikes, your schedule or plans, and at least one moment where you ask for help or clarification. Both parts yourself or with a partner.
2. **At least three places where you handle a gap without switching to English:** ask for a repeat, ask how to say something, or talk around a word you do not know.
3. **The handwritten script** of the conversation, fully accented and correct, turned in alongside the recording.
4. **A short written reflection (a few sentences):** what came easiest, what is still hardest, and where and with whom you will keep practicing after the course.

### Rubric (25 pts)
| Pts | Criterion |
|---|---|
| 6 | Range and correctness of structures used (ser/estar, verbs, tener/ir, gustar, questions) |
| 5 | Understandable pronunciation and steady, conversational pace |
| 4 | Stays in Spanish; handles gaps with repair and circumlocution, not English |
| 4 | Handwritten script correct: spelling, accents, agreement, punctuation |
| 3 | Register appropriate and consistent (tú/usted) |
| 3 | Reflection thoughtful, and a concrete plan to keep practicing |

**Graduation bar:** 23+/25, **and** a Spanish speaker who did not know you were a beginner would follow the whole conversation comfortably. Save the recording. It is your "before."
