# Practice Menu (Drills): SPAN-10

The lessons prescribe a default daily drill, but no single drill fits everyone every day. This is the full menu plus the spaced-repetition system that holds it together. **Pick one drill per active skill and rotate every few days** so nothing goes stale. Two things need daily reps for the whole course and beyond: **spaced-repetition review (A)** and **speaking out loud (E)**.

**Building your daily review (about 15 minutes, on top of the day's lesson):** 5 min spaced-repetition cards (A) · 5 min conjugation by hand (B) · 5 min speaking out loud (E). Everything written is done by hand; everything spoken is done out loud and, once a week, recorded.

> **The one rule above all drills:** *correct and out loud beats fast and silent.* If a drill gets sloppy, slow down and fix the error; do not paper over it with speed. And never write a Spanish word in this course without its accents, or a noun without its article.

---

## A. Spaced repetition (start Day 7, never stop)

The engine of long-term memory. Make a card for each vocabulary item and verb, review on a schedule, and let forgetting do its job.

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **A1: Make the cards** | One card per item: Spanish on one side (noun WITH its article), English on the other. Verbs get a card for the infinitive and the meaning | building the deck |
| **A2: Daily review pass** | Go through today's due cards; say each answer aloud before flipping; sort into "knew it" and "missed it" | the core memory drill |
| **A3: The leitner schedule** | Missed cards come back tomorrow; known cards stretch to 3 days, then a week, then two | spacing the reps efficiently |
| **A4: Reverse direction** | Once a word is easy English to Spanish, drill it Spanish to English too, then in a sentence | deeper retrieval |

A paper Leitner box (envelopes labeled "daily, 3-day, weekly, monthly") works as well as any app. The point is reviewing on a schedule, not just when something is new.

## B. Conjugation by hand (start Day 4, daily)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **B1: Full-table write-out** | Pick a verb; write all six present-tense forms by hand, saying each aloud | locking in the patterns |
| **B2: Conjugation sprint** | Pick five random verbs; write each full conjugation as fast as you can while staying correct; time it | speed with accuracy |
| **B3: Person-call** | Have a list of verbs; for a called person ("ellos, comer") write just that form | the form you need on demand |
| **B4: Boot-circle** | For a stem-changing verb, write the table and circle the four changed forms | seeing the boot pattern |

## C. Nouns, articles & agreement (start Day 3)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **C1: Article-and-plural** | For a noun, write it with el/la, then plural with los/las, then with un/una | the noun-plus-article habit |
| **C2: Four-form agreement** | Write a noun-plus-adjective in all four forms (masc/fem, sing/plural) | agreement reflex |
| **C3: Gender-guess** | Guess a new noun's gender from its ending, then check; track your hit rate | internalizing gender rules |

## D. ser vs. estar & sentence building (start Day 5)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **D1: ser-or-estar** | Take ten mixed sentence frames; fill the right verb and say why (identity vs. state/location) | the core choice |
| **D2: Transform** | Turn a statement into a question, then into a negative, then change the subject | flexible sentences |
| **D3: Question round** | Write or ask one question for each question word about the same person | question fluency |

## E. Speaking out loud (start Day 1, every day, no exceptions)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **E1: Read-aloud** | Read any Spanish text aloud, applying the pronunciation and stress rules | pronunciation and reading |
| **E2: Self-narration** | Narrate what you are doing or planning, out loud, in Spanish, for two minutes | spontaneous speech |
| **E3: Survival-phrase reps** | Run the 30 survival phrases out loud until they need no thought | real-interaction readiness |
| **E4: Shadowing** | Play a short clip of natural Spanish and speak along a beat behind it, copying the rhythm | accent and flow |
| **E5: Record and listen** | Once a week, record a one-minute monologue and listen back against the rubric | honest self-assessment |
| **E6: Circumlocution game** | Pick a word you do not know; talk your way around it in Spanish until the meaning is clear | staying in the conversation |

## F. Listening & input (start Day 1)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **F1: Daily input** | Listen to a few minutes of Spanish a little above your level (slow-news, learner podcasts) | training the ear |
| **F2: Catch-the-verb** | While listening, count or jot every verb you recognize | hearing structure |
| **F3: Dictation** | Have a sentence read (or use a clip with a transcript); write what you hear, then check | spelling, accents, sound-to-letter |

## Rules that make any drill work

1. **Correct first, fast later.** A drill done wrong trains the wrong form. The pace at which you are accurate is the right pace today.
2. **By hand for anything written, out loud for anything spoken.** No silent reading, no typing the conjugations. The friction is the learning.
3. **Always the accents and the articles.** A missing accent is a wrong answer; a noun without its gender is half-learned.
4. **Review before you add.** Re-touch yesterday at the start of every session. Spacing beats cramming every time.
5. **Record yourself weekly.** Uncomfortable and the fastest feedback you will get on pronunciation and pace.
6. **Keep one number.** Log your daily card count, your conjugation-sprint time, your longest unscripted spoken stretch. The line going up is the motivation.
7. **When a drill gets easy, retire it** and pull the next one. Easy drills are entertainment, not practice.
8. **Short and daily beats long and rare.** Forty focused minutes a day, with sleep between, wires a language in faster than weekend marathons.
