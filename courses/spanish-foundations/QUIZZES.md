# Quizzes and Final Exam: SPAN-10

Rules: **closed book.** Write your answers by hand on paper, with all accents and question marks, then grade against `ANSWER-KEYS.md`. Passing = 90%. The "say it" questions are graded by honest self-assessment against the stated standard; record yourself if unsure. Where a question asks you to write Spanish, a missing accent or wrong agreement is a wrong answer.

---

## Quiz 1: Sounds, People & the Verb To Be *(Day 6 · 40 points · about 40 min)*

### Part A: Sounds and stress (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Give the five Spanish vowel sounds in order.
2. What sound does the letter h make?
3. Which two letters sound the same in Spanish?
4. Where is the stress in a word ending in a vowel, n, or s?
5. Where is the stress in a word ending in any other consonant?
6. What does a written accent mark tell you?
7. Mark the stressed syllable: tra-ba-jo.
8. Mark the stressed syllable: es-pa-nol (español).

### Part B: Greetings and register (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Give the greeting for the morning and for the evening/night.
2. How do you say "My name is..."?
3. How do you say "Nice to meet you"?
4. What is the difference between tú and usted?
5. Which do you use with a stranger or in a work setting, as the safe default?
6. How do you ask "How are you?" formally?
7. How do you say "And you?" formally?
8. Translate: "Where are you from?" (formal)

### Part C: Nouns and articles (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What are the two genders, and what ending usually marks each?
2. Why must you always learn a noun with its article?
3. Give the four forms of "the."
4. Make plural: la mujer.
5. Make plural: el día.
6. Give the indefinite article for: ___ amigo and ___ casa.
7. What gender is "día," and why is it notable?
8. Add -s or -es: ciudad becomes ___.

### Part D: ser and estar (10 pts, 1 each)
1. Conjugate ser: yo, tú, ella.
2. Conjugate estar: nosotros, tú, ellos.
3. Choose and write: Yo ___ de México. (origin)
4. Choose and write: El libro ___ en la mesa. (location)
5. Choose and write: Nosotros ___ cansados. (condition)
6. Choose and write: Ella ___ médica. (profession)
7. State the one-line rule for choosing ser vs. estar.
8. "I am from Mexico" vs. "I am in Mexico": which verb for each?
9. Translate: "She is the boss."
10. Translate: "How are you?" (informal, condition)

### Part E: Numbers and time (6 pts, 1 each)
1. Write in words: 15, 21, 48.
2. How do you join tens and units, as in 45? Write 45 in words.
3. Why is it "es la una" but "son las dos"?
4. How do you say 4:30?
5. How do you say 6:45?
6. How do you say "at eight o'clock"?

## Quiz 2: Regular Verbs & Sentence Structure *(Day 13 · 40 points · about 40 min)*

### Part A: Regular verbs (10 pts, 1 each)
1. Give the six present-tense endings for -ar verbs.
2. Give the six endings for -er verbs.
3. Where exactly do -er and -ir verbs differ?
4. Conjugate hablar: yo, tú, nosotros.
5. Conjugate comer: ella, ustedes.
6. Conjugate vivir: yo, nosotros.
7. What ending does the yo form take for all three families?
8. What three English meanings can "hablo" carry?
9. What is the basic Spanish statement word order?
10. Why can you usually drop the subject pronoun?

### Part B: Questions and negation (8 pts, 1 each)
1. How do you turn a statement into a yes/no question?
2. Do you need a word like "do" to ask a question?
3. Give the question words for what, who, where, when.
4. Give the question words for why, how, how much.
5. What is the difference between "por qué" and "porque"?
6. Where do you put "no" to make a sentence negative?
7. Are double negatives correct? Give an example.
8. Turn into a yes/no question: Ella habla inglés.

### Part C: Adjectives (6 pts, 1 each)
1. How many forms does an adjective ending in -o have?
2. Give the four forms of "bueno."
3. Does an adjective ending in -e change for gender?
4. Where does a descriptive adjective normally go?
5. Make agree: una casa + grande, then make it plural.
6. Make agree: los amigos + bueno.

### Part D: tener and ir (10 pts, 1 each)
1. Conjugate tener: yo, tú, ellos.
2. Conjugate ir: yo, nosotros, ella.
3. How do you say "I am thirty years old"?
4. Give three tener expressions for physical states.
5. What does "tener que" plus an infinitive mean? Give an example.
6. What word follows ir to show destination?
7. Give the two mandatory contractions and when they apply.
8. Translate: "We are going to eat." (ir a + infinitive)
9. Translate: "He has to call the client."
10. In "Voy a trabajar," which verb is conjugated and which is the infinitive?

### Part E: Build it (6 pts) *(write by hand, fully accented)*
1. (3) Write three connected sentences about your day using a present-tense verb, a "tengo que..." and an "ir a..." 
2. (3) Write two questions and their answers using two different question words.

## Final Exam *(Day 21 · 60 points · about 60 min)*

### Part A: Rapid fundamentals (10 pts, 1 each, answer each in a few seconds)
1. The five vowel sounds? 2. The sound of h? 3. Where is stress in a word ending in s? 4. ser or estar for location? 5. ser or estar for origin? 6. The yo ending for all regular verbs? 7. Where do you put "no" for negation? 8. The two contractions a+el and de+el? 9. gusta or gustan with "los libros"? 10. tú or usted with a new client?

### Part B: Verbs (12 pts, 2 each)
1. Conjugate vivir fully (all six persons).
2. Conjugate ser fully.
3. Conjugate tener fully.
4. Conjugate querer fully (watch the stem change).
5. Conjugate poder fully (watch the stem change).
6. Conjugate ir fully.

### Part C: Choosing and forming (10 pts, 1 each)
1. ser or estar: Yo ___ ocupado hoy.
2. ser or estar: La oficina ___ grande.
3. ser or estar: ¿Dónde ___ el cliente?
4. ser or estar: Ellos ___ de Colombia.
5. Make agree: una pregunta + difícil.
6. Make plural: el problema.
7. Correct the contraction: Voy a el trabajo.
8. Make negative: Tengo tiempo.
9. Turn into a question: Tú trabajas mañana.
10. Give the usted command of "esperar."

### Part D: gustar, time & practical (10 pts, 1 each)
1. What does gustar literally mean?
2. gusta or gustan: Me ___ el café.
3. gusta or gustan: Me ___ los lunes.
4. Which form with an activity, like "leer"?
5. Translate: "I like to work here."
6. Translate: "She doesn't like the cold." (use gustar)
7. How do you say today's day if it is Monday?
8. Which verb does most weather use? Give an example.
9. Give a polite phrase to ask someone to repeat.
10. Give a polite phrase to ask someone to slow down.

### Part E: Write it (10 pts) *(by hand, fully accented)*
1. (5) Write a five-sentence paragraph about your typical day, using at least three different verbs, one tener que, and one ir a.
2. (5) Write a six-line dialogue in which one person asks for help and the other responds, using usted and at least two question words.

### Part F: Say it (8 pts) *(record it)*
1. (3) Read a short unfamiliar Spanish paragraph aloud; self-score on pure vowels and correct stress.
2. (3) Give a one-minute spoken self-introduction from memory (name, origin, job, two likes); self-score on correct forms and steady pace.
3. (2) Improvise three sentences about your plans for tomorrow using ir a; self-score on whether they came without a script.
