# Answer Keys: DM-101

Grade honestly. For reasoning questions, award the point if your answer captures the key idea (exact wording doesn't matter). For the applied "make the call" questions, the test is judgment, not matching my DC to the decimal: award credit if you correctly decided *whether* to roll and *whether there were stakes*, even if your DC differs from mine by a few points. Passing = 90%. If you miss something, re-read the cited day before relying on it at the table.

## Quiz 1: How the Game Runs (40 pts)

### Part A: The role, the loop, the pillars (10)
1. **Present a world, adjudicate what the players try to do in it, and narrate the results, so everyone (including you) has a good time.** (Day 1)
2. **Referee** (apply the rules fairly and consistently), **Narrator** (describe the world, NPCs, and results; set tone and pace), **Fan** (root for the characters and build moments for them). (Day 1)
3. **DM describes the situation → players say what they do → DM adjudicates → DM narrates the result** (and back). (Day 1)
4. **Exploration** (description + ability checks), **Social interaction** (roleplay + the Influence action), **Combat** (initiative + the action economy). (Day 1)
5. You genuinely **want the characters to be cool** and build situations that let them shine; it's the role that makes a table people come back to. (Day 1)
6. The **shared agreement** on the kind of game, boundaries, logistics, and conduct; established in **session zero**. (Days 1, 17)
7. You're the **impartial host**: not trying to beat the players, not there to serve their every whim; you run a fair world that says yes to clever ideas and "roll for it" to risky ones. (Day 1)
8. Any three: **the rules** (free rules are enough to start, with this course and linked references filling in DM-facing tools), **dice**, **something to track initiative/notes**, **players** (3–5), **your DM screen**. (Day 1)
9. **No.** The free 2024 rules are enough to start, and this course plus the linked references cover what you need for the labs. (Day 1)
10. It leads to **railroading** and ignores **player agency**; the players co-author the story through their choices, and the DM facilitates rather than dictates. (Day 1)

### Part B: Adjudication (10)
1. **"Say yes, or roll the dice":** roll **only when the outcome is uncertain AND interesting either way.** Otherwise auto-succeed or auto-fail. (Day 2)
2. **Ability check** (can they do it?), **saving throw** (can they resist it?), **attack roll** (do they hit?). (Day 2)
3. Default **15**; **up** for time pressure / bad conditions / poor approach; **down** (or grant advantage) for good tools / a clever approach / help. (Day 2)
4. So the roll **carries stakes and tension** and the players care. Strong example: "DC 15; on a hit you're up before the guard rounds the corner, on a miss you make it but knock a stone loose and he hears it." (Day 2)
5. **Roll 2d20, keep the higher** (worth roughly +5); it's fast, intuitive, and rewards clever play at no tracking cost (doesn't stack; cancels with disadvantage). (Day 2)
6. **10 + all modifiers** (advantage +5, disadvantage −5). Using **Passive Perception** avoids tipping the table off that something's there and saves constant rolls. (Day 2)
7. **Search** = actively discerning something that isn't obvious with Wisdom (Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival). **Study** = recalling or reasoning through information with Intelligence (Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion). (Day 2)
8. The **Study** action, an **Intelligence** check (Arcana/Religion/History as fits). (Day 2)
9. It **stalls the game**; a fast, fair, confident ruling beats a slow "correct" one, and you can revisit it after the session. (Days 2, 16)
10. Any clear auto-success, e.g., **opening an unlocked door, a trained climber on a ladder, recalling something obvious.** (Day 2)

### Part C: The pillars in practice (12)
1. A **unit of attention**: one location/situation with a reason to be there and a decision; thinking in scenes lets you **cut** past boring steps. (Day 3)
2. **Impression, most interesting feature, exit/hook** (then stop and ask "what do you do?"). (Day 3)
3. Time and light create **pressure and stakes** (torches burn out, rituals complete); without a clock players will search every room forever. (Day 3)
4. **Tell, trigger, effect.** **Telegraph** = give an observable hint so triggering it is a real failure, not a "gotcha." (Day 3)
5. On a **decision/question**, usually "what do you do?" (Day 3)
6. **A want, an attitude, a voice tag.** (Day 4)
7. **Friendly** (reasonable asks may just succeed; advantage if a roll is needed), **Indifferent** (default for strangers; roll normally if hesitant), **Hostile** (big asks may be impossible until the attitude shifts; disadvantage if a roll is possible). (Day 4)
8. Because the roll **resolves a real attempt**; roleplaying first keeps social play from collapsing into "I roll Persuasion," and a good argument can make the NPC willing, shift attitude, or justify advantage. (Day 4)
9. **Shifting an NPC's attitude** by appealing to what they actually want. (Day 4)
10. Power ≈ the **number of meaningful actions per round**; a lone monster gets one turn against the party's several, so it's outnumbered into irrelevance (and one stun shuts it down for a round). (Day 5)
11. **−2 to all d20 Tests and −5 ft Speed per level**; at **level 6, the creature dies.** (Day 5, Field Guide)
12. Fast (any two): telegraph the on-deck player, track HP by description not numbers, roll monster attacks fast. More-than-HP (any two): terrain, an objective beyond killing, smart monster tactics, hazards. (Day 5)

### Part D: Applied (8): make the call
1. **(2) Auto-yes**, no roll (trained, calm conditions). (Day 2)
2. **(2) Roll** Thieves' Tools (Dex), **DC ~15**; stakes: success = through before the patrol arrives; failure = still through, but caught here mid-room / the next patrol arrives. (Full marks for roll + ability + DC + both stakes; partial for getting "this is a roll with stakes.") (Day 2)
3. **(2) Auto-fail**: 40 ft is far beyond a normal jump; offer another approach. (A roll only if magically aided.) (Day 2)
4. **(1)** Any complete NPC: name + one want + attitude + voice tag.
5. **(1)** Any rewrite where failure **changes the situation**, e.g., "The pick snaps off in the lock, and you hear boots on the stair." (Day 15)

**Quiz 1 total: /40. 36+ = pass.**

## Quiz 2: The Craft (40 pts)

### Part A: Prep (10)
1. A **plot** is an expected sequence of events (it **snaps** when players deviate); a **situation** is pieces in tension that **react** to anything the players do (it bends). Situation-prep survives contact with players. (Day 8)
2. The **first scene, already in motion, requiring a decision**; a quiet tavern has no momentum and opens on dead air. (Day 8)
3. They're **portable facts** you can reveal wherever the players look, so a missed roll or skipped scene never dead-ends the session. (Day 8)
4. **Portable** = not chained to one scene or source; reachable multiple ways. It keeps the game from stalling on a single missed discovery. (Day 8)
5. Any four: **strong start, potential scenes, secrets/clues, fantastic locations, NPCs, monsters/encounters, rewards.** (Day 8)
6. Any three: **exact dialogue, the "right" solution, contingencies for unlikely actions, deep decision trees, full read-aloud for every room.** (Day 8)
7. **"Was I never stuck?"** (not "did it all happen?"). (Day 8)
8. It forces you to prep only the **high-leverage** material and keeps it **scannable** while you run. (Day 8)
9. From **one evocative sentence/image** each; you fill in the rest live. (Day 8)
10. Situation-prep + **portable clues** + an **NPC stock and stat-block reskins** respond wherever they go; you drop clues where they actually look. (Days 8, 19)

### Part B: Encounters (10)
1. **Pick a difficulty; read the per-character budget for the party's level; multiply by the number of characters; spend it on monsters' XP up to the budget.** (Day 9)
2. **No multiplier in 2024**: you simply **sum the monsters' XP.** (2014 multiplied the total by a factor based on monster count.) (Day 9)
3. **Level-3 Moderate = 225 per character × 4 = 900 XP.** (Day 9, Field Guide)
4. It gets **one turn** against the party's several, so it's **outnumbered on actions** and easily shut down by one condition. (Day 9)
5. Give it **minions, lair actions, or multi-action features** so it threatens the whole party every round. (Day 9)
6. **Many turns and attack rolls**, hard to neutralize all at once; it overperforms its XP. (Day 9)
7. Any three: **terrain, an objective beyond killing, a variety of monster roles** (and a reason to be there). (Day 9)
8. **Low** drains some resources; **Moderate** is a real fight with risk; **High** is dangerous and could go badly. (Day 9, Field Guide)
9. Any two: **reinforcements arrive (or don't), monsters flee when clearly losing, add or drop a wave, change the objective**, all without fudging dice. (Day 9)
10. Players can **feel when their choices don't matter**; silently rewriting outcomes breaks the trust that the rolls are real, which the whole game depends on. (Day 9)

### Part C: Adventures, worlds, NPCs, rewards (10)
1. **Location-based** = a place explored in any order (structure lives in the map); **event-based** = a situation in motion over time (structure lives in the timeline/factions). (Day 10)
2. **Entrance & guardian; puzzle/roleplay; trick/setback; climax/big battle; reward/revelation.** (Day 10)
3. For **any conclusion the players must reach, include at least three clues**; solves players missing, ignoring, or destroying clues (and dead-ending). (Day 10)
4. It's tied to **what the characters already want or fear** (backstory, bonds, a threat to something they value), not a generic "mysterious stranger." (Day 10)
5. **Top-down** = big to small (good with a published setting like **Greyhawk**); **bottom-up** = small to big and is **recommended for a first campaign.** (Day 11)
6. **Name + one-line identity, 3–5 locations, 3–5 NPCs with wants, 2–3 factions, 1 looming problem.** (Day 11)
7. **Want, method, asset, weakness, face, clock.** A **clock** = the faction's next moves, made whether or not the party intervenes. (Day 12)
8. They make threats **advance offscreen**, so the world moves on its own and the party's inaction has consequences. (Day 12)
9. **Treasure, magic items, advancement (leveling).** Pace magic items with **rarity and attunement** (a character can attune to at most 3). (Day 12)
10. A fiction-tied reward **doubles as a clue/hook** and is memorable, e.g., the drowned merchant's signet ring that opens the harbor vault. (Day 12)

### Part D: Applied (10): build it
1. **(4)** Level-5 Moderate = **750 × 5 = 3,750 XP.** Award full marks for: correct budget math + a plausible monster mix summing toward (not over) 3,750 + one terrain feature + one non-kill objective. Partial for correct math alone.
2. **(3)** A one-line solution + **three portable clues**, each reachable two ways. (Deduct if any clue is chained to a single scene/source.)
3. **(3)** A faction with **want, method, face, and a 3-step clock.**

**Quiz 2 total: /40. 36+ = pass.**

## Final Exam (60 pts)

### Part A: Foundations recall (10)
1. Present a world, adjudicate, narrate, so everyone has fun. 2. describe → act → adjudicate → narrate. 3. Exploration (description + checks), Social (roleplay + Influence), Combat (initiative + action economy). 4. When the outcome is **uncertain AND interesting either way.** 5. Default **15**; ladder: **very easy 5, easy 10, medium 15, hard 20, very hard 25, nearly impossible 30.** 6. Ability check, saving throw, attack roll. 7. So the roll has **stakes** and the players care. 8. **Friendly, Indifferent, Hostile.** 9. **−2 to all d20 Tests, −5 ft Speed per level; level 6 = death.** 10. It builds a table people return to / you create moments for the characters.

### Part B: The craft (10)
1. A plot **snaps** when players deviate; a situation **bends/reacts.** 2. Five of: strong start, scenes, secrets/clues, locations, NPCs, monsters, rewards. 3. For any needed conclusion, **≥3 clues.** 4. Pick difficulty → per-character budget → × number of characters → spend monster XP. 5. **No.** 6. Entrance/guardian, puzzle/roleplay, trick/setback, climax, reward/revelation. 7. **Bottom-up** small→big (good for new DMs); top-down big→small. 8. A faction's next offscreen moves; it makes the world move and inaction matter. 9. Treasure, magic items, advancement. 10. **Milestone:** simpler, paces the story to beats; **XP:** granular, rewards combat directly.

### Part C: Running and sustaining (12)
1. A quick **recap** then a **strong start**; never open with silence. 2. Any three: **cut to the action, vary the rhythm, read the room, introduce a clock when energy sags.** 3. **Sharing limited attention** so each player gets a moment; players who never get one stop coming back. 4. (1) what/why are they doing it; (2) auto-yes/no?; (3) ability + DC; (4) state stakes, roll; (5) narrate the changed situation; (6) move on, note to check later. 5. It **costs a roll (not free), applies to enemies too, and shouldn't trivialize a class feature.** 6. Ask them to read it; let it work **as written** unless clearly broken; if it's a slow edge case, **rule quickly now and look it up at the break.** 7. **Pitch/tone, expectations/logistics, characters & bonds, lines/veils (safety).** 8. Any two: **lines/veils, the X-card, open-door**; they set boundaries so players can take dramatic risks safely. 9. **T1 (1–4) local, T2 (5–10) regional, T3 (11–16) realm/planar, T4 (17–20) world/multiverse**; stakes grow with tier. 10. A recurring villain **accrues history and hatred**, learns from the party, and returns, so the finale lands. 11. **Reincorporation** = reaching backward to reuse an established detail; it makes improvisation feel authored and ties the world tight. 12. **Scheduling collapse**; defend with a realistic cadence + a "go" rule (play with, say, 3 of 5), ending on time, and a way to sideline absent characters.

### Part D: 2024 rules specifics (8)
1. **Influence** (social: persuade/deceive/intimidate), **Search** (actively discern something with Wisdom), **Study** (recall/reason through information with Intelligence), **Utilize** (use a non-weapon object), **Magic** (cast a spell / use a magic effect). 2. Any four with one effect each: **Prone** (disadvantage on attacks; melee against it has advantage, ranged disadvantage; half movement to stand), **Grappled** (Speed 0), **Frightened** (disadvantage on checks/attacks while the source is in sight; can't move closer), **Poisoned** (disadvantage on attacks and ability checks). 3. **Half = +2 AC/Dex saves; three-quarters = +5; total = can't be targeted directly.** 4. **Topple:** target makes a Con save or falls **Prone**; **Vex:** advantage on your **next** attack against that target; **Push:** push the target back (up to 10 ft); **Slow:** reduce the target's Speed by 10 ft. 5. At 0 HP, each turn roll a d20 (no mods): **10+ = success, under 10 = failure; three successes = stable, three failures = dead**; a nat 1 = two failures, a nat 20 = regain 1 HP; taking damage at 0 HP = one failure (a melee crit from within 5 ft = two). 6. **Level 5**; a Bastion Turn represents about **7 days.** 7. A character can be **attuned to at most 3 items at once** (attuning takes a short rest), which throttles how many strong items are active. 8. **Individual treasure** (coins, sometimes a small valuable, on a creature) and **treasure hoards** (coins + gemstones/art objects + possibly magic items), both scaled to the party's tier.

### Part E: Integrated DM problem (20)
1. **(3)** It's not a problem because you prepped a **situation, not a plot**: the cult situation responds wherever the party goes, your **portable clues** drop where they actually look, and your **NPC stock/reskins** cover anyone they meet. The dungeon is **waiting, not wasted.** Full marks: names situation-prep + portable clues. (Days 8, 19)
2. **(3)** Reskin a stat block (e.g., **Cult Fanatic** or **Spy**) as the contact the priest meets; **reincorporate** by making that contact someone already established (the frightened dockworker from session 1, the missing bell-ringer's kin, a face from an earlier scene). Full marks: a reskin + a genuine callback. (Days 12, 19)
3. **(3)** It's a roll: **Sleight of Hand (Dex), DC ~15** (the crowd helps; the priest is wary), stakes: success = you lift the letter unseen; failure = you get it but he feels the tug / a bystander notices and may cry out. Full marks: roll + ability + DC + both stakes. (Days 2, 16)
4. **(3)** A direct, character-anchored **spotlight pass**: "Kessa, you've been hanging back watching the crowd, what's your read, and what do you do?" It works because it **invites them in with something only they can answer**, without putting them on the spot for not engaging. (Day 15)
5. **(3)** **Hostile** means a big ask (just talk him down) may be **impossible** at his current attitude, and you cannot simply roll him into compliance. **Roleplay first**; find his **want/lever** (coerced into the cult? fears the god? loves the town) and use it to make him willing or **shift him toward Indifferent**. If a roll is now fair, the DC is **15 or his Intelligence score, whichever is higher**; a still-Hostile but influenceable NPC imposes disadvantage. Full marks: Hostile limits + attitude/situation shift + roleplay-then-roll. (Day 4)
6. **(3)** Advance the cult **clock to step 2** (e.g., the lower town floods as a "sign," the wards failing): the **world visibly changes**, so the party's choices (and their inaction this session) carry weight and create pressure to act. Full marks: a concrete clock advance + why it matters. (Days 12, 18)
7. **(2)** Any tense, unresolved **closing hook/image**: the stolen letter names someone they trust; floodwater begins rising in the streets; a child sleepwalks past the inn window. (Day 15)
