# Practice Menu: DM Drills

DM skill is not built by reading; it's built by **reps of the live reflexes**: inventing a name without freezing, setting a DC in two seconds, narrating a result that moves the story, voicing an NPC, saying "yes, and." These are short, repeatable, and most of them you do out loud, alone, in five minutes. This is the menu. **Pick a couple of daily warm-ups and a few weekly practices; rotate.** The DM who does ten minutes of reps before each session and a debrief after improves faster than one who only runs once a month and hopes.

**Building your routine:** one or two **daily** warm-ups (5–10 min, before you prep or play) + two or three **weekly** practices matched to where you're weak + the **debrief** every time you run. Say the spoken ones *out loud*; reading them silently does almost nothing.

---

## A. Daily warm-ups (5–10 min; the reflex builders)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **A1: Name on demand** | Roll the [name table](SUPPLEMENT-dm-toolkit.md#part-5-random-tables-the-improv-safety-net) or invent five NPC names out loud in 30 seconds. No hesitation allowed | killing the freeze when players ask "what's her name?" |
| **A2: Three-sentence room** | Pick any object near you (a lamp, a mug) and describe it as a fantastic location in three sentences (impression, feature, hook) | fast, evocative scene-framing |
| **A3: Snap ruling** | Invent a wild player action and, in 10 seconds, call the ability, DC, and stakes (success and failure) | adjudication speed (Day 2, Day 16) |
| **A4: NPC in 15 seconds** | Quick-build one NPC (name, look, want, attitude, voice tag) and say one line in their voice | running unplanned NPCs cold (Day 4) |
| **A5: Yes, and** | Take five "can I...?" player requests and answer each "yes, and/but ___" with stakes | keeping player agency alive (Day 19) |

## B. Narration and voice (the performance)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **B1: Result narration** | Take a flat outcome ("you fail," "you hit") and narrate it so the *situation changes* | the loop that never stalls (Day 15) |
| **B2: Voice tags** | Collect voice tags from real life (a coworker's cadence, a film character) and practice switching between three in a row | distinguishing NPCs at the table |
| **B3: Sensory pass** | Describe a scene using a sense other than sight (smell, sound, temperature) | immersion beyond "you see..." (Day 3) |
| **B4: The cut** | Take a boring stretch (travel, shopping, downtime) and narrate it into the next interesting moment in two sentences | pacing; cutting to the action (Day 15) |
| **B5: Strong start** | Write a session opening that drops players into a decision in the first 60 seconds | beginning sessions with momentum (Day 8, Day 15) |

## C. Adjudication reps (the referee)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **C1: To roll or not** | For ten actions, snap-call "auto-yes / auto-no / roll" before doing anything else | not drowning the game in rolls (Day 2) |
| **C2: DC ladder** | Take one task and set its DC at very easy through nearly impossible, justifying each shift | calibrated, defensible DCs |
| **C3: Stakes-first** | Rewrite weak prompts ("roll Perception") into stakes-first prompts (success/failure stated) | making every roll matter (Day 2) |
| **C4: Search vs. Study** | Sort ten player questions into Search (Wisdom: discern what isn't obvious) or Study (Intelligence: recall or reason through information) and the right check | clean use of the 2024 actions (Day 2) |
| **C5: Action economy** | For a given monster mix, count actions per side and predict the real difficulty vs. the XP budget | reading a fight beyond the math (Day 9) |

## D. Prep habits (the craft)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **D1: One-hour prep** | Prep a runnable session on the [one-page template](SUPPLEMENT-dm-toolkit.md#the-one-page-session-template) against a timer | low-prep that survives players (Day 8) |
| **D2: Clue audit** | For any mystery, check every conclusion has 3 clues and each clue is reachable two ways | mysteries that never dead-end (Day 10) |
| **D3: Reskin reps** | Take an NPC you need and name the existing stat block you'd reskin (Commoner, Guard, Bandit Captain, Knight, Cult Fanatic) | never building a stat block you don't need (Day 12) |
| **D4: Budget a fight** | Pick a party and difficulty, read the [XP budget](SUPPLEMENT-dm-toolkit.md#encounter-xp-budget-per-character), and fill it with monsters | fast, correct encounter math (Day 9) |
| **D5: Clock tick** | Advance each faction clock one step and write how the world visibly changes | a living, reactive world (Day 12, Day 18) |

## E. Table and campaign habits (over time)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **E1: The recap** | Open each session with a 60-second recap (better: have a player do it) | continuity and re-entry (Day 18) |
| **E2: The debrief** | After every session, write what flowed, what stalled, who got the spotlight, and your next strong start | the single fastest way to improve (Day 21) |
| **E3: Spotlight check** | Mid-session, mentally scan: who hasn't acted in a while? Turn to them | every player gets a moment (Day 15) |
| **E4: Loose-threads mining** | Keep a running list of dangling threads and NPCs; pull one forward each session (reincorporation) | a world that feels authored (Day 19) |
| **E5: Watch a DM** | Watch 20 minutes of an experienced DM running online; note one technique to steal | calibrating your eye to good play |

---

## Rules that make any practice work

1. **Say it out loud.** This is a spoken craft. Silent reading builds almost nothing; narrating to a recorder or an empty room builds the actual reflex. Every spoken drill, do spoken.
2. **Speed over polish in warm-ups.** The point of A1–A5 is to kill hesitation. A fast, mediocre answer beats a slow, perfect one; at the table you don't get the slow one.
3. **Debrief every session.** E2 is the highest-leverage habit in this whole menu. A DM who debriefs improves every single session; one who doesn't plateaus for years.
4. **Run, then drill, then run.** Drills sharpen skills you've used live. If you haven't run yet, run a short session (even solo); the reps mean more once you've felt where you stalled.
5. **Steal relentlessly.** Names, voices, scenes, structures, techniques from other DMs, books, films, and tables. Originality is overrated; reincorporation and good theft are the job.
