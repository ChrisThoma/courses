# Mastery Rubric: DM-101

Grade yourself in each domain. **Mastery = Level 4 in every domain.** Levels:

- **1 (Novice):** Aware of the concept; can't apply it at the table without stopping the game.
- **2 (Developing):** Can apply it slowly, with effort and frequent fumbles.
- **3 (Proficient):** Can apply it reliably with brief thought; the game keeps moving; rare errors.
- **4 (Master):** Instant, fair, and invisible. You do it without breaking the fiction, and you can teach it.

---

## Domain 1: The role and the loop

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Thinks the DM "controls the story"; unsure what to actually say first |
| 2 | Runs the describe → act → adjudicate → narrate loop haltingly; sometimes forgets to hand the spotlight back |
| 3 | Runs the loop smoothly; balances referee/narrator/fan; upholds the social contract |
| 4 | The loop is invisible; reads the table's energy and adjusts; is genuinely a fan of the characters and it shows |

**Test:** Describe a situation, prompt the players, take an action, adjudicate it, and narrate the result, in one clean pass, without telling anyone what to do.

## Domain 2: Adjudication (the d20)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Calls for a roll for everything, or for nothing; can't set a DC |
| 2 | Knows checks/saves/attacks exist; hunts for the right DC and stalls |
| 3 | Decides quickly whether a roll is needed, sets a defensible DC, applies advantage/passive correctly |
| 4 | "Say yes or roll the dice" is reflex; sets stakes before the roll; runs the 2024 actions cold without slowing play |

**Test:** A player says "I want to swing across on the chandelier." In ten seconds, decide whether to roll, name the ability and a DC, and state what happens on a success and on a failure.

## Domain 3: Running exploration

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Reads boxed text and waits; scenes drag or get skipped |
| 2 | Describes a space but forgets time, light, or the senses; pacing lurches |
| 3 | Frames scenes with a clear hook, manages time and travel, describes economically, runs traps/hazards fairly |
| 4 | Paces exploration like a movie: every scene earns its place, the world feels alive, dead air never happens |

**Test:** Drop the party into a new location cold and run two minutes of exploration that establishes the space, gives them something to do, and ends pointed at a decision.

## Domain 4: Social interaction and NPCs

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | NPCs are all "the DM"; gives away information or stonewalls at random |
| 2 | Voices a few NPCs; reacts inconsistently; resolves talking with one die roll |
| 3 | Gives NPCs a want and an attitude, runs the 2024 Influence action and attitudes, roleplays then rolls |
| 4 | Conjures a distinct, motivated NPC on the spot; social scenes have stakes and turn on choices, not just dice |

**Test:** Improvise a named NPC with a voice, a want, and an attitude, and run a 90-second negotiation that the players could win or lose.

## Domain 5: Running combat

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Loses track of turns; monsters stand still and full-attack; combat is a slow slog |
| 2 | Runs the action economy but forgets reactions, conditions, or monster tactics |
| 3 | Runs initiative cleanly, uses the 2024 conditions/exhaustion/weapon mastery correctly, gives monsters simple tactics |
| 4 | Combat is fast and cinematic: terrain and objectives matter, monsters play smart, and turns never drag |

**Test:** Run one round of a three-monster fight from memory: roll initiative, take a monster turn with a tactic and a condition or mastery in play, and keep each player's turn under a minute.

## Domain 6: Prep and adventure design

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Either over-prepares a novel nobody reads, or shows up cold |
| 2 | Preps scenes but no strong start, no clues, or a single railroaded path |
| 3 | Preps a session in ~an hour: strong start, a handful of scenes, secrets/clues, fantastic locations, only what's used |
| 4 | Preps situations, not plots; the prep survives contact with players; reuses and recombines material effortlessly |

**Test:** In 60 minutes, prep a runnable session: a strong start, 3–5 scenes, five secrets/clues, two NPCs, and a reward, on one page.

## Domain 7: Encounter building and the reward economy

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Throws monsters at the party and hopes; treasure is random or forgotten |
| 2 | Uses the XP budget mechanically; fights are accidentally trivial or lethal; rewards are an afterthought |
| 3 | Builds Low/Moderate/High encounters with the 2024 budget, accounts for action economy, gives tier-appropriate treasure and magic items |
| 4 | Tunes difficulty to the table on the fly, designs encounters with objectives and terrain, runs treasure/bastions/downtime as a deliberate economy |

**Test:** Build a Moderate encounter for a named party with the 2024 XP budget, then say how you'd make it harder or easier mid-fight without cheating, and name an appropriate reward.

## Domain 8: Worldbuilding and factions

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Either no setting, or a 40-page bible the players never touch |
| 2 | Has a setting but it's static scenery; factions are names with no agenda |
| 3 | Builds a home base and a few factions with wants and clocks; builds top-down/bottom-up as needed; only what's used |
| 4 | The world feels lived-in and reactive; factions pursue goals offscreen; lore exists to create play, not to be admired |

**Test:** Pitch a campaign premise in three sentences and name three factions, each with a want, a method, and the next thing it will do whether or not the party intervenes.

## Domain 9: Running live (pacing, spotlight, rulings, improv)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Freezes when players go off-script; stops the game to look up rules |
| 2 | Keeps going but the spotlight clumps on one player; rulings are inconsistent |
| 3 | Paces a session, shares the spotlight, makes a fair ruling and moves on, improvises a scene when needed |
| 4 | The seams never show: rulings feel fair and consistent, every player gets their moment, and off-script is just more game |

**Test:** Given a player action that ignores your entire prep, make a ruling, improvise the next scene, and reincorporate something already established, in under a minute of thinking.

## Domain 10: The campaign over time

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Runs disconnected one-shots; no throughline; loses momentum after a few sessions |
| 2 | Has an arc but it's a railroad; leveling and threats feel arbitrary |
| 3 | Runs an arc with milestone or XP leveling, recurring villains, clocks/fronts, and session recaps |
| 4 | Sustains a campaign players show up for: threats escalate believably, payoffs land, and the world changes because of the party |

**Test:** Sketch a three-tier campaign arc with a leveling plan, one recurring villain with an advancing clock, and how you'll open and close a typical session.
