# DM-101: Dungeon Mastering (Running D&D 5e, 2024 Rules)

**Format:** Self-paced intensive, modeled on a college-style craft course
**Time commitment:** ~60–90 min/day, 6 days/week, 3 weeks (one rest/review day per week), plus one longer session at the end to actually run a game
**Prerequisites:** None. You do not need to have played D&D, and you do not need to own all three core books to start (the free 2024 rules, this course, and the linked references cover what you need to begin; see the Field Guide). You do need a willingness to talk out loud, make decisions on the fly, and be wrong in front of friends.

> **The promise of this course:** in three weeks you will go from "I've never run a game" to sitting behind the screen and running a real session of your own Dungeons & Dragons, on the 2024 ruleset, with players who are having a good time. You will know what your job actually is, how to prep in an hour instead of ten, how to make a fair ruling in two seconds, how to build an encounter that isn't a slog or a slaughter, and how to keep a campaign alive past session three (where most of them quietly die). You will finish with a prepped, playtested first session and a campaign framework you can keep running for a year.

## What this course is (and isn't)

This course teaches you to **build and run a Dungeons & Dragons campaign**, and, more importantly, the *judgment* behind every choice, so you can handle the table you actually get rather than the one a book imagines. It is built on the **2024 revision of 5th Edition** (the Player's Handbook, Dungeon Master's Guide, and Monster Manual released September 2024 through February 2025), and it flags where 2024 differs from the 2014 rules many older guides still teach. You will learn:

- **The DM's real job:** referee, narrator, and the players' biggest fan, running the core gameplay loop that every session is made of.
- **Adjudication:** how to handle any action a player throws at you with the d20, ability checks, saving throws, DCs, advantage/disadvantage, and the new 2024 actions (Influence, Study, Search, Utilize, Magic).
- **The three pillars:** running exploration, social interaction, and combat so each one is fun and fast, including the 2024 changes to conditions, exhaustion, and weapon mastery.
- **The craft of prep:** low-prep adventure design, the 2024 encounter-building math, building only the world you'll use, NPCs and factions you can run cold, and the reward economy (treasure, magic items, bastions, downtime).
- **Running it live:** pacing a session, spotlight management, making rulings instead of looking up rules, improvising when the players go off the map, and steering a campaign across an arc and many sessions.

It deliberately **omits** things a new DM does not need on day one: encyclopedic monster and spell knowledge (you look those up), heavy homebrew of subsystems and custom classes (learn the defaults first, break them later), published-adventure-by-adventure walkthroughs, and deep lore of any one official setting. Those doors are real and worth opening; this course gets you running confidently so that when you open them, you know what you're changing and why.

## A note on the table (important: read this)

You can take this course **without a group lined up.** Most of the work (prep, design, narration practice, rules adjudication) is solo, and the understanding installs the same whether or not your friends are free this week. Throughout the lessons, tasks that genuinely require live players are marked **[needs a table]**, and every one of them comes with a **solo version** you can do today: run both sides yourself, narrate to an empty room or a recorder, or rope in a single willing friend. The capstone (Days 20–21) is the one place you will want real players; start recruiting them in Week 1 (the Field Guide and Day 17 tell you how). If the worst happens and nobody is free, you can run the capstone solo or with one person and still pass. Running for real people is the only thing that builds the reflexes, so get to a table as soon as you can.

## How to use this course

1. Read `SYLLABUS.md`, then `RUBRIC.md` so you know what mastery looks like, then **Parts 1–2 of the Field Guide** so the quick-reference is in your head before Day 1.
2. Each day, work the lesson in order: **Lecture → Examples → Lab (do it) → Self-check.**
3. **Always with a real game in mind.** Knowledge you never run is trivia. Wherever you can, narrate out loud, make the ruling, write the prep, or run the scene. Where you need players you don't have, do the solo version and bank the rest.
4. Take quizzes closed-book. Grade yourself honestly against `ANSWER-KEYS.md` and the rubric. A DM who can't adjudicate a check cold will stall every five minutes at the table.
5. Do the projects. They are not busywork: they are your actual campaign, built one playable piece at a time. By Day 21 you assemble them and run the thing.

## A note on pace

Three weeks is enough to *understand* everything here and to produce and run a real first session with a real campaign behind it. Mastery (the kind where a player does something you never imagined and you just smile and make the call) comes from the recurring practices in `DRILLS.md` carried across many sessions. A campaign is the only final exam that matters, and it never really ends. Start the drills now; keep running. The game rewards the DM who keeps showing up behind the screen.
