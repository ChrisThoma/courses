# Mastery Rubric: MUS-21G

Grade yourself in each domain. **Mastery = Level 4 in every domain.** Levels:

- **1 (Novice):** Aware of the concept, can't apply it without references.
- **2 (Developing):** Can apply it slowly with effort, frequent errors.
- **3 (Proficient):** Can apply it reliably with brief thought; rare errors.
- **4 (Master):** Instant, accurate, and can teach it / apply it in real musical contexts.

---

## Domain 1: Fretboard fluency

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Knows open-string names; finds notes by counting frets from the nut |
| 2 | Knows notes on strings 6 and 5; finds others via octave shapes with pauses |
| 3 | Names any note on any string within ~5 seconds; knows all natural notes cold |
| 4 | Names any note (incl. sharps/flats) anywhere within ~3 seconds; instantly finds all locations of a given note across the neck |

**Test:** Random note + string called out → point to the fret. 20 trials, 18+ correct in under 3 s each.

## Domain 2: Intervals

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Knows half/whole steps; can count semitones slowly |
| 2 | Can name intervals between natural notes with counting |
| 3 | Names any interval between two notes; knows fretboard shapes for all intervals within an octave |
| 4 | Instantly names/plays any interval from any note, ascending or descending; recognizes m2–P8 by ear (melodic) |

**Test:** (a) "Major 6th above E?": answer in 3 s. (b) Play 10 random intervals; identify 9+ by ear.

## Domain 3: Scales and keys

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Can recite W-W-H-W-W-W-H; plays major scale in one position |
| 2 | Spells major scales in common keys; plays 2 positions; knows the circle of fifths exists |
| 3 | Spells any major or natural minor scale in any key; knows key signatures via circle of fifths; plays 3+ positions; knows relative major/minor pairs |
| 4 | Spells all major, natural/harmonic/melodic minor, both pentatonics, blues scale, and all modes from any root; plays them across the whole neck; identifies a song's key from its chords |

**Test:** "Spell F# harmonic minor": answer in 15 s. "Key with 3 flats?": instant.

## Domain 4: Chord construction

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Plays open chords; knows chords "come from scales" |
| 2 | Knows triad formulas (1-3-5 etc.); can spell major/minor triads with effort |
| 3 | Spells all four triad types and all common 7th chords from any root; can find a voicing for any spelled chord |
| 4 | Spells any chord through 13ths incl. sus/add/slash; plays 2+ voicings anywhere on the neck; identifies a chord's quality from its notes; hears maj/min/dim/dom7/maj7/min7 quality |

**Test:** "Spell Ab maj7. Now play it two ways, one above the 5th fret." (Under 20 s total.)

## Domain 5: Diatonic harmony & progressions

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Has heard of I-IV-V |
| 2 | Can list the diatonic chords of C major; understands Roman numerals |
| 3 | Names the diatonic triads and 7ths of any major or minor key; analyzes a simple progression by Roman numeral; knows the common cadences |
| 4 | Analyzes real songs incl. borrowed chords, secondary dominants, and key changes; transposes any progression instantly; predicts "what chord wants to come next" and can explain why |

**Test:** Given a 6-chord progression from a real song, identify the key and Roman numerals in under 2 minutes.

## Domain 6: Ear training

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Hears "happy vs. sad" (major vs. minor) |
| 2 | Recognizes a few intervals via song associations |
| 3 | Identifies all intervals within an octave (melodic); major/minor/dim triads; hears I, IV, V movement |
| 4 | Identifies 7th-chord qualities; transcribes a simple chord progression (diatonic, 4 chords) by ear; sings any requested interval |

**Test:** Transcribe the progression of a simple pop song chorus by ear; verify against published chords.

## Domain 7: Applied musicianship (the point of all of it)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Plays memorized songs without knowing why they work |
| 2 | Can write a progression by trial and error |
| 3 | Writes progressions with intent; improvises with the correct scale over a given key |
| 4 | Composes progressions + melodies using diatonic and non-diatonic tools deliberately; targets chord tones when improvising; can re-harmonize a melody; explains every choice in theory terms |

**Test:** The Final Project (see `PROJECTS.md`), scored against its own rubric.
