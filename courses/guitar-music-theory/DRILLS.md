# Drill Menu — MUS-21G

The lessons prescribe a default 10-minute daily drill, but no single drill fits everyone every day. This is the full menu: **pick one drill per active skill, rotate every few days** so nothing goes stale. Variety isn't a luxury here — interleaving and randomness are what turn slow derivation into instant recall.

**Building your 10 minutes:** 3–4 min from Skill A (fretboard) + 2–3 min from Skill B (intervals) + 2–3 min from whichever of C/D/E matches the current week + 1–2 min of F (ear) every single day.

---

## A. Fretboard note-finding (start Day 1, never stop)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **A1 — Paper-scrap lottery** | 12 note names in a hat (incl. sharps/flats); draw one, find it on a named string in ≤3 s | the default; true randomness |
| **A2 — One-note, whole neck** | Pick one note; find it on all six strings via octave shapes, ascending then descending. New note tomorrow | connecting strings; Week 1 |
| **A3 — Fret freeze** | Pick one fret (say, 7); name the note on every string at that fret, low to high | vertical knowledge; reveals B-string blind spots |
| **A4 — Metronome ladder** | Metronome at 40: one note named + found per click. Raise 5 bpm when you go a full minute clean | converting accuracy into speed |
| **A5 — Dark drill** | Eyes closed: finger a fret by feel using the dot markers, *then* name it, then look | stage-ready recall; advanced |

## B. Intervals (start Day 2)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **B1 — Interval math sprint** | Two hats: 12 roots, 12 intervals. Draw one of each, name the resulting note aloud, verify on the neck | the paper skill |
| **B2 — Shape reps** | Random root on string 6/5, random interval: *play* it. Score B-string crossings double | the fretboard skill |
| **B3 — Sing-then-play** | Play a root, name an interval, **sing** the target pitch, then play to check | ties intervals to your ear — highest payoff per minute |
| **B4 — Reverse engineer** | Play any two random frets; name the interval between them | the inverse skill; keeps you honest |

## C. Scales & keys (start Day 3)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **C1 — Key recitation** | Random key → say its signature, spell its scale, name its relative minor. Target: 15 s | Week 1–2 default |
| **C2 — Position roulette** | Random key + random position (root-6 / root-5 / one-string): play the major scale, degrees aloud | fretboard transfer |
| **C3 — Circle lap** | Play one octave of every major scale around the circle of fifths (C, G, D, …), one position each, no stopping | endurance + circle fluency |
| **C4 — Scale surgery** (Week 3) | Random root: spell natural minor, then *say only the changed notes* for harmonic, melodic, Dorian, Mixolydian | parallel-mode thinking |

## D. Chords & harmony (start Day 8)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **D1 — Spelling bee** | Random root + random quality (maj, m, dim, aug, maj7, 7, m7, m7b5): spell it in ≤10 s | the construction skill |
| **D2 — Diatonic recitation** | Random key: recite all seven diatonic chords with numerals in ≤15 s | Week 2 default |
| **D3 — CAGED roulette** | Random chord + random shape letter: play it | neck coverage |
| **D4 — Numeral flash** | Random key + random numeral ("key of E, play the vi"): play any voicing in ≤5 s | the gigging skill — how musicians actually call chords |
| **D5 — One-progression passport** | Take one progression (e.g., I–V–vi–IV); play it in 3 random keys in 3 different neck regions | transposition under pressure |

## E. Progressions & analysis (start Day 11)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **E1 — Radio analyst** | Any song that's playing: find the key on your low E string, guess the numerals in real time. No pausing allowed | the lifetime habit |
| **E2 — Cadence improv** | Random cadence type drawn from a hat: improvise a 4-bar progression ending with it | composition reflexes |
| **E3 — Numeral dictation** | Write 8 random numeral strings on cards (incl. one borrowed chord or V7/x after Day 19); draw and play | reading the language fluently |

## F. Ear training (start Day 2 — every day, no exceptions)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **F1 — Self-played intervals** | Shuffle interval cards; play each from a fixed root, eyes closed, identify before looking | no equipment needed |
| **F2 — App reps** | Any ear-training app (interval + chord-quality modes), 20 questions | true randomness; trackable scores |
| **F3 — Sing-back** | Play a random note; sing a named interval above it; check. (B3 in reverse) | production, not just recognition — the stronger skill |
| **F4 — Quality ladder** (Week 2+) | One root, all qualities (maj→m→dim→aug, then the 7ths) in random order, eyes closed | chord-quality hearing |
| **F5 — Degree drone** (Week 3) | Loop a drone; play random scale tones; name the degree by feel | melody transcription engine |
| **F6 — One-song transcription** (post-course) | One simple song per week, by ear, verified after | the endgame |

---

## Rules that make any drill work

1. **Random beats sequential.** Going around the circle in order trains the *order*, not the *knowledge*. Hats, cards, dice, or a shuffled list.
2. **Speed pressure, gently.** A metronome or a 3-second rule turns "I can work it out" into "I know it." Add pressure only after accuracy is ~100%.
3. **Log one number per drill** (score or bpm). The line going up is the motivation system.
4. **When a drill gets easy, retire it** and pull the next one from the menu. Easy drills are entertainment, not practice.
