# Quizzes and Final Exam — MUS-21G

Rules: **closed book, guitar allowed only where a question says "play."** Write answers on paper, then grade with `ANSWER-KEYS.md`. Passing = 90%.

---

## Quiz 1 — Foundations *(Day 6 · 40 points · ~45 min)*

### Part A — The chromatic system (8 pts, 1 each)
1. List the 12 notes ascending from E (sharps).
2. Which two pairs of natural notes have no note between them?
3. Enharmonic equivalent of G#? Of Cb?
4. How many semitones in one octave?
5. What note is 3 semitones above B?
6. What note is 5 semitones below C?
7. True/false: fret 12 of any string is the same letter as the open string.
8. How are adjacent guitar strings tuned, and which pair is the exception?

### Part B — Fretboard (8 pts, 1 each)
Name the note:
1. String 6, fret 8   2. String 5, fret 10   3. String 6, fret 6   4. String 5, fret 4
5. String 4, fret 5   6. String 3, fret 7   7. String 2, fret 8   8. String 1, fret 10

### Part C — Intervals (10 pts, 1 each)
1. Semitones in a P4? 2. In a M6? 3. In a tritone?
4. M3 above A? 5. m3 above F#? 6. P5 above B? 7. m7 above E? 8. M2 below C?
9. Name the interval C→Ab. 10. Name the interval E→C#.

### Part D — Major scale & keys (10 pts, 1 each)
1. The major scale step formula?
2. Spell D major. 3. Spell Bb major. 4. Spell B major.
5. Key signature of E major? 6. Key with 3 flats?
7. The order sharps are added?
8. A key signature shows F#, C#, G#, D# — what major key?
9. Which scale degree is the leading tone?
10. Why does F major use Bb instead of A#?

### Part E — Played (4 pts) *(guitar allowed)*
1. (2) Play a one-octave major scale from the note C# (any string/position), naming degrees aloud.
2. (2) Play these intervals from G (string 6, fret 3), cross-string shapes: M3, P5, octave.

## Quiz 2 — Harmony *(Day 13 · 40 points · ~45 min)*

### Part A — Triads (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Formula for a major triad? For diminished?
2. Spell: 3. F minor 4. A major 5. C# diminished 6. Eb major 7. G augmented
8. What single note change turns D major into D minor?

### Part B — Diatonic harmony (10 pts, 1 each)
1. The diatonic triad quality pattern of a major key (I through vii°)?
2–4. All seven diatonic triads (names + numerals) of: D major · F major · A major
5. In any major key, which chords have dominant function?
6. Why does V pull toward I? (One sentence; mention the leading tone.)
7. A song uses G, C, D, Em and feels resolved on G. Key and numerals?
8. Transpose C–Am–F–G to the key of E.
9. Which diatonic chord is diminished, and on what degree?
10. Two diatonic chords that can substitute for I (tonic function)?

### Part C — Seventh chords (10 pts, 1 each)
1. "A7" — spell it, and name its quality precisely.
2. Spell: 3. Dmaj7 4. F#m7 5. Bm7b5 6. Ebmaj7 7. C#7
8. The diatonic 7th-chord pattern of a major key?
9. On which degree does the dominant 7th occur naturally?
10. Which two notes in C7 form a tritone?

### Part D — Progressions & CAGED (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Define authentic cadence. 2. Define deceptive cadence.
3. Roman numerals of the '50s progression?
4. Write a ii–V–I in Bb (chord names).
5. What does CAGED stand for / claim?
6. In the E shape, which string holds the root?
7. Name the fret where you'd play C major as an A-shape barre.
8. Why does C→Em sound smooth? (Hint: shared notes.)

### Part E — Played (4 pts) *(guitar allowed)*
1. (2) Play Dm7–G7–Cmaj7 with movable grips, naming each chord's quality.
2. (2) Play one chord (your choice) in three different CAGED shapes, announcing each shape.

## Final Exam *(Day 21 · 60 points · ~60 min)*

### Part A — Speed fundamentals (10 pts, 1 each — answer each in ≤10 seconds)
1. Note at string 5, fret 6? 2. M6 above D? 3. Key with 4 sharps?
4. Relative minor of Bb major? 5. Spell E7. 6. Semitones in m6?
7. Diatonic vii chord of A major? 8. P4 above F#? 9. Spell G harmonic minor (just the altered note vs natural minor is fine — or all 7). 10. Name the interval F→D.

### Part B — Minor keys (10 pts, 1 each)
1. Natural minor formula in degrees?
2. Spell C natural minor. 3. Spell F# natural minor. 4. Spell A melodic minor (ascending).
5. What note differs between E natural and E harmonic minor?
6. Why was harmonic minor invented?
7. Diatonic triads of B minor (natural)?
8. Relative major of G minor? Parallel major of G minor — and what's the difference between those two questions?
9. In A minor, what chord is bVII?
10. The classic minor cadence uses which non-natural-minor chord?

### Part C — Pentatonics, blues, modes (12 pts, 1 each)
1. Minor pentatonic formula? 2. Major pentatonic formula?
3. G major pentatonic = which minor pentatonic?
4. The blues scale adds which degree?
5. Write the 12-bar blues form in Roman numerals.
6. What's theoretically "wrong" (and stylistically right) about blues harmony? (One sentence.)
7. Dorian's formula — and its character note?
8. Mixolydian is the major scale with what alteration?
9. D Dorian comes from which major scale?
10. Spell A Mixolydian.
11. What establishes a mode — your starting note, or the harmony underneath?
12. Which mode fits an Am7–D7 two-chord vamp?

### Part D — Advanced harmony (12 pts, 1 each)
1. Csus4 — spell it. What's suspended?
2. Difference between Csus2 and Cadd9?
3. C/E — what is it, in Day-8 vocabulary?
4. Spell G13 — then name the notes you'd actually keep on guitar.
5. Borrowed chords come from where?
6. Name three common borrowed chords in a major key.
7. What is V7/V in the key of C (chord name)?
8. Recipe for a secondary dominant targeting any chord x?
9. In C major: C–C7–F–Fm–C. Label every chord with numerals/function.
10. IV→iv→I — why does this progression ache? (Which note moves, and how?)
11. A song in G uses an F major chord. Two plausible theory explanations?
12. Which two chord tones define an extended chord's identity?

### Part E — Analysis (8 pts)
Given: a song's verse is **Em–C–G–D**, repeated; the chorus is **G–D–Em–C**; the bridge ends **Am–D7–G**.
1. (2) What key, and what's the evidence?
2. (2) Numerals for verse and chorus.
3. (2) What's the relationship between the verse and chorus progressions?
4. (2) Analyze the bridge ending — what device and cadence is Am–D7–G?

### Part F — Played (8 pts) *(guitar allowed)*
1. (2) Play a 12-bar blues in E (comping), counting bars aloud.
2. (2) Play A minor pentatonic Box 1 and one adjacent box, connecting them in a phrase.
3. (2) Play I–V7/vi–vi–IV in G (work out the chords first, then play).
4. (2) Play any major scale, then its parallel Mixolydian, over a recorded/looped drone of the root — make the b7 audible and deliberate.
