# Mastery Rubric: PHOT-10

Grade yourself in each domain. **Mastery = Level 4 in every domain.** Levels:

- **1 (Novice):** Aware of the concept, can't apply it without references or luck.
- **2 (Developing):** Can apply it slowly with effort and frequent errors.
- **3 (Proficient):** Can apply it reliably with brief thought; rare errors.
- **4 (Master):** Instant, accurate, and can teach it / apply it under real shooting pressure.

---

## Domain 1: Exposure control

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Knows the camera has aperture/shutter/ISO settings; relies on Auto |
| 2 | Can get a roughly correct exposure in good light using a semi-auto mode |
| 3 | Sets exposure deliberately in A, S, and M; uses exposure compensation; reads the histogram |
| 4 | Walks into any light and dials a correct, *intentional* exposure in seconds in Manual; uses the histogram to expose to the right when it helps; recovers from tricky light (backlight, snow, stage) without thinking |

**Test:** Given five varied scenes, set full Manual exposure for each in under 30 s, justify each setting, and land a usable histogram.

## Domain 2: Aperture & depth of field

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Knows "lower f-number = blurrier background" |
| 2 | Can choose a wide or narrow aperture for an obvious case |
| 3 | Predicts depth of field from aperture, focus distance, and focal length; chooses aperture for a desired look |
| 4 | Controls the plane of focus precisely; uses hyperfocal thinking for landscapes; balances DoF against shutter/ISO consequences instinctively |

**Test:** Produce three portraits of the same subject at f/2, f/4, f/8 and explain what changed and why you'd pick each.

## Domain 3: Shutter & motion

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Knows fast shutter = freeze, slow = blur |
| 2 | Avoids camera shake in good light most of the time |
| 3 | Chooses shutter speed to freeze or convey motion on purpose; knows the hand-hold rule and uses it |
| 4 | Executes deliberate motion blur, panning, and long exposure; instantly picks a shutter speed for any moving subject |

**Test:** Deliver a frozen-action frame, a motion-blur frame, and a clean panning shot, each on purpose.

## Domain 4: Focus

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Half-press and hope |
| 2 | Gets the subject sharp in easy, static scenes |
| 3 | Chooses AF area and mode for the situation; nails focus on eyes for portraits; uses back-button or focus-and-recompose deliberately |
| 4 | Tracks moving subjects in continuous AF; switches focus strategy by genre without fumbling; uses manual focus + focus peaking when AF fails |

**Test:** 20 frames across static and moving subjects; 18+ critically sharp on the intended point.

## Domain 5: Reading & using light

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Notices "bright" vs. "dark" |
| 2 | Knows to avoid harsh midday sun on faces |
| 3 | Identifies the direction, hardness, and color of light in a scene and positions subject/self to use it |
| 4 | Predicts how a scene's light will read as a photo before shooting; finds and shapes good light anywhere, any time of day |

**Test:** In one location, make the *same* subject look three distinctly different ways using only light position and time.

## Domain 6: Composition

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Centers everything; subject sometimes cut off |
| 2 | Applies rule of thirds when reminded |
| 3 | Composes with intent: clear subject, controlled background, deliberate use of line/balance/framing; explains choices |
| 4 | Composes fluidly and breaks "rules" knowingly for effect; controls the whole frame edge-to-edge; achieves depth and visual flow consistently |

**Test:** Given a fixed subject, make five genuinely different strong compositions and articulate the idea behind each.

## Domain 7: Color & white balance

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Leaves WB on Auto, never thinks about it |
| 2 | Knows the named WB presets exist |
| 3 | Sets WB by scene; understands the Kelvin scale; reads a color cast and corrects it |
| 4 | Uses WB creatively (warm/cool for mood), nails color in mixed light, and corrects color confidently in editing |

**Test:** Correct a mixed-light frame to neutral, then make a deliberately warm and a deliberately cool version, and defend each.

## Domain 8: Editing & workflow (the digital darkroom)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Uses straight-out-of-camera JPEGs or random filters |
| 2 | Can brighten/crop a photo with no real method |
| 3 | Has a repeatable RAW workflow: import → cull → tone → color → local → sharpen → export; edits don't look overdone |
| 4 | Edits to a clear intent, uses local adjustments and the tone curve fluently, maintains a sane catalog, and knows exactly when to stop |

**Test:** Take one RAW file from import to a finished export and write the 7-step recipe you used.

## Domain 9: Seeing & voice (the point of all of it)

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1 | Takes snapshots of whatever is in front of the camera |
| 2 | Occasionally makes a photo they're proud of, can't say why |
| 3 | Makes photographs with clear intent; can critique a photo in the craft's vocabulary |
| 4 | Produces a coherent body of work with a recognizable point of view; reads any photograph critically and applies that reading to their own |

**Test:** The Final Portfolio (see `PROJECTS.md`), scored against its own rubric.
