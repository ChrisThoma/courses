# Quizzes and Final Exam: PHOT-10

Rules: **closed book, camera allowed only where a question says "shoot/play."** Write answers on paper, then grade with `ANSWER-KEYS.md`. Passing = 90%.

---

## Quiz 1: The Camera & Exposure *(Day 6 · 40 points · ~45 min)*

### Part A: How a camera sees (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Name the three controls that determine exposure.
2. What does the aperture physically change inside the lens?
3. What does "clipping the highlights" mean?
4. Which exposure error is unrecoverable even in RAW: over- or under-exposure of the highlights?
5. Name the side effect (the *other* thing it controls) tied to aperture.
6. …to shutter speed.
7. …to ISO.
8. What is "a stop"?

### Part B: Aperture & shutter (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Which is a bigger opening, f/2.8 or f/8?
2. Moving from f/4 to f/8: how many stops, and more or less light?
3. Name the three things that control depth of field.
4. You want a creamy blurred background: open up or stop down?
5. Around which apertures is a typical lens sharpest?
6. Does 1/1000 let in more or less light than 1/125, and by how many stops?
7. What shutter speed roughly freezes a walking person?
8. The hand-hold rule for a 35mm-equivalent lens gives roughly what minimum shutter speed?

### Part C: ISO & the triangle (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What does raising ISO cost you?
2. State the rule for choosing ISO.
3. True/false: underexposing and brightening later is cleaner than just using a higher ISO.
4. You're at f/5.6, 1/250, ISO 400 and open one stop to f/4. What shutter speed keeps the same brightness?
5. From the same starting exposure, how do you keep brightness equal if you *raise* ISO one stop: name one compensating move.
6. Which control do you decide *first*, and based on what?
7. Define reciprocity in one sentence.
8. What does Auto-ISO with a minimum-shutter-speed setting buy you?

### Part D: Metering, histogram, modes, focus (12 pts, 1 each)
1. What average brightness does the camera's meter assume the scene is?
2. Which way (+ or −) do you compensate for a snowy/white scene, and why?
3. Which way for a mostly-black scene?
4. A backlit subject becomes a silhouette: name a fix.
5. What does a spike jammed against the *right* edge of the histogram indicate?
6. Is there one "correct" histogram shape? Explain in one line.
7. In Aperture-priority, what does the camera set for you?
8. In Shutter-priority, what do you set?
9. AF-S or AF-C for a child running toward you?
10. For a shallow-DoF portrait, where exactly do you focus?
11. What does back-button focus separate from each other?
12. Your handheld frames are *evenly* soft across the whole image: focus error or camera shake?

### Part E: Shot (4 pts) *(camera allowed)*
1. (2) Set a correct **Manual** exposure for the room you're in; state your f-stop, shutter, ISO and justify each in one phrase.
2. (2) Without changing brightness, give a *second* correct exposure of the same scene that yields a shallower depth of field; state what you traded.

## Quiz 2: Light & Composition *(Day 13 · 40 points · ~45 min)*

### Part A: Qualities of light (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Which light direction best reveals texture and form?
2. What physical property of a source determines whether light is hard or soft?
3. How does an overcast sky turn harsh sun into soft light?
4. Why is overhead noon sun unflattering for faces?
5. Name two ways to make a small, hard light source softer.
6. What does back light create (name one effect)?
7. Front light is even but has what weakness?
8. Define "contrast" as a quality of light, in one line.

### Part B: Color & white balance (8 pts, 1 each)
1. Low Kelvin = warm or cool color?
2. Roughly what Kelvin is midday sun?
3. What does white balance do?
4. You leave WB on "Tungsten" but shoot outdoors at noon: what color cast results?
5. Why does shooting RAW make in-camera WB far less critical?
6. Name one *creative* (non-corrective) use of white balance.
7. Which WB approach gives perfect neutrality off a reference?
8. Why is mixed light (warm lamp + cool window) hard to white-balance?

### Part C: Composition (12 pts, 1 each)
1. What's the first question to ask before any frame?
2. What habit does the rule of thirds cure?
3. Name two uses of negative space.
4. Name two ways to fix a cluttered background.
5. Capa's advice in four words?
6. What do leading lines do, and where should they point?
7. Name three techniques for creating depth in a 2D photo.
8. A break in a pattern becomes what?
9. How does a low camera angle make a subject feel?
10. Why is standing eye-level usually the weakest viewpoint?
11. What is compositional "framing"?
12. Name one thing to always check at the four edges of the frame.

### Part D: Lenses & light playbook (8 pts, 1 each)
1. What actually determines perspective: the lens, or your distance?
2. Why do short-telephoto lenses flatter faces?
3. Why does an ultra-wide distort a close-up face?
4. What is the 35mm-equivalent focal length especially good for?
5. Name the three reasons golden-hour light is so flattering.
6. Your only option is harsh midday sun: name two strategies.
7. The correct order to fight low light with your three controls?
8. What does fill light do, and name one cheap way to add it?

### Part E: Shot (4 pts) *(camera allowed)*
1. (2) Find soft, directional light and place a subject in it; shoot it side-lit, then move so it's back-lit. State what changed.
2. (2) Take one frame that uses a leading line *and* a clear foreground/midground/background; name each element.

## Final Exam *(Day 21 · 60 points · ~60 min)*

### Part A: Speed fundamentals (10 pts, 1 each; answer each in ≤15 s)
1. Bigger opening: f/2 or f/5.6? 2. Stops between 1/60 and 1/500? 3. Raising ISO costs what? 4. Slider to rescue a blown sky? 5. AF mode for a running dog? 6. Low Kelvin = warm or cool? 7. The hand-hold rule for 35mm-equiv? 8. Where to focus for a shallow portrait? 9. Which histogram edge spike = lost highlights? 10. The "sweet spot" aperture range for sharpness?

### Part B: Exposure & reciprocity (10 pts)
1. (1) State the three legs of the exposure triangle and the side effect of each.
2. (2) A scene is correct at f/8, 1/250, ISO 200. Give a correct exposure with *one more stop of depth of field* (deeper DoF), compensating on another control. State the trade.
3. (2) Same baseline: give a correct exposure that *freezes faster motion*, compensating. State the trade.
4. (1) Why expose to the right (ETTR), and what's the one thing you must not do while doing it?
5. (2) For each, state the exposure-comp direction and rough amount: (a) snowy field, (b) black dog on a dark couch, (c) subject backlit by a bright window.
6. (2) Define a "stop," and explain why one stop on aperture, shutter, or ISO are interchangeable for brightness.

### Part C: Light & color (10 pts, 1 each)
1. Property of a source that makes its light hard vs. soft?
2. Best light direction for revealing texture?
3. Three reasons golden hour flatters.
4. Two strategies for harsh midday sun.
5. Why are overcast days good for portraits?
6. Roughly what Kelvin is open shade, and is it warm or cool?
7. What does white balance correct?
8. One creative use of WB.
9. What does a reflector (or white wall) do for harsh side light?
10. Why is window light (no direct sun) such prized portrait light?

### Part D: Composition & lenses (10 pts, 1 each)
1. The first question before any frame?
2. The rule of thirds cures what default?
3. Two uses of negative space.
4. Two fixes for a cluttered background.
5. Where should leading lines point?
6. Three ways to create depth.
7. What actually creates perspective?
8. Why do telephotos flatter faces and wides distort them (one sentence)?
9. What does "zoom with your feet" force you to do?
10. How does a low angle change a subject's feel?

### Part E: Workflow & critique (12 pts, 1 each)
1. Name two things RAW recovers/changes that JPEG largely can't.
2. The 3-2-1 backup rule?
3. State the 7-step edit order.
4. Which tonal slider rescues a too-bright sky?
5. What does an S-curve do?
6. Vibrance vs. Saturation: which protects skin, and why?
7. Name two local adjustments that direct the viewer's eye.
8. Why apply sharpening at 100% and with masking?
9. Color space and rough long-edge size for a web export?
10. The most common beginner *editing* mistake?
11. List any four of the six critical-reading questions.
12. In one sentence, what *is* a photographer's "style"?

### Part F: Shot/Applied (8 pts) *(camera + editor allowed)*
1. (2) Set full Manual for your current light, justify all three settings, and land a histogram with no clipped highlights.
2. (2) Make the *same* subject look two distinctly different ways using only light position/time and composition (no editing).
3. (2) Take one RAW frame through the full 7-step workflow and write the recipe you used (one line per step).
4. (2) Run the 6-question critical reading on one of your own photos in writing; end with the single change that would most improve it.
