# Assignment Menu: PHOT-10

The lessons prescribe a default daily warm-up drill, but no single drill fits everyone every day. This is the full menu: **pick one drill per active skill, rotate every few days** so nothing goes stale. The two skills that need *daily, lifelong* reps are settings recall (A) and seeing (E/F); keep those going forever.

**Building your daily ~15–20 minutes:** 3–4 min of settings recall (A) + 3–4 min of light-spotting (E) + 5–10 min of the week's focus skill (B/C/D) + 2–3 min of critique/seeing (F) every single day.

---

## A. Settings recall: make the camera reflex (start Day 1, never stop)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **A1: Stop ladder** | Name the next/previous full stop on a random control ("one stop down from f/4?" → f/5.6; "one faster than 1/125?" → 1/250) | the unit of light; Week 1 default |
| **A2: Reciprocity sprint** | Start from "f/8, 1/250, ISO 200." Draw a random change, say the compensating move on another control | the core exposure skill |
| **A3: Walk-up exposure** | At any spot, *before* metering, say the full Manual exposure you'd start with; then check against meter/histogram | converting math into instinct |
| **A4: Meter-fooling flashcards** | Random tricky scene → name exposure-comp direction + amount ("snow" → +1.5; "black cat" → −1.5) | beating the gray meter |
| **A5: Blind dial** | With the camera at your eye, change aperture/shutter/ISO to a called value *without lowering it* | stage-ready handling; advanced |

## B. Focus & sharpness (start Day 6)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **B1: Eye-pin** | 10 frames at your widest aperture, focus dead on the near eye; check each at 100%; log your hit rate | portraits; the make-or-break skill |
| **B2: AF-S vs AF-C** | Subject walks toward you; shoot both modes; see one go soft, one track | matching mode to motion |
| **B3: Zone-focus reps** | Pre-focus at ~2.5 m, f/8, MF; shoot street-style with no refocusing; check the sharp zone | speed capture; the X100VI street method |
| **B4: Shake limit** | Handheld static subject down the shutter range; find your slowest still-sharp speed | knowing your handheld floor |
| **B5: Peaking practice** | Manual focus only, using peaking + magnify, on tricky low-contrast subjects | when AF fails |

## C. Aperture, shutter & exposure looks (Week 1 focus)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **C1: Aperture ladder** | Same subject across f/2→f/16; watch DoF change | seeing depth of field |
| **C2: Freeze & flow** | One moving subject frozen (≥1/500) and blurred (≤1/15) on purpose | shutter control |
| **C3: Panning** | ~1/30, track a passing subject, fire while moving with it | motion with intent; low hit rate is normal |
| **C4: Guess-the-settings** | On any photo (yours or others'), guess aperture from the background blur and shutter from any motion; verify in the info | reading exposure backward |

## D. Color & white balance (start Day 9)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **D1: Kelvin sense** | Name warm/cool + rough Kelvin + neutralizing preset for a random source | color-temperature intuition |
| **D2: WB tour** | Same indoor scene across Auto/Daylight/Tungsten/Cloudy/Shade; spot the neutral one | seeing color casts |
| **D3: Cast-spotting** | In any photo, name the cast (too warm/cool/green/magenta) and the correction | editing color; Week 3 |
| **D4: Creative WB** | Deliberately warm a sunset / cool a scene for mood | WB as expression |

## E. Light-spotting & composition (start Day 8; every day, no exceptions)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **E1: Light read** | Wherever you are, name the main light's direction + hardness + color, and where you'd put a subject | the seeing habit; daily |
| **E2: Name-the-subject** | Scroll recent shots; state each subject in ≤3 words. Any you can't = a lesson | clarity of intent |
| **E3: Eye-path trace** | On any photo, find the entry point and trace where the composition leads the eye | flow and leading lines |
| **E4: See-it-first** | Walk 10 min without shooting; narrate potential frames aloud (light, angle, subject placement) before raising a camera | composing before clicking |
| **E5: One subject, five ways** | Pick a subject; make five genuinely different strong compositions of it | compositional range |
| **E6: Height hunt** | Shoot one subject from low / eye-level / high; pick the best and say why | breaking the standing-default |

## F. Editing & critique (start Day 15)

| Drill | How | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **F1: Cull-rate** | Open a past shoot; pick the single best frame and say why in one sentence | decisiveness |
| **F2: First-slider** | Flash a photo problem; name the *first* slider you'd reach for | editing reflexes |
| **F3: Where's-the-eye** | On any edited photo, name the brightest/sharpest point; is it the subject? If not, name the local fix | directing attention |
| **F4: Two-line critique** | On any photo: one line on what works, one on what you'd change (craft vocabulary only) | the critical eye; Domain 9 |
| **F5: Six-question read** | Run the full critical-reading framework (Day 19) on one photo, in writing | deep reading; do weekly |
| **F6: Re-edit the old** | Re-develop an old RAW with your current skills; compare | proof your eye is growing |

## Rules that make any drill work

1. **Random beats sequential.** Drilling f-stops *in order* trains the order, not the knowledge. Shuffle, use a list, or just take whatever the world hands you.
2. **Always with a camera in reach** for the shooting drills. Theory you can't execute is trivia.
3. **Speed pressure, gently.** A 3-second rule on the recall drills turns "I can work it out" into "I know it." Add pressure only once accuracy is solid.
4. **Keep one number.** Log your eye-focus hit rate, your handheld shake limit, your keeper rate. The line going up is the motivation.
5. **Review every shoot.** The drill that matters most isn't shooting; it's *looking critically at what you shot*. Five minutes of honest review beats fifty more frames.
6. **When a drill gets easy, retire it** and pull the next from the menu. Easy drills are entertainment, not practice.
