# PHOT-10 Syllabus: Foundations of Photography & the Digital Camera

## Course description

A three-week intensive in the fundamentals of photography with a digital camera. Students learn how a camera converts light into an image, master the exposure triangle (aperture, shutter speed, ISO), gain reliable control over focus and depth of field, learn to read and use natural light, study the working grammar of composition, and finish with a complete RAW-to-export editing workflow and a small portfolio. By the end, students can walk into any lighting situation, choose settings on purpose, and explain every decision behind a finished photograph. Camera controls are taught generically and mapped specifically to the Fujifilm X100VI.

## Learning objectives

By the end of this course you will be able to:

1. **Explain the exposure triangle** and predict how changing aperture, shutter speed, or ISO affects brightness, depth of field, motion, and noise.
2. **Expose any scene correctly and intentionally** using Manual and the semi-auto modes (A/S/P), reading the histogram and metering rather than guessing.
3. **Control focus and depth of field**: choose an aperture for a desired look, nail focus reliably, and freeze or blur motion on purpose.
4. **Read light**: identify direction, quality (hard/soft), and color of light, and position yourself or your subject to use it.
5. **Compose deliberately** using framing, balance, lines, and perspective, and explain why a frame works.
6. **Manage color**: set white balance, understand RAW vs. JPEG, and produce accurate or intentional color.
7. **Edit start to finish**: a repeatable RAW workflow from import to export, plus the discipline to know when to stop.
8. **Produce a coherent set of photographs** that show a point of view, and critique your own and others' work in the language of the craft.

## Daily structure (~1.5–2 hours)

| Block | Time | Activity |
|---|---|---|

Grade yourself against `RUBRIC.md`. **Mastery threshold: 90%.** If you score below 90% on a quiz, re-study that week's material on the review day before moving on; the course is cumulative and gaps compound (you cannot compose well if you're still fighting your exposure).

## Course policies (yes, even self-taught courses need them)

- **No chimping-and-hoping.** Before every frame in this course, you decide your settings on purpose. "I left it on Auto" is not an answer in this class.
- **Shoot RAW from Day 15 on** (Week 1–2 may be JPEG so you focus on the camera, not the computer). Keep your files; you'll re-edit them later.
- **Keep your failures.** Deleting bad frames deletes the lesson. Review them.
- **Closed-book quizzes.** The point is to find gaps, not to feel good.
- **The daily shoot is non-negotiable.** Twenty deliberate frames a day beats 500 on the weekend.
- **Falling behind:** use the review day. If you're more than a day behind, compress by shrinking the shoot count (marked ★ optional exercises first), never by skipping lectures or quizzes.
